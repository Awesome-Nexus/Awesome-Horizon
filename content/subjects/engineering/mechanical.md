## ğŸ’¡ Study Tips

### 1. Master the Fundamentals
Statics, dynamics, and strength of materials are the foundation. Know them cold.

### 2. Do Problems
Engineering is learned by solving problems. Do 10+ problems per concept.

### 3. Use Software Early
Start using CAD and FEA software in your first year. Employers expect proficiency.

### 4. Build Projects
- Join Formula SAE, Baja SAE, or solar car teams
- Design and build something
- Document everything for your portfolio

### 5. Intern Early
Start internships after sophomore year. Experience matters more than GPA.

### 6. Learn to Code
Python and MATLAB are essential. C++ is valuable for embedded systems.

## â“ FAQ

**Q: How much math do I need?**
A: Calculus (1-3), differential equations, and linear algebra are essential.

**Q: Do I need a master's degree?**
A: Not required, but helpful for specialized roles or research. Many jobs only require a BS.

**Q: What's the difference between ME and other engineering?**
A: ME is broad and deals with physical systems. Other fields are more specialized.

**Q: Is ME hard?**
A: It's challenging but rewarding. Requires strong math and spatial reasoning.

**Q: Can I transition to software/AI?**
A: Yes! Many MEs become software engineers or data scientists. The problem-solving skills transfer.
