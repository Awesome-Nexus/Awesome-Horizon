## â“ FAQ

**Q: How much math do I need?**
A: Calculus is essential. Linear algebra and differential equations are needed for advanced topics.

**Q: Is physics hard?**
A: It requires mathematical maturity and abstract thinking. With dedication, anyone can learn it.

**Q: Should I major in physics?**
A: If you love understanding how things work and enjoy math, yes. It's great preparation for many careers.

**Q: Can I self-study physics?**
A: Yes! MIT OCW, textbooks, and problem sets make it possible. Find a study group or online community.

**Q: What's the difference between physics and engineering?**
A: Physics seeks to understand nature. Engineering applies that understanding to build things.
