## 1. K-12 MATHEMATICS

### 1.1 Core Learning Platforms

#### Khan Academy
- **URL**: https://www.khanacademy.org/math
- **Description**: Nonprofit providing free, world-class education with interactive practice problems, videos, and articles. Covers math from K-12 through early college.
- **Level**: K-12, Early College
- **Origin**: USA
- **Key Features**:
  - Personalized learning dashboard
  - Khanmigo AI-powered assistant
  - Standards-aligned practice (Common Core)
  - Covers: Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, Trigonometry, Calculus, Statistics

#### IXL
- **URL**: https://www.ixl.com/math
- **Description**: Comprehensive K-12 math practice platform with adaptive learning technology
- **Level**: K-12
- **Origin**: USA
- **Key Features**: 
  - Interactive questions with immediate feedback
  - Detailed analytics and progress tracking
  - Covers all major curricula

#### Brilliant.org
- **URL**: https://brilliant.org/math/
- **Description**: Interactive problem-solving platform with visual, hands-on learning approach
- **Level**: Elementary to College
- **Origin**: USA (team from MIT, Harvard, Stanford, Microsoft, Google)
- **Key Features**:
  - 10+ million learners worldwide
  - Visual, interactive canvas
  - Courses: Arithmetic, Algebra, Calculus, Probability, Number Theory
  - Personalized practice with intelligent feedback

### 1.2 Singapore Math Resources

#### Official Singapore Math
- **URL**: https://www.singaporemath.com/
- **Description**: The world-renowned Singapore Math curriculum using the CPA (Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract) approach
- **Level**: K-6
- **Origin**: Singapore
- **Key Features**:
  - #1 in education for 30+ years (TIMSS & PISA)
  - Dimensions Math PK-5 program
  - Strong problem-solving focus
  - Mental math and bar modeling

#### E-Singapore Math
- **URL**: https://esingaporemath.com/
- **Description**: Online adaptive Singapore Math curriculum
- **Level**: K-6
- **Origin**: Singapore
- **Features**: 1,500+ lessons, 40,000+ exercises, diagnostic learning

#### Mathodology / think! Mathematics
- **URL**: https://thinkmathematics.com/
- **Description**: Research-backed curriculum by Dr. Yeap Ban Har and Sarah Schaefer
- **Level**: Pre-K to Grade 6
- **Origin**: Singapore/USA

### 1.3 Math Olympiad & Competition Resources

#### Art of Problem Solving (AoPS)
- **URL**: https://artofproblemsolving.com/
- **Description**: The premier resource for math olympiad training and competition mathematics
- **Level**: Middle School to Olympiad
- **Origin**: USA
- **Key Resources**:
  - Alcumus adaptive learning system
  - AoPS Wiki with extensive problem database
  - Online courses (WOOT, AMC prep)
  - Community forums
  - Books by Richard Rusczyk
  - Team USA IMO training (6 team members in 2024 were AoPS alumni)

#### AoPS For the Win!
- **URL**: https://artofproblemsolving.com/ftw
- **Description**: Real-time math competition practice platform

#### MATHCOUNTS Trainer
- **URL**: https://artofproblemsolving.com/mathcounts_trainer
- **Description**: Practice for MATHCOUNTS competitions

### 1.4 Interactive Math Platforms

#### Desmos
- **URL**: https://www.desmos.com/
- **Description**: Free online graphing calculator and mathematical tools
- **Level**: Middle School to College

#### GeoGebra
- **URL**: https://www.geogebra.org/
- **Description**: Dynamic mathematics software for geometry, algebra, and calculus
- **Level**: K-12 to University
- **Origin**: Austria

- **Description**: Free MIT course materials including lectures, notes, and assignments

- **Origin**: USA (Harvard)

## 3. GRADUATE/ADVANCED MATHEMATICS

### 3.1 Research-Level Wiki & Reference

#### nLab
- **URL**: https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/HomePage
- **Description**: Wiki for research-level mathematics, physics, and philosophy with focus on category theory, homotopy theory, and higher structures
- **Level**: Graduate to Research
- **Origin**: International (founded by Urs Schreiber)
- **Key Topics**:
  - Category theory
  - Higher category theory
  - Homotopy type theory
  - Algebraic geometry
  - Mathematical physics
- **Features**: Heavily interlinked, research-level content

#### MathOverflow
- **URL**: https://mathoverflow.net/
- **Description**: Q&A site for professional mathematicians
- **Level**: Research
- **Origin**: International (Stack Exchange)

### 3.2 Terence Tao's Resources

#### Terence Tao's Blog
- **URL**: https://terrytao.wordpress.com/
- **Description**: Updates on research, expository papers, open problems
- **Level**: Graduate to Research
- **Origin**: USA (UCLA)
- **Topics**: Harmonic analysis, PDE, combinatorics, number theory, compressed sensing

#### Terence Tao's UCLA Page
- **URL**: https://www.math.ucla.edu/~tao/
- **Resources**:

  - Books
  - Course notes
  - Career advice
  - Writing advice

## 8. COUNTRY-SPECIFIC RESOURCES

### 8.1 Russian Mathematical Tradition

#### Mathematical Schools and Resources
- **Moscow Mathematical Olympiad**: Historic problems and traditions
- **Kvant Magazine**: https://kvant.mccme.ru/ (Russian physics and math magazine)
- **Key Figures**:
  - **Andrey Kolmogorov**: Probability theory, analysis
  - **Vladimir Arnold**: Dynamical systems, topology
  - **Israel Gelfand**: Functional analysis, representation theory

#### Arnold's Works
- **Mathematical Methods of Classical Mechanics**
- **Ordinary Differential Equations**
- **Lectures on Partial Differential Equations**

#### Russian Problem Books
- **Problems in Calculus of One Variable** by I.A. Maron
- **Higher Algebra** by Kurosh
- **Mathematical Analysis** by Demidovich

### 8.2 French Mathematics (Bourbaki Tradition)

#### Nicolas Bourbaki
- **Éléments de mathématique**: https://www.bourbaki.fr/
- **Description**: Influential series aiming at completely self-contained treatment of mathematics
- **Publisher**: Springer (current)
- **Books**:
  - Theory of Sets
  - Algebra (Chapters 1-10)
  - General Topology
  - Functions of a Real Variable
  - Topological Vector Spaces
  - Integration
  - Lie Groups and Lie Algebras
  - Commutative Algebra
  - Spectral Theory

#### French Mathematical Institutes
- **Institut des Hautes Études Scientifiques (IHÉS)**
- **École Normale Supérieure (ENS)** Paris
- **Université Paris-Saclay**

### 8.3 Eastern European Olympiad Resources

#### Hungary
- **KöMaL Magazine**: https://www.komal.hu/
- **Description**: Hungarian mathematical and physical journal for secondary schools
- **Features**: Monthly problems, famous for training IMO participants

#### Poland
- **Polish Mathematical Olympiad**: https://om.mimuw.edu.pl/
- Strong tradition in competition mathematics

#### Romania
- **Romanian Master of Mathematics and Sciences**
- Strong tradition in mathematical competitions

#### Bulgaria
- **Bulgarian Mathematical Olympiad**
- Historic strength in competition math

### 8.4 Asian Math Competition Resources

#### China
- **Chinese Mathematical Olympiad**
- **Tsinghua University, Peking University courses**

#### Japan
- **Japanese Mathematical Olympiad**
- **University of Tokyo resources**

#### South Korea
