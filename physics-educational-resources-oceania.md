# Physics Educational Resources in Oceania (Pacific Islands)

## Research Report: Comprehensive Guide to Physics Education in the Pacific Islands

*This report covers physics educational resources across Pacific Island countries and territories beyond Australia and New Zealand, including Fiji, Samoa, Tonga, Vanuatu, Solomon Islands, Papua New Guinea, Palau, Marshall Islands, Micronesia, Nauru, Kiribati, Tuvalu, Cook Islands, Niue, Guam, Northern Mariana Islands, American Samoa, French Polynesia, and New Caledonia.*

## Detailed Country/Territory Breakdown

### FIJI
Fiji serves as the regional hub for physics education in the Pacific, hosting the main campus of the University of the South Pacific (USP).

**Key Institutions:**
- **University of the South Pacific (USP)**: The premier regional university owned by 12 Pacific Island governments
  - Discipline of Physics offers BSc, MSc, and PhD programs
  - Research strengths: Space and Atmospheric Physics, Renewable Energy, Environmental Physics
  - Active research groups in Communications/Space Physics and Renewable Energy Technologies
  
- **Fiji National University (FNU)**: 
  - College of Engineering & TVET offers Master of Science in Physics
  - Postgraduate Diploma in Physics available

- **University of Fiji**:
  - Bachelor of Science in Physics and Information Technology (double major)

### PAPUA NEW GUINEA
The largest Pacific Island nation with multiple universities offering comprehensive physics programs.

**Key Institutions:**
- **University of Papua New Guinea (UPNG)**:
  - Comprehensive BSc in Physics with specializations in Electronics, Astronomy, Meteorology, Energy Technology
  - Curriculum includes: Quantum Physics, Thermodynamics, Photovoltaic Systems, Robotics & Automation

- **Papua New Guinea University of Technology (Unitech)**:
  - School of Applied Physics established 1965
  - BSc in Applied Physics with Electronics
  - Postgraduate: MSc, MPhil, and PhD programs

- **Divine Word University**:
  - Science programs through Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences

### SOLOMON ISLANDS
The Solomon Islands National University recently established dedicated science programs.

**Key Institutions:**
- **Solomon Islands National University (SINU)**:
  - School of Science established 2020
  - BSc with Physics major and double major options (Mathematics & Physics, Physics & Computing Science)
  - Led by Dr. Lionel Cliff Kakai (PhD in Science and Mathematics Education)

### VANUATU
Developing physics education through USP campus and new national university.

**Key Institutions:**
- **USP Emalus Campus**:
  - In-country science programs launched
  - Postgraduate Diploma in Mathematics and Physics launched 2025

- **National University of Vanuatu (NUV)**:
  - New national university developing science teacher education

### SAMOA
Strong regional presence through USP campus and National University.

**Key Institutions:**
- **National University of Samoa (NUS)**:
  - Department of Science offers Physics courses
  - Faculty of Science includes Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Agriculture

- **USP Samoa Campus (Alafua)**:
  - Physics courses through STEMP
  - School of Agriculture and Food Technology based here

### TONGA
Physics education available through USP regional campus.

**Key Institutions:**
- **USP Tonga Campus**:
  - Offers science and physics programs through distance/flexible learning
  - In-country science programs available

### PACIFIC ISLANDS (US TERRITORIES)

**Guam:**
- **University of Guam**: Physics courses (PH 210-252), Pre-Engineering program

**Northern Mariana Islands:**
- **Northern Marianas College**: Physical Science (NS101) with laboratory

**American Samoa:**
- **American Samoa Community College**: Physics courses, AS in Marine Science

### FRENCH TERRITORIES

**French Polynesia:**
- **University of French Polynesia (UPF)**: BS in Physics and Chemistry; Master's programs

**New Caledonia:**
- **University of New Caledonia (UNC)**: Bachelor's in Physics, Chemistry; science programs

### SMALLEST PACIFIC ISLANDS
For smaller island nations (Palau, Marshall Islands, Micronesia, Nauru, Kiribati, Tuvalu, Cook Islands, Niue, Tokelau), physics education is primarily provided through:
- **USP Campus networks**: Foundation Science programs, degree pathways
- **Community Colleges**: Introductory physics and physical science courses
- **Distance Learning**: Online and mixed-mode delivery

## Regional Organizations Supporting Physics Education

### The Pacific Community (SPC)
- Regional science capacity building
- Climate change education incorporating physics
- Pacific Sea Level Monitoring network
- Geoscience Division based in Noumea

### Secretariat of the Pacific Regional Environment Programme (SPREP)
- Climate Science and Information Programme
- Supports National Meteorological Services
- Weather and climate forecasting capacity building

### South Pacific Physics Society (SPPS)
- Regional professional body for physics educators and researchers
- Promotes physics teaching and research across Pacific Islands
