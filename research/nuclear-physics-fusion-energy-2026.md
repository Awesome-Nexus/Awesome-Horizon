A comprehensive research compilation covering fusion energy developments, nuclear fission reactors, research facilities, nuclear astrophysics, data resources, and professional societies.

## 1. Fusion Energy (2024-2025)

### ITER (International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor)
- **Location**: Saint-Paul-lès-Durance, France
- **Status**: Assembly phase ongoing; construction began in 2010
- **Updated Timeline** (June 2024 revamp):
  - First plasma: Targeted for D-D operation in 2035
  - Full magnetic energy and plasma current operation: Post-2035
  - Original first plasma target (2018) was revised to 2025, then further updated
- **Key Milestones**:
  - All eight torus and cryostat cryopumps completed (Fusion for Energy, 2025)
  - Cryopumps manufactured by Research Instruments and Alsymex
  - Components will trap gas particles at -269°C for fuel reprocessing
- **Goal**: 500 MW fusion power output for 400+ seconds with 50 MW plasma heating input
- **Website**: https://www.iter.org/

### NIF (National Ignition Facility)
- **Location**: Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, California, USA
- **2024-2025 Achievements**:
  - Multiple ignition shots achieved since first net energy gain in December 2022
  - Historic 2022 shot achieved Q > 1 (3.15 MJ output from 2.05 MJ laser input)
  - AI-driven models now predicting fusion ignition shots days in advance
  - Machine learning for real-time control of rapid-fire laser systems (Nov 2025)
  - 200th use of TARDIS experimental platform (July 2025)
- **Recent Developments**:
  - First experimental measurements of nuclear reactions in high energy density plasma environments similar to stellar conditions (Jan 2026)
  - Crossed-laser technique for plasma measurement with signals >1 billion times stronger than traditional Thomson scattering (Jan 2026)
  - Partnership with Fraunhofer ILT (Germany) for next-generation fusion lasers (Dec 2025)
- **Website**: https://lasers.llnl.gov/

### SPARC (MIT/Commonwealth Fusion Systems)
- **Location**: Devens, Massachusetts, USA
- **Status**: Assembly started; major construction milestones achieved
- **Timeline**:
  - First plasma: Scheduled for 2026
  - Net power demonstration (Q > 1): Target 2027
- **Technical Specifications**:
  - Fusion gain Q > 2, expected Q ≈ 11
  - 140 MW fusion power capability for 10-second bursts
  - Magnetic field: 12.2 T using YBCO high-temperature superconducting magnets
  - Major radius: 1.85 m, Minor radius: 0.57 m
- **2024-2025 Milestones**:
  - Cryostat base installed (2025) - 75 tonnes, 24 feet diameter
  - First of 18 toroidal field magnets completed (Jan 2026)
  - Central solenoid prototype demonstrated (Nov 2024)
  - Building construction largely completed
- **ARC Power Plant**: Planned as follow-on commercial plant (~400 MWe, grid connection early 2030s)
- **Virginia Project**: Agreement with Dominion Energy for Chesterfield County site (Dec 2024)
- **Website**: https://cfs.energy/

### Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS)
- **Funding**: Backed by Eni, Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Khosla Ventures, Temasek, Equinor
- **Technology**: REBCO high-temperature superconducting magnets (20 T field strength achieved Sept 2021)
- **Recent Progress**:
  - Google signed 200MW offtake agreement
  - Independent financing planned for commercial fusion power plant
- **Website**: https://cfs.energy/

### TAE Technologies
- **Location**: Foothill Ranch, California, USA
- **Device**: Norman (fifth-generation fusion platform)
- **Technology**: Field-reversed configuration (FRC), neutral beam injection
- **Fuel**: Hydrogen-boron (p-B11) - aneutronic fusion
- **Status**: Continuing operations and experiments on Norman device
- **Website**: https://tae.com/

### Helion Energy
- **Location**: Everett, Washington & Malaga, Washington (Orion plant)
- **Devices**: 
  - Trenta (6th gen): First private reactor to reach 100 million°C (June 2021)
  - Polaris (7th gen): Operations began 2024, expected to demonstrate first electricity in 2025
  - Orion: First commercial fusion power plant under construction
- **Key Developments**:
  - **Microsoft Contract** (May 2023): World's first fusion power purchase agreement
  - 50 MWe target after 1-year ramp-up
  - Commercial operation target: 2028
  - Site work began July 2025 in Chelan County, Washington
  - Constellation Energy serves as power marketer
- **Technology**:
  - Pulsed, non-ignition fusion system
  - Direct electricity recovery (no steam turbine)
  - Deuterium-helium-3 fuel
- **Website**: https://www.helionenergy.com/

### Zap Energy
- **Location**: Seattle, Washington & San Diego, California
- **Technology**: Sheared-flow-stabilized (SFS) Z-pinch
- **Devices**:
  - **FuZE-3** (3rd generation): Record plasma pressures of 1.6 GPa total (830 MPa electron pressure)
    - Temperature: >1 keV (21 million°F / 11.7 million°C)
    - Electron densities: 3-5 × 10²⁴ m⁻³
    - Three-electrode configuration with independent acceleration/compression control
  - **FuZE-Q**: Highest performing by power and neutron yield (operational)
  - **Century**: Engineering test platform achieving 39 kW sustained power
    - 100+ plasma shots at 0.2 Hz (1 shot per 5 seconds)
    - 20-fold power increase since 2024 commissioning
    - 500 kA current pulses, liquid metal wall
    - DOE certified 1,000+ consecutive shots (Feb 2025)
- **Results**: Published in Nov 2025 at APS Division of Plasma Physics meeting
- **Team**: 150 employees
- **Website**: https://www.zapenergy.com/

### JET (Joint European Torus) - Final Results
- **Location**: Culham, Oxfordshire, UK (UKAEA)
- **Status**: Decommissioning phase (operations ceased Dec 2023)
- **Final DTE3 Campaign (Aug-Oct 2023) Results** (announced Feb 2024):
  - **World Record**: 69.26 MJ fusion energy released
  - Duration: 5.2 seconds sustained fusion
  - Fuel: 0.21 mg deuterium-tritium (equivalent to a fruit fly)
  - Q ~ 0.33 (not breakeven, but highest total fusion energy ever)
- **Scientific Achievements**:
  - 96 research papers submitted (18 led by UKAEA scientists in 2024)
  - First real-time control of high fusion power via D-T fuel mix adjustment
  - Edge Localized Modes (ELM) insights for wall protection
  - Laser-based tritium deposition measurement (LID-QMS technique)
  - Negative triangularity experiments (inverted plasma) for up to 1 minute
- **Decommissioning**: JET Decommissioning and Repurposing programme (JDR) until ~2040
  - 66 tiles/components retrieved in late 2024 for analysis
  - First D-T tokamak decommissioning - providing insights for future plants
- **Legacy**: Operating since 1983; crucial precursor to ITER
- **Website**: https://www.euro-fusion.org/jet/

## 2. Nuclear Fission & Reactors

### Advanced Small Modular Reactors (SMRs)

#### NuScale Power
- **Design**: VOYGR SMR Power Plant
- **Technology**: Pressurized light-water reactor
- **Capacity**: 77 MWe per module (uprated from 50 MWe)
- **Regulatory Status**:
  - First and only SMR certified by U.S. NRC (Jan 2023 - 50 MWe design)
  - 77 MWe uprate approved by NRC (May 2025) - second design approval
- **Deployment Timeline**: Target 2029-2030
- **Projects**:
  - UAMPS Carbon Free Power Project: 6-module plant at Idaho National Lab
  - Poland: VOYGR-12 agreement with KGHM (target 2029)
  - Ghana: Agreement signed Aug 2024 for VOYGR-12
  - Romania, Czech Republic, Jordan: Active agreements
- **Applications**: Electricity, desalination, hydrogen production, data centers
- **Funding**: DOE provided $600M+ since 2014
- **Website**: https://www.nuscalepower.com/

#### X-energy (Xe-100)
- **Design**: Xe-100 High-Temperature Gas-Cooled Reactor (HTGR)
- **Capacity**: 80 MWe per unit (scalable to 320 MWe, up to 960 MWe)
- **Fuel**: TRISO-X fuel (tristructural isotropic particles)
- **Key Developments**:
  - **Amazon Investment**: $500M Series C-1 led by Amazon Climate Pledge Fund (Oct 2024)
    - Total raise: ~$700M by Feb 2025
  - 320 MWe project with Energy Northwest in Washington State
    - Expandable to 960 MWe by 2039
  - Part of Amazon's 5 GW nuclear commitment
- **Demonstration**: Dow Seadrift, Texas project targets 2030
- **Fuel Production**: TX-1 facility targeting 8 metric tons/year by 2027
- **DOE Support**: ARDP funding alongside TerraPower
- **Website**: https://www.x-energy.com/

#### TerraPower (Natrium Reactor)
- **Founded**: 2008 by Bill Gates and private investors
- **Design**: Natrium Sodium-Cooled Fast Reactor
- **Capacity**: 345 MWe (peaking to 500 MWe with molten salt storage)
- **Location**: Kemmerer, Wyoming (former Naughton coal plant site)
- **Timeline**:
  - Groundbreaking: June 2024
  - NRC approval expected: December 2026
  - Operation target: 2030
- **Key Features**:
  - Non-nuclear construction ongoing (sodium test facility)
  - Wyoming permit received Jan 2025
  - HALEU fuel agreements with Centrus and ASP Isotopes
- **Funding**:
  - DOE ARDP: Up to $2 billion (TerraPower matches portion)
  - Private funding: $830M+ by 2022
- **Impact**: Will power ~400,000 homes, create 250 permanent jobs
- **Partnerships**: KBR, HD Hyundai (2025) for global deployment
- **Website**: https://www.terrapower.com/

#### Kairos Power (Hermes Reactor)
- **Technology**: Fluoride Salt-Cooled High-Temperature Reactor (KP-FHR)
- **Location**: Oak Ridge, Tennessee (Heritage Center)
- **Hermes 1** (Demonstration Reactor):
  - 35 MWt, non-power (heat only)
  - Groundbreaking: July 2024
  - Nuclear construction started: May 2025
  - First non-water-cooled reactor approved for U.S. construction in 50+ years
- **Hermes 2** (Demonstration Plant):
  - 50 MWe, grid-connected
  - Construction permit: November 2024
  - Will supply TVA grid for Google data centers by 2030
- **Engineering Test Units**:
  - ETU 1.0: Completed 2,000+ hours (mid-2024)
  - ETU 2.0: Demonstrating modular construction (2025)
  - ETU 3.0: Final training platform installed July 2025
- **Key Partners**:
  - Google: Power purchase agreement, Master Plant Development Agreement
  - DOE: Up to $303M through ARDP
  - Los Alamos National Lab: TRISO fuel fabrication
  - BWXT: Commercial TRISO fuel production
- **Innovation**: 3D-printed carbon-fiber molds for concrete structures (with ORNL)
- **Fuel**: HALEU secured from DOE (Jan 2026 contract)
- **Website**: https://kairospower.com/

### Bill Gates & Nuclear Energy Investments
- **TerraPower**: Primary investment through Breakthrough Energy
- **Mission**: Advanced reactors for carbon-free baseload power
- **Focus Areas**:
  - Coal-to-nuclear transitions
  - Advanced reactor demonstrations
  - HALEU fuel supply chain development
- **Public Advocacy**: Active promoter of nuclear energy for climate goals

## 3. Nuclear Physics Research Facilities

### FRIB (Facility for Rare Isotope Beams)
- **Location**: Michigan State University, East Lansing, Michigan, USA
- **Operator**: MSU for U.S. DOE Office of Science
- **Status**: Operational since May 2022
- **Cost**: $765 million total project cost
- **Capabilities**:
  - World's most powerful heavy-ion accelerator
  - Maximum energy: 295 MeV/u
  - Length: 517 meters
- **2024-2025 Achievements**:
  - **Record Beam Power**: 20.0 kW uranium beam (March 2025) - doubled previous record
  - Previous record: 10.4 kW uranium beam (2024) - discovered 3 new isotopes in 8 hours
  - New isotopes discovered: Gallium-88, Arsenic-93, Selenium-96
  - $49.7 million DOE upgrade approved (March 2025)
  - First beta-delayed neutron emission from fluorine-25 observed
- **Research Areas**:
  - Nuclear structure and astrophysics
  - Fundamental interactions
  - Rare isotope production (210+ isotopes made for experiments)
  - Societal applications (medicine, security, industry)
- **Users**: 1,800+ registered users from 180 institutions, 50 countries
- **Website**: https://frib.msu.edu/

### TRIUMF (Canada)
- **Location**: Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada
- **Status**: Canada's national particle accelerator center
- **Key Facilities**:
  - **ISAC** (Isotope Separator and Accelerator): Rare isotope beams
  - **ARIEL** (Advanced Rare IsotopE Laboratory): Upgrade project
- **Capabilities**: World's largest cyclotron (520 MeV)
- **Research**: Nuclear physics, materials science, life sciences
- **Website**: https://www.triumf.ca/

### GANIL (France)
- **Location**: Caen, Normandy, France
- **Facility**: Grand Accélérateur National d'Ions Lourds
- **SPIRAL2**: Superconducting linear accelerator for rare isotopes
- **Capabilities**:
  - Heavy ion beams up to 95 MeV/u
  - Research in nuclear structure, reactions, astrophysics
- **Website**: https://www.ganil-spiral2.eu/

### RIKEN (Japan)
- **Location**: Wako, Saitama, Japan
- **Facility**: RI Beam Factory (RIBF)
- **Capabilities**: 
  - Radioactive isotope beam production
  - BigRIPS separator for rare isotopes
- **Research**: Nuclear structure far from stability, nuclear astrophysics
- **Website**: https://www.riken.jp/en/research/ribf/

### J-PARC (Japan)
- **Location**: Tokai, Ibaraki, Japan
- **Facility**: Japan Proton Accelerator Research Complex
- **Hadron Facility**: 
  - 50 GeV proton synchrotron
  - Hadron experimental hall
- **Research**: Hadron physics, nuclear physics, materials science
- **Website**: https://j-parc.jp/

### COSY (Germany) - Operations Concluded
- **Location**: Forschungszentrum Jülich
- **Status**: Operations terminated end of 2023
- **History**: 
  - 184 m cooler synchrotron and storage ring
  - Protons: 45-2700 MeV, Deuterons: 90-2100 MeV
  - Beam cooling capabilities
- **Legacy**: 30+ years of hadron physics research
- **Transition**: IKP activities transferred to GSI Darmstadt (completion 2027)
- **Current Focus**:
  - FAIR project development (HESR, PANDA)
  - Charged-particle storage ring EDM search
- **Website**: https://www.fz-juelich.de/en/ikp

## 4. Nuclear Astrophysics

### CERN ISOLDE
- **Location**: CERN, Geneva, Switzerland
- **Facility**: Isotope Separator On-Line Device
- **Capabilities**:
  - 1.4 GeV proton beam from PS Booster
  - 700+ isotopes of 70+ elements
  - High-resolution laser spectroscopy
  - Penning trap mass spectrometry
- **Nuclear Astrophysics**: 
  - r-process nucleosynthesis studies
  - Stellar reaction rates
  - Halo nuclei research
- **Website**: https://isolde.web.cern.ch/

### JUNA (China)
- **Full Name**: Jinping Underground Nuclear Astrophysics
- **Location**: Jinping Underground Laboratory, Sichuan, China
- **Depth**: ~2,400 meters underground (world's deepest)
- **Capabilities**:
  - Ultra-low background measurements
  - Stellar nuclear reaction rates
  - Direct measurements at Gamow peak energies
- **Research Focus**:
  - Big Bang nucleosynthesis
  - Stellar hydrogen/helium burning
  - Neutron sources for s-process
- **Significance**: Complements LUNA and other underground facilities

### LUNA (Italy)
- **Full Name**: Laboratory for Underground Nuclear Astrophysics
- **Location**: Gran Sasso National Laboratories (LNGS), Italy
- **Depth**: 1,400 meters underground
- **Mission**: Charged-particle-induced nuclear reactions of astrophysical interest
- **History**: Operating since 1991
- **Accelerators**:
  - Original: 50 kV accelerator
  - LUNA400: 400 kV accelerator
  - New: 3.5 MV accelerator at Bellotti Facility (2024+)
- **Research Areas**:
  - Solar fusion reactions (pp-chain, CNO cycle)
  - Big Bang nucleosynthesis
  - Helium and carbon burning
  - NeNa and MgAl cycles
  - s-process neutron sources
- **2024-2025 Publications**: 90+ papers; recent work on ²¹Ne(p,γ)²²Na, ¹⁴N(p,γ)¹⁵O, ¹²C+¹²C reactions
- **Website**: https://luna.lngs.infn.it/

## 5. Nuclear Data Resources

### NNDC (National Nuclear Data Center)
- **Location**: Brookhaven National Laboratory, USA
- **Operator**: U.S. DOE Office of Science
- **Databases**:
  - **ENSDF** (Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File): Recommended nuclear structure/decay data
  - **NuDat**: Interactive chart of nuclides with level schemes
  - **NSR** (Nuclear Science References): Bibliographic database
  - **XUNDL** (Experimental Unevaluated Nuclear Data List)
- **Additional Resources**:
  - Chart of Nuclides
  - Nuclear Wallet Cards
  - Atlas of Neutron Resonances
  - MIRD (Medical Internal Radiation Dose)
- **Website**: https://www.nndc.bnl.gov/

### ENDF (Evaluated Nuclear Data Files)
- **Purpose**: Standard nuclear data for nuclear science and engineering
- **Contents**: 
  - Cross sections
  - Angular distributions
  - Energy spectra
  - Reaction products
- **ENDF/B-VIII.0**: Current U.S. standard library
- **Management**: CSEWG (Cross Section Evaluation Working Group)
- **Website**: https://www.nndc.bnl.gov/endf/

### EXFOR (Experimental Nuclear Reaction Data)
- **Purpose**: International database of experimental nuclear reaction data
- **Coverage**: 
  - Cross sections
  - Spectra
  - Angular distributions
  - Resonance parameters
- **Management**: International Network of Nuclear Reaction Data Centres (NRDC)
- **Coordination**: IAEA Nuclear Data Section
- **Website**: https://www-nds.iaea.org/exfor/

### Additional Resources
- **JANIS**: Java-based Nuclear Data Information System (NEA/OECD)
- **TENDL**: TALYS Evaluated Nuclear Data Library
- **Jeff-3.3**: Joint Evaluated Fission and Fusion (OECD/NEA)
- **CENDL**: Chinese Evaluated Nuclear Data Library
- **JENDL**: Japanese Evaluated Nuclear Data Library
