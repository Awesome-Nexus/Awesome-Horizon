## 1. MAJOR CHEMISTRY DISCIPLINES

### Organic Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of carbon-containing compounds, their structure, properties, and reactions
- **Key Topics**: Hydrocarbons, functional groups, reaction mechanisms, stereochemistry, synthesis
- **Applications**: Pharmaceuticals, polymers, petrochemicals, agrochemicals
- **Resources**: 
  - [Organic Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Organic_Chemistry)
  - [Virtual Textbook of Organic Chemistry](https://www2.chemistry.msu.edu/faculty/reusch/VirtTxtJml/intro1.htm)

### Inorganic Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of inorganic compounds, metals, minerals, and organometallic compounds
- **Key Topics**: Coordination chemistry, crystal field theory, bioinorganic chemistry, solid-state chemistry
- **Applications**: Catalysis, materials science, semiconductors, batteries
- **Resources**:
  - [Inorganic Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Inorganic_Chemistry)
  - [ACS Inorganic Chemistry Division](https://www.divched.org/)

### Physical Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of macroscopic and microscopic phenomena in chemical systems using physics principles
- **Key Topics**: Thermodynamics, kinetics, quantum chemistry, statistical mechanics, spectroscopy
- **Applications**: Energy storage, reaction optimization, atmospheric chemistry
- **Resources**:
  - [Physical Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Physical_and_Theoretical_Chemistry_Textbook_Maps)
  - [NIST Chemistry WebBook](https://webbook.nist.gov/chemistry/)

### Analytical Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of material composition, separation, identification, and quantification
- **Key Topics**: Chromatography, spectroscopy, electrochemistry, mass spectrometry, sampling
- **Applications**: Environmental monitoring, quality control, forensic science, clinical diagnostics
- **Resources**:
  - [Analytical Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Analytical_Chemistry)
  - [Analytical Sciences Digital Library](http://www.asdlib.org/)

### Biochemistry
- **Description**: Study of chemical processes within living organisms
- **Key Topics**: Proteins, enzymes, nucleic acids, metabolism, molecular biology
- **Applications**: Medicine, biotechnology, agriculture, nutrition
- **Resources**:
  - [Biochemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Biochemistry)
  - [Biochemistry Online](http://www.biochemistry.org/)

### Materials Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of designing and synthesizing new materials with desirable properties
- **Key Topics**: Nanomaterials, polymers, ceramics, composites, electronic materials
- **Applications**: Electronics, aerospace, renewable energy, biomedical devices
- **Resources**:
  - [Materials Science LibreTexts](https://eng.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Materials_Science)
  - [Materials Research Society](https://www.mrs.org/)

### Nuclear Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of radioactivity, nuclear processes, and nuclear properties
- **Key Topics**: Radioactive decay, nuclear reactions, isotopes, nuclear medicine, radiation chemistry
- **Applications**: Medical imaging, cancer treatment, power generation, dating techniques
- **Resources**:
  - [Nuclear Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Nuclear_and_Radiochemistry)
  - [American Nuclear Society](https://www.ans.org/)

### Environmental Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of chemical phenomena in natural environments
- **Key Topics**: Atmospheric chemistry, water chemistry, soil chemistry, pollution, green chemistry
- **Applications**: Climate science, pollution control, sustainability, remediation
- **Resources**:
  - [Environmental Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Environmental_Chemistry)
  - [ACS Green Chemistry Institute](https://www.acs.org/greenchemistry)

### Medicinal Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of designing, synthesizing, and developing pharmaceutical agents
- **Key Topics**: Drug design, structure-activity relationships, pharmacokinetics, natural products
- **Applications**: Drug discovery, pharmaceutical development, therapeutic agents
- **Resources**:
  - [Medicinal Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Biological_Chemistry)
  - [ChEMBL Database](https://www.ebi.ac.uk/chembl/)

### Polymer Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of macromolecules and polymer materials
- **Key Topics**: Polymer synthesis, polymer characterization, polymer physics, biopolymers
- **Applications**: Plastics, textiles, coatings, biomedical materials, composites
- **Resources**:
  - [Polymer Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Polymer_Chemistry)
  - [American Chemical Society Polymer Division](https://polyacs.org/)

### Theoretical Chemistry
- **Description**: Study of chemical systems using theoretical physics and mathematics
- **Key Topics**: Quantum chemistry, molecular modeling, computational methods, statistical mechanics
- **Applications**: Drug design, materials prediction, reaction mechanism elucidation
- **Resources**:
  - [Theoretical Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Physical_and_Theoretical_Chemistry_Textbook_Maps)
  - [Psi4 Open-Source Quantum Chemistry](https://psicode.org/)

### Computational Chemistry
- **Description**: Application of computer simulation to solve chemical problems
- **Key Topics**: Molecular dynamics, density functional theory, quantum calculations, cheminformatics
- **Applications**: Drug discovery, materials design, catalyst optimization
- **Resources**:
  - [Computational Chemistry LibreTexts](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Physical_and_Theoretical_Chemistry_Textbook_Maps/Supplemental_Modules_(Physical_and_Theoretical_Chemistry)/Quantum_Mechanics)
  - [Avogadro - Molecular Editor](https://avogadro.cc/)

## 7. CHEMISTRY HISTORY & NOBEL LAUREATES

### Pioneers of Chemistry

#### Marie Curie (1867-1934)
- **Nationality**: Polish-French
- **Nobel Prizes**:
  - Physics (1903) - Radioactivity research with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel
  - Chemistry (1911) - Discovery of radium and polonium
- **Achievements**:
  - First woman to win a Nobel Prize
  - First person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences
  - Pioneer in radioactivity research
  - Developed mobile X-ray units during WWI
- **Legacy**: Curie Institutes in Paris and Warsaw continue her work

#### Dmitri Mendeleev (1834-1907)
- **Nationality**: Russian
- **Achievement**: Created the periodic table of elements (1869)
- **Contributions**:
  - Arranged elements by atomic mass
  - Predicted properties of undiscovered elements (gallium, scandium, germanium)
  - Left gaps for unknown elements
- **Legacy**: The periodic table remains fundamental to chemistry education

#### Linus Pauling (1901-1994)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Nobel Prizes**:
  - Chemistry (1954) - Research on chemical bonds
  - Peace (1962) - Campaign against nuclear weapons testing
- **Contributions**:
  - Electronegativity scale
  - Nature of chemical bonds
  - Protein structure (alpha helix)
  - Vitamin C research
- **Legacy**: One of the most influential chemists of the 20th century

#### Robert Bunsen (1811-1899)
- **Nationality**: German
- **Contributions**:
  - Bunsen burner (with Peter Desaga)
  - Spectroscopy (with Gustav Kirchhoff)
  - Discovery of cesium and rubidium
  - Bunsen cell battery
