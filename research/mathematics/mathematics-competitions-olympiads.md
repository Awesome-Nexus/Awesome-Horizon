## 1. INTERNATIONAL OLYMPIADS

### 1.1 International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO)

**Overview:**
- **Founded:** 1959 in Romania
- **Frequency:** Annual (except 1980 due to internal strife in Mongolia)
- **Participants:** 100+ countries
- **Age Limit:** Under 20 years old
- **Status:** The oldest and most prestigious international science olympiad

**Format:**
- **Duration:** Two consecutive days
- **Problems:** 6 problems total (3 per day)
- **Time:** 4.5 hours per day
- **Scoring:** 7 points per problem, maximum 42 points
- **Topics:** Geometry, number theory, algebra, combinatorics (no calculus required)
- **Difficulty Order:** Q1, Q4, Q2, Q5, Q3, Q6 (increasing difficulty)

**Team Composition:**
- 6 students per country
- 1 team leader
- 1 deputy leader
- Observers (optional)

**Awards:**
- Gold, Silver, Bronze medals (approximately 1:2:3 ratio)
- Honorable Mention: For participants who score 7 points on at least one problem but don't win a medal
- Special Prizes: For solutions of outstanding elegance (rare; last awarded in 2005)

**Top Performing Countries (All-Time):**
| Rank | Country | Gold | Silver | Bronze |
|------|---------|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | China | 185 | 37 | 6 |
| 2 | United States | 151 | 120 | 30 |
| 3 | Russia | 106 | 62 | 12 |
| 4 | South Korea | 95 | 83 | 28 |
| 5 | Hungary | 88 | 174 | 116 |

**Notable Achievements:**
- **China:** 25 first-place finishes; 16 all-gold teams
- **United States:** 9 first-place finishes; 4 all-gold teams
- Only three perfect team scores in history: USA (1994), China (2022), Luxembourg (1981 - 1 member)

**URLs:**
- Official Website: https://www.imo-official.org/
- Problem Archive: https://www.imo-official.org/problems.aspx

### 1.2 International Mathematics Competition for University Students (IMC)

**Overview:**
- **Founded:** 1994
- **Location:** Blagoevgrad, Bulgaria (primary host since 2010)
- **Participants:** 400+ students from 50+ countries (as of 2024)
- **Age Limit:** Maximum 23 years old

**Format:**
- **Duration:** 5-6 days
- **Examinations:** Two 5-hour exams
- **Problems:** 5 problems per exam (until 2008: 6 problems)
- **Topics:** Algebra, Analysis (Real and Complex), Combinatorics, Geometry

**History:**
- 1994: Started in Plovdiv, Bulgaria with 49 participants from 6 countries
- 1996-1999: Part of EU TEMPUS Project
- 2020-2021: Held online due to COVID-19
- 2022: Hybrid format (in-person and online)

**Notable Alumni:**
- **Caucher Birkar:** Fields Medalist (2018)
- **Maryna Viazovska:** Fields Medalist (2022)
- **Peter Scholze:** Fields Medalist (2018) - also IMO participant

**URLs:**
- Official Website: https://imc-math.org.uk/

### 1.3 William Lowell Putnam Mathematical Competition

**Overview:**
- **Founded:** 1938 (established by Elizabeth Lowell Putnam in memory of her husband)
- **Administered by:** Mathematical Association of America (MAA)
- **Participants:** Undergraduate students in USA and Canada
- **Frequency:** Annual (first Saturday in December)
- **Status:** Widely considered the most prestigious university-level mathematics competition in the world

**Format:**
- **Duration:** Two 3-hour sessions
- **Problems:** 12 problems total (6 per session)
- **Scoring:** 10 points per problem, maximum 120 points
- **Difficulty:** Extremely challenging; median score often 0 or 1

**Awards:**
- **Putnam Fellows:** Top 5 individuals ($2,500 each)
- **Elizabeth Lowell Putnam Prize:** Awarded to top female participant (since 1992)
- **Team Prizes:** $25,000 (1st), $20,000 (2nd), $15,000 (3rd), $10,000 (4th), $5,000 (5th)
- **Top 100:** Names published in American Mathematical Monthly

### 1.4 Mathematical Contest in Modeling (MCM) / Interdisciplinary Contest in Modeling (ICM)

**Overview:**
- **Founded:** 1985
- **Organized by:** Consortium for Mathematics and its Applications (COMAP)
- **Sponsors:** SIAM, INFORMS, NSA, MAA
- **Participants:** International teams of 3 undergraduates
- **Frequency:** Annual (first/second weekend in February)

**Format:**
- **Duration:** 96 hours (4 days)
- **Team Size:** 3 students
- **Deliverable:** Research paper solving a real-world problem

**Problem Types:**
- **Problem A:** Continuous mathematics (geometry, physics, engineering)
- **Problem B:** Discrete mathematics
- **Problem C:** Data insights (added 2016) - database analysis

**Sample Past Problems:**
- Estimate global effects of asteroid impact on Antarctica (1999)
- Study velociraptor hunting strategies based on fossil data (1997)
- Develop efficient airplane boarding methods (2007)

**Awards:**
- Unsuccessful Participant
- Successful Participant (~65%)
- Honorable Mention (~11%)
- Meritorious Winner (~4%)
- Outstanding Winner & Finalist (~1.5%)

**Participation Growth:**
- Initially US-dominated
- 2007: US teams comprised only 34% of participants
- 2014: Asian teams reached 88.9% of participation

**URLs:**
- Official Website: https://www.comap.com/

## 2. REGIONAL OLYMPIADS

### 2.1 USAMO / USAJMO (United States)

**USAMO (USA Mathematical Olympiad):**
- **Founded:** 1972
- **Participants:** ~270 students annually
- **Format:** 6 proof problems, 9 hours (2 days)
- **Scoring:** 0-7 per problem, max 42 points
- **Qualification:** Based on AMC 12 + AIME scores

**USAJMO (USA Junior Mathematical Olympiad):**
- **Founded:** 2010 (split from USAMO)
- **Participants:** ~230 students annually
- **Eligibility:** Grade 10 and below
- **Qualification:** Based on AMC 10 + AIME scores

**Selection Index:**
- USAMO Index = AMC 12 Score + 10 × AIME Score
- USAJMO Index = AMC 10 Score + 10 × AIME Score

**Pathway to IMO:**
1. AMC 10/12 → AIME → USAMO/USAJMO → MOP (Mathematical Olympiad Program) → IMO Team Selection

**URLs:**
- https://www.maa.org/math-competitions/amc-1012

### 2.2 British Mathematical Olympiad (BMO)

## 3. NATIONAL COMPETITIONS (MAJOR COUNTRIES)

### 3.1 American Mathematics Competitions (AMC) - USA

**AMC 8:**
- **Grades:** 8 and below
- **Format:** 25 multiple-choice, 40 minutes
- **Founded:** 1985 (as AJHSME)
- **When:** January
- **No calculator**

**AMC 10:**
- **Grades:** 10 and below
- **Format:** 25 multiple-choice, 75 minutes
- **Founded:** 2000
- **When:** November
- **Scoring:** 6 points correct, 1.5 blank, 0 wrong

**AMC 12:**
- **Grades:** 12 and below
- **Format:** 25 multiple-choice, 75 minutes
- **Founded:** 1950 (as AHSME)
- **Pathway to:** AIME → USAMO → IMO

**AIME (American Invitational Mathematics Examination):**
- **Qualification:** Top ~5% on AMC 10/12
- **Format:** 15 problems, 3 hours
- **Answers:** Integer from 0-999
- **Scoring:** 1 point per correct answer

**Historical Context:**
| Era | Name | Questions | Notes |
|-----|------|-----------|-------|
| 1950-1951 | Annual High School Contest | 50 | NY only |
| 1952-1959 | Nationwide | 50 | |
| 1960-1967 | - | 40 | |
| 1968-1972 | - | 35 | |
| 1973-1982 | AHSME | 35 → 30 | |
| 1983-1999 | AHSME | 30 | AIME introduced |
| 2000-present | AMC | 25 | Split into AMC 10/12 |

**URLs:**
- https://www.maa.org/math-competitions/amc-1012

### 3.2 United Kingdom Mathematics Trust (UKMT)

**Overview:**
- **Founded:** 1996
- **Status:** Charity organization
- **Reach:** UK and international schools

**Junior Mathematical Challenge (JMC):**

- **Grades:** Year 13 and below

- **When:** October
- **Scoring:** Start with 25, +4 correct, -1 wrong

**Team Competitions:**
- **Team Maths Challenge:** Years 8-9 (regional → national)

## 4. SPECIALIZED COMPETITIONS

### 4.1 Mathematical Kangaroo (Kangourou sans Frontières)

**Overview:**
- **Founded:** 1991 (France), based on Australian model (1978)
- **Participants:** 6+ million students from 90+ countries (2024)
- **Status:** Largest mathematics competition in the world by participation

**History:**
- **1978:** Peter O'Halloran starts Australian Mathematics Competition
- **1991:** French teachers André Deledicq and Jean Pierre Boudine launch "Kangourou des Mathématiques"
- **1994:** Association Kangourou Sans Frontières founded in Strasbourg
- **1996:** 1 million+ participants from 17 countries
- **2014:** 6 million participants from 57 countries

**Format:**
- **Levels:** 6 categories (Pré-écolier to Student, ages 6-19)
- **Questions:** 24-30 multiple-choice
- **Duration:** 50-75 minutes
- **Scoring:** 3/4/5 point problems; penalty for wrong answers
- **When:** Third Thursday of March

**Awards:**
- Every participant receives a gift (books, games, educational materials)
- Certificates of achievement

**Participating Countries (75+ members):**
Europe: France, Germany, UK, Poland, Romania, Russia, Spain, Italy, etc.
Asia: Kazakhstan (first Asian member, 2003), China, India, Japan
Africa: Tunisia (first African member)
Americas: USA, Canada, Mexico, Brazil

**URLs:**
- AKSF: https://www.aksf.org/
- USA: https://mathkangaroo.org/

### 6.2 Summer Programs

#### Ross Mathematics Program

**Overview:**
- **Founded:** 1957 (at Notre Dame), moved to Ohio State in 1964
- **Founder:** Arnold Ross (1906-2002)
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Focus:** Number theory
- **Acceptance Rate:** ~15%

**Philosophy:**
- "Think deeply of simple things" (Gauss-inspired)
- Emphasis on personal discovery through observation and experimentation
- "Prove or disprove and salvage if possible"

**Structure:**
- Daily lectures on elementary number theory
- Problem seminars 3x weekly
- Daily problem sets
- Students discover proofs independently

**Notable Alumni:**
- Karl Rubin (mathematician)
- Numerous professors at top universities
- Inspired PROMYS and other programs

**Legacy:**
- 2,000+ students over 40+ summers
- "Ross-1s" (direct students) and "Ross-2s" (PROMYS attendees)
- Helped start similar programs in Germany, India, Australia

**URL:**
- https://rossprogram.org/
