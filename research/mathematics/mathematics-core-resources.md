## 1. Free Online Courses & Platforms

### Khan Academy
- **URL:** https://www.khanacademy.org/math
- **Type:** Non-profit Educational Platform
- **Description:** Comprehensive mathematics education from basic arithmetic through college-level calculus, linear algebra, and differential equations. Founded by Sal Khan in 2008, it offers practice exercises, instructional videos, and a personalized learning dashboard. Covers K-12 curriculum plus early college topics including multivariable calculus and statistics.
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Languages:** Available in 50+ languages
- **Notable Features:** Adaptive practice problems, mastery learning system, parent/teacher dashboards

### MIT OpenCourseWare (OCW)
- **URL:** https://ocw.mit.edu/search/?d=Mathematics
- **Type:** University Open Educational Resource
- **Description:** MIT's initiative to publish all course materials online. Mathematics department offers undergraduate and graduate courses including single and multivariable calculus, linear algebra, differential equations, probability, statistics, discrete mathematics, and advanced topics. Includes lecture notes, assignments, exams with solutions, and video lectures.
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Notable Courses:** 18.01 Single Variable Calculus, 18.06 Linear Algebra (Gilbert Strang), 18.03 Differential Equations

### Coursera - Mathematics
- **URL:** https://www.coursera.org/browse/math-and-logic
- **Type:** MOOC Platform
- **Description:** Partners with top universities (Stanford, Princeton, Johns Hopkins, etc.) to offer mathematics courses. Topics range from pre-calculus to advanced machine learning mathematics, including specialized courses in financial mathematics, cryptography, and mathematical thinking.
- **Access Level:** Audit free; certificates paid
- **Notable Courses:** "Introduction to Mathematical Thinking" (Keith Devlin, Stanford), "Mathematics for Machine Learning" (Imperial College London)

### edX - Mathematics
- **URL:** https://www.edx.org/learn/math
- **Type:** MOOC Platform
- **Description:** Founded by Harvard and MIT, offers university-level mathematics courses from institutions worldwide. Includes the famous Harvard "Science & Cooking" mathematics applications and MIT's rigorous theoretical courses.
- **Access Level:** Audit free; verified certificates paid
- **Notable Partners:** Harvard, MIT, UC San Diego, Delft University, University of Queensland

### Brilliant.org
- **URL:** https://brilliant.org/
- **Type:** Interactive Learning Platform
- **Description:** Interactive problem-solving platform with courses in mathematics, physics, and computer science. Uses active learning methodology with step-by-step guidance, instant feedback, and adaptive difficulty. Covers topics from basic algebra through advanced contest mathematics.
- **Access Level:** Free daily problems; full access subscription-based
- **Notable Features:** Interactive visualizations, daily challenges, streak tracking, community discussions
- **User Base:** 10+ million learners worldwide

### 3Blue1Brown (YouTube Channel)
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@3blue1brown
- **Type:** Educational YouTube Channel
- **Description:** Created by Grant Sanderson, produces animated videos explaining mathematical concepts with a focus on visualization. The "Essence of" series (Calculus, Linear Algebra, Neural Networks) is particularly renowned for making abstract concepts intuitive.
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Notable Series:** Essence of Calculus, Essence of Linear Algebra, Neural Networks, Differential Equations
- **Subscriber Count:** 5+ million

### Numberphile
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@numberphile
- **Type:** Educational YouTube Channel
- **Description:** Brady Haran's channel featuring interviews with mathematicians about interesting numbers, problems, and concepts. Covers recreational mathematics to cutting-edge research in an accessible format.
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Notable Guests:** Terence Tao, John Conway (RIP), Hannah Fry, Alex Bellos

### Mathologer
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@Mathologer
- **Type:** Educational YouTube Channel
- **Description:** Run by Burkard Polster at Monash University, presents deep mathematical topics with rigorous explanations and beautiful visualizations. Known for thorough explorations of famous problems and paradoxes.
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Notable Videos:** "Riemann Hypothesis explained," "Pi is wrong (Tau manifesto)," "Hilbert's Hotel"

### Art of Problem Solving (AoPS)
- **URL:** https://artofproblemsolving.com/
- **Type:** Educational Company & Community
- **Description:** Premier resource for mathematics competition preparation. Founded by Richard Rusczyk, offers online courses, books, forums, and Alcumus adaptive learning system. The online community is one of the largest mathematics forums worldwide.
- **Access Level:** Free community and resources; courses and books paid
- **Notable Features:** Alcumus (adaptive problem solver), For the Win! (competition game), MATHCOUNTS Trainer, AMC/AIME preparation
- **Community Size:** 400,000+ registered users

### Paul's Online Math Notes
- **URL:** https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/
- **Type:** Personal Educational Website
- **Description:** Created by Paul Dawkins at Lamar University, provides comprehensive notes for Algebra, Calculus I-III, and Differential Equations. Known for clear explanations, extensive examples, and practice problems with solutions.
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Notable Sections:** Calculus notes widely used by students worldwide, Cheat Sheets for quick reference

## 2. Free Textbooks & Libraries

### OpenStax (Rice University)
- **URL:** https://openstax.org/subjects/math
- **Type:** Open Educational Resource Publisher
- **Description:** Rice University's non-profit initiative publishing peer-reviewed, openly licensed textbooks. Offers comprehensive mathematics textbooks from Prealgebra through Calculus and Statistics.

- **Notable Textbooks:**
  - Prealgebra
  - Elementary Algebra
  - Intermediate Algebra
  - College Algebra
  - Algebra and Trigonometry
  - Calculus Volumes 1-3
  - Introductory Statistics
  - Business Statistics
- **Impact:** 36.7+ million students served, $2.9+ billion in student savings

### MIT Open Learning Library
- **URL:** https://openlearning.mit.edu/
- **Type:** Open Educational Resource Platform
- **Description:** MIT's collection of free educational materials beyond OCW, including interactive courses and textbooks for self-paced learning.
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Notable Features:** Some courses offer MIT-level assessments and progress tracking

### Project Euclid
- **URL:** https://projecteuclid.org/
- **Type:** Mathematics and Statistics Publishing Platform
- **Description:** Cornell University Library initiative providing access to scholarly literature in theoretical and applied mathematics and statistics. Provides both open access and subscription-based content from independent and society journals.
- **Access Level:** Mixed open access and subscription
- **Notable Journals:** Duke Mathematical Journal, Notre Dame Journal of Formal Logic

### arXiv Mathematics Section
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/archive/math
- **Type:** Preprint Repository
- **Description:** Open-access repository for mathematics preprints. Contains 150,000+ mathematics papers across categories including Algebraic Geometry, Number Theory, Combinatorics, Analysis, and more.
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Categories:** math.AG, math.AT, math.AP, math.CT, math.CA, math.CO, math.AC, math.CV, math.DG, math.DS, math.FA, math.GM, math.GN, math.GT, math.GR, math.HO, math.IT, math.KT, math.LO, math.MP, math.MG, math.NT, math.NA, math.OA, math.OC, math.PR, math.QA, math.RT, math.RA, math.SP, math.ST, math.SG

### zbMATH Open
- **URL:** https://zbmath.org/
- **Type:** Mathematics Abstracting and Reviewing Service
- **Description:** Comprehensive abstracting and reviewing service in pure and applied mathematics. Contains over 4 million entries from 3,000+ journals and 170,000+ books.
- **Access Level:** Free search; full reviews may require subscription
- **History:** Formerly Zentralblatt MATH, now open access since 2021

### American Institute of Mathematics (AIM) Approved Textbooks
- **URL:** https://aimath.org/textbooks/
- **Type:** Textbook Evaluation Initiative
- **Description:** AIM maintains a list of open-source and commercial textbooks that meet their evaluation criteria for quality and appropriateness for undergraduate courses.
- **Access Level:** Free access to approved textbook list; individual textbook licenses vary
- **Categories:** Abstract Algebra, Calculus, Differential Equations, Linear Algebra, Real Analysis, Topology

### Bookboon
- **URL:** https://bookboon.com/en/mathematics-ebooks
- **Type:** E-book Publisher
- **Description:** Offers free downloadable textbooks in mathematics and engineering mathematics, supported by advertising.
- **Access Level:** Free with registration
- **Notable Topics:** Engineering Mathematics, Statistics, Financial Mathematics, Calculus

### Digital Library of Mathematical Functions (DLMF)
- **URL:** https://dlmf.nist.gov/
- **Type:** Reference Work
- **Description:** National Institute of Standards and Technology's digital successor to Abramowitz and Stegun. Comprehensive reference on special functions of applied mathematics.
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Notable Features:** Interactive 3D graphics, math-aware search, downloadable data

## 3. Major Mathematical Organizations

### International Mathematical Union (IMU)
- **URL:** https://www.mathunion.org/
- **Type:** International Non-Governmental Organization
- **Description:** International non-governmental and non-profit scientific organization with the purpose of promoting international cooperation in mathematics. A member of the International Science Council (ISC). Organizes the International Congress of Mathematicians (ICM) every four years.
- **Founded:** 1920 (reconstituted 1951)
- **Membership:** 80+ member countries
- **Key Activities:**
  - International Congress of Mathematicians (ICM)
  - Fields Medal and other IMU awards
  - International Commission on Mathematical Instruction (ICMI)
  - Commission for Developing Countries (CDC)
  - IMU-Simons Research Fellowship Program

### American Mathematical Society (AMS)
- **URL:** https://www.ams.org/
- **Type:** Professional Society
- **Description:** Dedicated to advancing research and connecting the diverse global mathematical community through publications, conferences, advocacy, and awareness programs.
- **Founded:** 1888
- **Membership:** 27,000+
- **Publications:** 27 journals including Journal of the AMS, Bulletin of the AMS, Notices of the AMS, Mathematics of Computation
- **Key Activities:**
  - MathSciNet (comprehensive mathematics database with 4M+ items)
  - AMS Book Program
  - Employment services (MathJobs)
  - Mathematics Genealogy Project
  - Annual Joint Mathematics Meetings

### Mathematical Association of America (MAA)
- **URL:** https://www.maa.org/
- **Type:** Professional Society
- **Description:** The world's largest community of mathematicians, students, and enthusiasts focused on mathematics accessible at the undergraduate level.
- **Founded:** 1915
- **Membership:** 25,000+
- **Key Activities:**
  - American Mathematics Competitions (AMC 8/10/12, AIME, USAMO)
  - Putnam Mathematical Competition
  - MAA American Mathematical Monthly and other journals
  - MAA MathFest (annual summer meeting)
  - Project NExT (New Experiences in Teaching)

### European Mathematical Society (EMS)
- **URL:** https://euromathsoc.org/
- **Type:** Regional Professional Society
- **Description:** Promotes mathematical research, education, and cooperation in Europe. Represents mathematics within the European Union and internationally.
- **Founded:** 1990
- **Membership:** 60+ mathematical societies
- **Key Activities:**
  - European Congress of Mathematics (every 4 years)
  - EMS Press (publishing house)
  - EMS Prizes for young mathematicians
  - Committee for Developing Countries

### London Mathematical Society (LMS)
- **URL:** https://www.lms.ac.uk/
- **Type:** National Professional Society
- **Description:** Major British learned society for mathematics founded in 1865, making it one of the oldest mathematical societies in the world.
- **Founded:** 1865
- **Key Activities:**
  - Prizes: De Morgan Medal, Fröhlich Prize, Whitehead Prizes
  - Publications: Proceedings, Journal, Bulletin, Transactions
  - Grants for research and conferences
  - LMS-CMI Research Schools

### Société Mathématique de France (SMF)
- **URL:** https://smf.emath.fr/
- **Type:** National Professional Society
- **Description:** Leading French mathematical society promoting mathematics research and education.
- **Founded:** 1872

## 4. Major Awards & Prizes

### Fields Medal
- **Awarding Body:** International Mathematical Union (IMU)
- **URL:** https://www.mathunion.org/imu-awards/fields-medal
- **Description:** Regarded as the highest honor a mathematician can receive, often described as the "Nobel Prize of Mathematics." Awarded to 2-4 mathematicians under 40 years of age every four years at the International Congress of Mathematicians.
- **First Awarded:** 1936
- **Monetary Award:** CA$15,000
- **Age Limit:** Under 40 years old on January 1 of the award year
- **Notable Laureates:** Maryam Mirzakhani (first female recipient, 2014), Grigori Perelman (declined, 2006), Terence Tao (2006), Maryna Viazovska (2022)
- **Total Recipients:** 64 (as of 2022)

### Abel Prize
- **Awarding Body:** Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters
- **URL:** https://www.abelprize.no/
- **Description:** Annual prize awarded for outstanding scientific work in the field of mathematics. Often considered a "Nobel Prize for Mathematics" without age restrictions.
- **First Awarded:** 2003
- **Monetary Award:** 7.5 million NOK (approximately $750,000 USD)
- **2025 Laureate:** Masaki Kashiwara (algebraic analysis and representation theory)
- **Notable Laureates:** Jean-Pierre Serre (2003), Andrew Wiles (2016), Karen Uhlenbeck (first woman, 2019), Dennis Sullivan (2022), Luis Caffarelli (2023)

### Wolf Prize in Mathematics
- **Awarding Body:** Wolf Foundation (Israel)
- **URL:** https://wolf-fund.org.il/
- **Description:** One of the most prestigious awards in mathematics, often seen as a step toward the Fields Medal or Abel Prize.
- **First Awarded:** 1978
- **Monetary Award:** $100,000
- **Frequency:** Almost annually
- **Notable Laureates:** Israel Gelfand (1978), Carl Siegel (1978), John Tate (2002/03), Andrew Wiles (1995/96), Ingrid Daubechies (2023)

### Chern Medal
- **Awarding Body:** IMU and Chern Medal Foundation
- **URL:** https://www.mathunion.org/imu-awards/chern-medal
- **Description:** Awarded every four years at the ICM to an individual whose lifelong outstanding achievements place them at the highest level of mathematical research. Also recognizes contributions to mathematics education and outreach.
- **First Awarded:** 2010
- **Monetary Award:** $250,000 (plus $250,000 for donation to mathematics causes)
- **2022 Laureate:** Barry Mazur

### IMU Abacus Medal
- **Awarding Body:** International Mathematical Union
- **URL:** https://www.mathunion.org/imu-awards/imu-abacus-medal
- **Description:** Awarded for outstanding contributions in mathematical aspects of information sciences. Formerly known as the Nevanlinna Prize (1982-2018).
- **First Awarded:** 1982 (as Nevanlinna Prize), 2022 (as Abacus Medal)
- **Age Limit:** Under 40 years old
- **2022 Laureate:** Mark Braverman (interactive information complexity)

### Carl Friedrich Gauss Prize
- **Awarding Body:** IMU and German Mathematical Union
- **URL:** https://www.mathunion.org/imu-awards/carl-friedrich-gauss-prize
- **Description:** Awarded every four years for outstanding mathematical contributions that have found significant applications outside mathematics.
- **First Awarded:** 2006
- **2022 Laureate:** Elliott Lieb (mathematical physics and analysis)

### Leelavati Prize
- **Awarding Body:** IMU
- **URL:** https://www.mathunion.org/imu-awards/leelavati-prize
- **Description:** Awarded for outstanding contributions to increasing public awareness of mathematics, named after the 12th-century Leelavati treatise by Bhaskara II.
- **First Awarded:** 2010
- **2022 Laureate:** Nikolai Andreev (Russia) for mathematical animations and outreach

### Clay Mathematics Institute Millennium Prize Problems
- **Awarding Body:** Clay Mathematics Institute
- **URL:** https://www.claymath.org/millennium-problems/
- **Description:** Seven of the most difficult open problems in mathematics, each with a $1 million prize for a correct solution.
- **Problems:**
  1. **Riemann Hypothesis:** Distribution of prime numbers
  2. **P vs NP:** Computational complexity problem
  3. **Navier-Stokes Existence and Smoothness:** Fluid dynamics equations
  4. **Yang-Mills Existence and Mass Gap:** Quantum field theory
  5. **Hodge Conjecture:** Algebraic geometry
  6. **Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture:** Elliptic curves
  7. **Poincaré Conjecture:** Solved by Grigori Perelman (2003, declined prize)
- **Status:** Poincaré Conjecture solved; 6 problems remain open

## 5. Open Problems & Research Areas

### Millennium Prize Problems

#### Riemann Hypothesis
- **Formulated:** 1859 by Bernhard Riemann
- **Statement:** All non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function have real part equal to 1/2
- **Significance:** Would unlock deep understanding of the distribution of prime numbers
- **Verification:** Checked for first 10 trillion zeros; all lie on critical line

#### P vs NP Problem
- **Field:** Computational Complexity Theory
- **Statement:** Is every problem whose solution can be quickly verified by a computer also quickly solvable by a computer?
- **Implications:** Solution worth $1 million; would impact cryptography, optimization, and virtually all computational fields
- **Status:** Widely believed that P ≠ NP, but unproven

#### Navier-Stokes Equations
- **Field:** Partial Differential Equations / Fluid Dynamics
- **Statement:** Prove or give a counterexample: Do solutions to the Navier-Stokes equations exist for all time, and are they smooth?
- **Significance:** Fundamental equations for fluid motion; mathematical foundation of aerodynamics and weather prediction

### Other Major Open Problems

#### Collatz Conjecture (3n+1 Problem)
- **Statement:** Starting from any positive integer, repeatedly apply: if even, divide by 2; if odd, multiply by 3 and add 1. Does this always reach 1?
- **Verification:** Tested for all numbers up to 2^68
- **Difficulty:** Despite simple statement, no proof known

#### Goldbach Conjecture
- **Strong Form:** Every even integer greater than 2 can be expressed as the sum of two primes
- **Weak Form:** Every odd number greater than 5 can be expressed as the sum of three primes (proved by Harald Helfgott, 2013)
- **Verification:** Strong form verified up to 4 × 10^18

#### Twin Prime Conjecture
- **Statement:** Are there infinitely many twin primes (pairs of primes differing by 2)?
- **Progress:** Yitang Zhang (2013) proved there are infinitely many prime pairs with gap less than 70 million; subsequent work reduced this to 246

#### abc Conjecture
- **Statement:** Deep connection between addition and multiplication of integers
- **Claimed Proof:** Shinichi Mochizuki (2012) claimed proof using "inter-universal Teichmüller theory"
- **Status:** Proof remains controversial; verification ongoing after 10+ years

### Recent Breakthroughs

#### Yitang Zhang - Bounded Gaps Between Primes (2013)
- **Achievement:** Proved there are infinitely many pairs of primes separated by at most 70 million
- **Impact:** Opened the door to rapid progress; gap now reduced to 246 (or 12 assuming Elliott-Halberstam conjecture)
- **Recognition:** 2014 MacArthur Fellow, 2014 Rolf Schock Prize

#### Maryna Viazovska - Sphere Packing in 8 Dimensions (2016)
- **Achievement:** Proved that the E8 lattice provides the densest packing of identical spheres in 8 dimensions
- **Subsequent:** Also solved the 24-dimensional case (Leech lattice)
- **Recognition:** Fields Medal 2022 (second woman ever)

#### Grigori Perelman - Poincaré Conjecture (2003)
- **Achievement:** Solved the 3D Poincaré Conjecture using Ricci flow with surgery
- **Recognition:** Fields Medal 2006 (declined), Millennium Prize 2010 (declined)

## 6. Mathematics History & Biographies

### Ancient and Classical Mathematicians

#### Euclid (fl. 300 BCE)
- **Contribution:** "Elements" - the most influential mathematics textbook ever written, establishing axiomatic method
- **Known For:** Euclidean geometry, proof of infinitude of primes, Euclidean algorithm

#### Archimedes (287-212 BCE)
- **Contribution:** Greatest mathematician of antiquity; anticipated integral calculus
- **Known For:** Method of exhaustion, calculation of pi, Archimedean solids, principle of buoyancy
- **Quote:** "Give me a place to stand, and I shall move the Earth"

#### Isaac Newton (1643-1727)
- **Contribution:** Co-developed calculus, universal gravitation, optics
- **Mathematical Works:** Principia Mathematica, method of fluxions, binomial theorem

#### Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (1646-1716)
- **Contribution:** Independent co-developer of calculus; superior notation (still used today)
- **Contributions:** Binary numeral system, logic, calculating machines

### Masters of Modern Mathematics

#### Leonhard Euler (1707-1783)
- **Status:** Most prolific mathematician in history
- **Contributions:** 886 papers and books; introduced modern mathematical notation (e, i, π, f(x), Σ)
- **Fields:** Analysis, number theory, graph theory, topology, mechanics
- **Famous Formulas:** e^(iπ) + 1 = 0 (Euler's identity)

#### Carl Friedrich Gauss (1777-1855)
- **Title:** "Prince of Mathematicians"
- **Contributions:** Fundamental theorem of algebra, Gaussian distribution, non-Euclidean geometry, number theory (Disquisitiones Arithmeticae)
- **Quote:** "Mathematics is the queen of sciences, and number theory is the queen of mathematics"

#### Bernhard Riemann (1826-1866)
- **Contributions:** Riemannian geometry (foundation of general relativity), Riemann hypothesis, Riemann integral, complex analysis
- **Legacy:** Riemann hypothesis remains most famous unsolved problem

#### Évariste Galois (1811-1832)
- **Contributions:** Galois theory, group theory, founding abstract algebra
- **Tragedy:** Died at 20 in a duel; his work was published posthumously

### Pioneering Women in Mathematics

#### Emmy Noether (1882-1935)
- **Contributions:** Abstract algebra, Noether's theorem (fundamental to theoretical physics), ring theory
- **Recognition:** Einstein called her the most significant creative mathematical genius since women began higher education

#### Sofia Kovalevskaya (1850-1891)
- **Contributions:** First woman to obtain a doctorate in mathematics, partial differential equations, analysis
- **Firsts:** First woman appointed to a full professorship in Northern Europe (Stockholm University)

#### Maryam Mirzakhani (1977-2017)
- **Contributions:** Dynamics and geometry of Riemann surfaces and their moduli spaces
- **Recognition:** First woman and first Iranian to win Fields Medal (2014)
- **Tragedy:** Died of breast cancer at age 40

### 20th Century Giants

#### Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887-1920)
- **Story:** Self-taught Indian mathematician discovered by G.H. Hardy
- **Contributions:** Number theory, infinite series, continued fractions, partition function
- **Legacy:** Ramanujan primes, Ramanujan theta function, mock theta functions

#### G.H. Hardy (1877-1947)
- **Contributions:** Number theory, mathematical analysis, collaboration with Ramanujan and Littlewood
- **Writings:** "A Mathematician's Apology" - classic essay on mathematical aesthetics

#### Alexander Grothendieck (1928-2014)
- **Contributions:** Revolutionized algebraic geometry, category theory, topos theory
- **Approach:** Emphasized abstract structural properties over concrete calculations
- **Later Life:** Withdrew from mathematics and society, lived in seclusion

#### Nicolas Bourbaki
- **Identity:** Collective pseudonym for a group of mostly French mathematicians
- **Contributions:** Series of textbooks "Éléments de mathématique" that rigorously rebuilt mathematics from set theory
- **Influence:** Dominated French mathematics 1940s-1960s; controversial influence on education

### Contemporary Mathematicians

#### Terence Tao (b. 1975)
- **Recognition:** Fields Medal 2006, often called "Mozart of mathematics"
- **Contributions:** Partial differential equations, combinatorics, harmonic analysis, additive number theory, compressed sensing
- **Distinction:** Won IMO gold at age 12; youngest ever full professor at UCLA (age 24)

#### Grigori Perelman (b. 1966)
- **Achievement:** Solved Poincaré Conjecture (one of seven Millennium Problems)
- **Recognition:** Declined Fields Medal (2006) and $1 million Clay Millennium Prize (2010)
- **Status:** Withdrew from mathematics; lives in St. Petersburg

## 7. Online Encyclopedias & References

### MathWorld
- **URL:** https://mathworld.wolfram.com/
- **Type:** Online Mathematics Encyclopedia
- **Description:** The web's most extensive mathematics resource, created by Eric W. Weisstein and sponsored by Wolfram Research. Contains 13,000+ detailed entries covering all areas of mathematics.
- **Features:** Interactive examples, downloadable Mathematica notebooks, animations
- **Access Level:** Completely free

### PlanetMath
- **URL:** https://planetmath.org/
- **Type:** Community Mathematics Encyclopedia
- **Description:** Collaborative encyclopedia created by the mathematics community. Born as a response to MathWorld's temporary shutdown in 2000.
- **Features:** User-contributed content, LaTeX support, collaborative editing
- **Access Level:** Completely free

### nLab
- **URL:** https://ncatlab.org/
- **Type:** Wiki-Laboratory for Mathematics
- **Description:** Focuses on category theory and its applications to physics, mathematics, and philosophy. The "n" stands for higher category theory.
- **Features:** Collaborative research notes, cutting-edge topics
- **Access Level:** Completely free

### ProofWiki
- **URL:** https://proofwiki.org/
- **Type:** Mathematics Proof Repository
- **Description:** Online compendium of mathematical proofs with axioms, definitions, and theorems organized by subject.
- **Features:** Detailed proofs, cross-references, axiomatic development
- **Access Level:** Completely free

### On-Line Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences (OEIS)
- **URL:** https://oeis.org/
- **Type:** Integer Sequence Database
- **Description:** Database of 390,000+ integer sequences, founded by Neil J.A. Sloane in 1964. Essential tool for mathematicians, scientists, and enthusiasts.
- **Features:**
  - Search by sequence, keyword, or formula
  - Graphs and plots of sequences
  - Audio representations (MIDI files)
  - Code implementations (Maple, Mathematica, PARI, etc.)
  - Cross-references between related sequences
- **Access Level:** Completely free
- **Citation:** OEIS Foundation Inc. (2026), The On-Line Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences, Published electronically at https://oeis.org.

### Digital Library of Mathematical Functions (DLMF)
- **URL:** https://dlmf.nist.gov/
- **Type:** Reference Work
- **Description:** Official successor to Abramowitz and Stegun's Handbook of Mathematical Functions. Comprehensive reference on special functions.
- **Features:** Interactive 3D graphics, MathML, downloadable data, references
- **Access Level:** Completely free

### ncatlab
- **URL:** https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/HomePage
- **Type:** Mathematics and Physics Wiki
- **Description:** Lab book for collaborative work on mathematics, physics, and philosophy. Focuses on higher category theory and related fields.
- **Access Level:** Completely free

### MathSciNet
- **URL:** https://mathscinet.ams.org/
- **Type:** Mathematical Reviews Database
- **Description:** Comprehensive database of mathematical publications maintained by the American Mathematical Society. Contains over 4 million items with expert reviews.
- **Access Level:** Subscription required (available through academic institutions)
- **Features:** Author identification, citation tracking, reviews of mathematical literature
