# Physics in Russia: A Comprehensive Overview

Russia has historically been one of the world's strongest nations in physics research, with a legacy spanning from the Russian Empire through the Soviet era to the present day. This document provides a comprehensive overview of Russian physics institutions, notable scientists, research areas, and international collaborations.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies and the Academy Structure

### Russian Academy of Sciences (RAS)

The **Russian Academy of Sciences** (Российская академия наук, РАН) is the national academy of Russia and the supreme scientific institution in the country.

**History and Structure:**
- Founded: February 8, 1724, in Saint Petersburg by Peter the Great (originally as the St. Petersburg Academy of Sciences)
- Headquarters: Moscow (since 1934)
- Current President: Gennady Krasnikov (since September 2022)
- Membership: ~1,960 living Russian members (841 full, 1,119 corresponding) and ~445 foreign members

**Organization:**
- 13 specialized scientific divisions
- 4 territorial branches:
  - **Siberian Branch (SB RAS)** - 12,500+ researchers, 199 Academy members
  - **Ural Branch (UB RAS)** - 113 Academy members
  - **St. Petersburg Branch (SPbB RAS)** - 185 Academy members
  - **Far East Branch (FEB RAS)** - 71 Academy members
- 15 regional scientific centers
- ~1,008 research institutions and units
- ~125,000 total employees (~47,000 researchers)

**Key Physics-Related Institutes under RAS:**
- Lebedev Physical Institute (FIAN)
- Ioffe Physical-Technical Institute
- Landau Institute for Theoretical Physics
- Budker Institute of Nuclear Physics
- Institute for Theoretical and Experimental Physics (ITEP)
- Institute for High Energy Physics (IHEP)
- Keldysh Institute of Applied Mathematics
- Space Research Institute (IKI)

### Russian Physical Society

The Russian Physical Society (RPS) serves as the professional organization for physicists in Russia, organizing conferences, publishing journals, and promoting physics education.

## 2. Major University Physics Departments

### Moscow State University (MSU)

**M.V. Lomonosov Moscow State University** is Russia's oldest and most prestigious university.

**Overview:**
- Founded: January 23, 1755
- Location: Moscow (main campus on Sparrow Hills)
- Students: ~39,000
- Faculty: ~5,000

**Physics at MSU:**
- Faculty of Physics - one of the largest and most renowned physics faculties globally
- Skobeltsyn Institute of Nuclear Physics
- Sternberg Astronomical Institute
- N.N. Bogolyubov Institute for Theoretical Problems of Microphysics
- Research Computing Center (supercomputing facilities)

**Notable Physics Alumni:**
- Andrei Sakharov
- Vitaly Ginzburg
- Vladimir Arnold
- Andrey Kolmogorov
- Grigory Margulis
- Yakov Sinai

### Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology (MIPT/PhysTech)

Often called the "MIT of Russia," MIPT is Russia's leading specialized physics and technology university.

**Overview:**
- Founded: November 25, 1946 (officially established 1951)
- Location: Dolgoprudny, Moscow Oblast
- Students: ~6,000
- Founder: Pyotr Kapitsa (Nobel Laureate)

**The "Phystech System":**
- Rigorous selection of gifted students
- Education by leading scientists in research environments
- Individualized curriculum
- Base organizations system (students work at research institutes from 3rd year)

**Departments/Schools:**
- Landau Phystech School of Physics and Research
- Phystech School of Applied Mathematics and Computer Science
- Phystech School of Radio Engineering and Computer Technology
- Phystech School of Electronics, Photonics and Molecular Physics
- I.V. Kurchatov Phystech School of Nature-like, Plasma and Nuclear Technologies

**Base Organizations Include:**
- Kurchatov Institute
- Lebedev Physical Institute (FIAN)
- Landau Institute
- Joint Institute for Nuclear Research
- Space Research Institute
- ITEP

**Nobel Laureates Affiliated:**
- Lev Landau
- Pyotr Kapitsa
- Nikolay Semyonov
- Vitaly Ginzburg
- Alexandr Prokhorov
- Alexei Abrikosov
- Igor Tamm
- Andrei Sakharov
- Andre Geim
- Konstantin Novoselov

### Saint Petersburg State University (SPbU)

**Overview:**
- Founded: 1724 (claims succession from Peter the Great's Academy)
- Location: Saint Petersburg
- Students: ~32,400

**Physics at SPbU:**
- Faculty of Physics (located in Peterhof campus)
- Faculty of Mathematics and Mechanics
- Faculty of Applied Mathematics and Control Processes

**Nobel Laureate Alumni:**
- Lev Landau (Physics, 1962)
- Aleksandr Prokhorov (Physics, 1964)
- Nikolay Semyonov (Chemistry, 1956)

### Novosibirsk State University (NSU)

Located in the heart of Siberia's "Academgorodok" (Academic Town).

**Overview:**
- Founded: 1958
- Location: Novosibirsk (Akademgorodok)
- Students: ~7,200

**Physics at NSU:**
- Faculty of Physics
- Interdisciplinary Centre for Elementary Particle Physics and Astrophysics (ICEPPA)
- Close integration with Siberian Branch of RAS

**Research Areas:**
- Elementary particle physics
- Photonics and quantum optics
- Astrophysics and cosmic rays

**Notable Alumni:**
- Arkady Vainshtein (Dirac Medal, 2016)
- Vladimir Zakharov (Dirac Medal, 2003)
- Efim Zelmanov (Fields Medal, 1994)

### Other Notable Universities:

- **Saint Petersburg Polytechnic University** - Historic physics and engineering programs
- **Tomsk State University** - Strong physics department in Siberia
- **Ural Federal University (Yekaterinburg)** - Physics and materials science
- **Kazan Federal University** - Historic physics tradition
- **National Research Nuclear University MEPhI** - Nuclear physics specialist

## 3. National Research Centers

### Kurchatov Institute

**National Research Centre "Kurchatov Institute"** is Russia's leading nuclear research institution.

**Overview:**
- Founded: February 11, 1943 (as Laboratory No. 2)
- Location: Moscow
- Named after: Igor Kurchatov (father of Soviet atomic bomb)

**Key Areas:**
- Nuclear energy and reactors
- Thermonuclear fusion and plasma physics
- Solid state physics
- Superconductivity
- Nanotechnology

**Historical Significance:**
- Designed majority of Soviet nuclear reactors
- Home of F-1 reactor (first non-American reactor to achieve criticality, 1946)
- Birthplace of the tokamak concept
- Developed T-3 tokamak that sparked global fusion research

**Tokamak Development:**
| Tokamak | Year | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| TMF | 1955 | First tokamak experiment |
| T-1 | 1958 | First tokamak with all-metal chamber |
| T-3 | 1960 | First to produce thermonuclear reactions |
| T-4 | 1971 | First with carbon limiter |
| T-7 | 1979 | First with superconducting magnets |
| T-15 | 1988 | First large-scale superconducting tokamak |
| T-15MD | 2021 | Supports ITER research |

### Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR/Dubna)

**JINR** is an international research center for nuclear sciences located in Dubna, 110 km north of Moscow.

**Overview:**
- Founded: March 26, 1956
- Membership: 13 countries (Russia, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Vietnam, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Cuba, Moldova, Mongolia, Romania, Uzbekistan, Egypt)
- Staff: ~5,500 (including ~1,200 researchers with PhDs)
- Director General: Grigory Trubnikov

**Laboratories:**
1. **Bogoliubov Laboratory of Theoretical Physics (BLTP)** - Theoretical physics
2. **Veksler and Baldin Laboratory of High Energy Physics (VBLHE)** - Nuclotron, NICA
3. **Laboratory of Particle Physics (LPP)** - Particle physics
4. **Dzhelepov Laboratory of Nuclear Problems (DLNP)** - Synchrocyclotron
5. **Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions (FLNR)** - Superheavy element synthesis
6. **Frank Laboratory of Neutron Physics (FLNP)** - IBR-2 reactor, neutron research
7. **Laboratory of Information Technologies (LIT)** - Computing
8. **Laboratory of Radiation Biology (LRB)** - Radiobiology

**Major Facilities:**
- **Nuclotron** - Superconductive particle accelerator (7 GeV)
- **Synchrophasotron** - 4 GeV proton accelerator
- **IBR-2** - High-flux pulsed fast-neutron reactor
- **NICA** (Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility) - Heavy ion collider
- **Superheavy Element Factory** - Opened 2019, tenfold increase in beam intensity

**Scientific Achievements:**
- 1957: Prediction of neutrino oscillation (Bruno Pontecorvo)
- Element synthesis: 107 (Bohrium), 114 (Flerovium), 115 (Moscovium), 116 (Livermorium), 117 (Tennessine), 118 (Oganesson)

### Ioffe Institute

**Ioffe Physical-Technical Institute** is one of Russia's largest physics research centers, located in Saint Petersburg.

**Overview:**
- Founded: September 23, 1918
- Location: Saint Petersburg
- Staff: ~2,000 (including ~560 PhD, ~250 Doctor of Science)
- Director: Sergei V. Ivanov

**Historical Significance:**
- Considered the "cradle of Soviet physics"
- Directed by Abram Ioffe for decades
- Landau, Kapitsa, Kurchatov, Tamm, Zeldovich worked here

**Research Divisions:**
- Center for Nano-Heterostructure Physics
- Solid State Electronics
- Solid State Physics
- Plasma Physics, Atomic Physics and Astrophysics
- Physics of Dielectrics and Semiconductors

**Nobel Prizes for Work at Ioffe Institute:**
- 1956: Nikolay Semyonov (Chemistry) - Chain reactions
- 2000: Zhores Alferov (Physics) - Semiconductor heterostructures

### Lebedev Physical Institute (FIAN)

**P.N. Lebedev Physical Institute** is Russia's premier physics institute in Moscow.

**Overview:**
- Founded: 1934
- Location: Moscow
- Staff: ~1,600
- Director: Nikolai Kolachevsky

**Nobel Prizes to FIAN Scientists:**
- 1958: Pavel Cherenkov, Igor Tamm, Ilya Frank - Cherenkov-Vavilov effect
- 1964: Nikolay Basov, Alexander Prokhorov - Laser/maser principle
- 2003: Vitaly Ginzburg - Superconductivity and superfluids theory

**Research Areas:**
- Laser technology
- Dark matter
- Nanostructures
- Superconductivity
- Cosmic rays
- Gamma-astronomy
- Crystallography (cubic zirconia/Fianit)

**Facilities:**
- 1.2 GeV electron synchrotron "Pakhra" (Troitsk)
- Andreyev Acoustics Institute

### Budker Institute of Nuclear Physics (BINP)

Located in Novosibirsk's Akademgorodok.

**Overview:**
- Founded: 1959
- Location: Novosibirsk (Akademgorodok)
- Staff: ~3,000
- Founder: Gersh Budker
- Director: Pavel V. Logatchov

**Focus Areas:**
- High-energy physics
- Particle physics
- Plasma physics
- Synchrotron radiation
- Accelerator physics

**Major Facilities:**
- **VEPP-4** - e+e- collider (up to 12 GeV)
- **VEPP-2000** - e+e- collider (0.4-2.0 GeV)
- **Siberian Synchrotron Radiation Centre**
- **NovoFEL** - Novosibirsk Free Electron Laser
- **GOL-3** - Long open plasma trap
- **BNCT** - Neutron source for cancer therapy

**Historical Note:**
- Built VEP-1 in 1961 (first Soviet particle collider)
- Contributed to CERN's LHC (provided beamline magnets, 1993-2001)

### Institute for Theoretical and Experimental Physics (ITEP)

**Overview:**
- Founded: December 1, 1945 (as Laboratory No. 3)
- Location: Moscow
- Founder: Abram Alikhanov
- Director: Viktor Yegorychev

**Research Areas:**
- Theoretical and mathematical physics
- Quantum field theory and string theory
- High energy particle physics (CERN collaborations)
- Nuclear physics
- Plasma physics
- Solid state physics
- Nanotechnology
- Medical physics (PET devices, proton therapy)

**Historical Significance:**
- Tradition of Landau school
- Isaak Pomeranchuk led seminars from 1950s
- Quantum chromodynamics breakthroughs (Shifman, Ioffe, Vainshtein)

**Pomeranchuk Prize:**
- Annual international award for theoretical physics since 1998

## 4. Major Research Facilities

### Tokamaks and Fusion Research

Russia invented the tokamak and remains a leader in fusion research:

**Russian Tokamak Program (Kurchatov Institute):**
- T-15MD - Current flagship, supports ITER
- T-10, T-11 - Historical milestones
- T-7 - First superconducting tokamak (1979)

**International Projects:**
- **ITER** - Russia is a key partner (9.1% contribution)
  - Supplies superconducting conductors (Nb3Sn and NbTi)
  - Manufacturing first wall panels
  - Plasma component testing at Efremov Institute

### Particle Accelerators

**In Russia:**
- **Nuclotron** (JINR) - 7 GeV superconducting synchrotron
- **Synchrophasotron** (JINR) - 4 GeV proton accelerator
- **VEPP-4** (BINP) - 12 GeV electron-positron collider
- **Synchrocyclotron** (JINR) - 680 MeV

### Neutron Sources

- **IBR-2** (JINR) - High-flux pulsed fast-neutron reactor
- **IREN** (JINR) - Neutron source facility

### Synchrotron Radiation Facilities

- **Siberian Synchrotron Radiation Centre** (BINP)
- **NovoFEL** (BINP) - Free electron laser

### Space Research

- **Space Research Institute (IKI RAS)** - Russia's premier space science institute
- Specializes in planetary science, astrophysics, space plasma physics

### Biographies of Key Figures

#### Lev Landau (1908-1968)
- **Achievement:** Nobel Prize 1962 for superfluidity theory
- **Contributions:** Landau damping, Fermi liquid theory, Ginzburg-Landau theory, Course of Theoretical Physics (10 volumes with Lifshitz)
- **Legacy:** Landau Institute for Theoretical Physics named after him

#### Pyotr Kapitsa (1894-1984)
- **Achievement:** Nobel Prize 1978 for low-temperature physics
- **Contributions:** Discovery of superfluidity, ultrastrong magnetic fields, founder of MIPT
- **History:** Worked with Rutherford at Cambridge; prevented from returning to UK in 1934

#### Andrei Sakharov (1921-1989)
- **Achievement:** Nobel Peace Prize 1975 for human rights advocacy
- **Contributions:** Co-inventor of the tokamak, "Third Idea" for hydrogen bomb, Sakharov conditions for baryogenesis
- **Legacy:** Sakharov Prize for Freedom of Thought

#### Igor Kurchatov (1903-1960)
- **Title:** "Father of the Soviet atomic bomb"
- **Contributions:** Led Soviet nuclear program, initiated tokamak research

#### Vitaly Ginzburg (1916-2009)
- **Achievement:** Nobel Prize 2003 for superconductivity theory
- **Contributions:** Ginzburg-Landau theory, Soviet H-bomb development

#### Yakov Zeldovich (1914-1987)
- **Contributions:** Cosmology, black hole radiation (before Hawking), combustion theory, Soviet nuclear program

#### Alexei Abrikosov (1928-2017)
- **Achievement:** Nobel Prize 2003 for superconductivity theory
- **Contributions:** Type-II superconductors, vortex lattice (Abrikosov vortices)

#### Nikolay Semyonov (1896-1986)
- **Achievement:** Nobel Prize in Chemistry 1956 for chain reaction theory
- **First Russian Nobel laureate in Chemistry**

#### Andre Geim (b. 1958) and Konstantin Novoselov (b. 1974)
- **Achievement:** Nobel Prize 2010 for graphene discovery
- **Background:** Both MIPT graduates; worked at University of Manchester

### Other Notable Physicists

- **L.D. Landau's Students:** Evgeny Lifshitz, Lev Pitaevskii, Isaak Khalatnikov
- **Theoretical Physics:** Alexander Polyakov, Sasha Zamolodchikov, Karen Ter-Martirosian
- **Plasma Physics:** Roald Sagdeev, Lev Artsimovich
- **Particle Physics:** Vladimir Veksler, Bruno Pontecorvo
- **Condensed Matter:** Boris Rauschenbach, Alexei Kitaev

## 6. Research Areas of Strength

### Plasma Physics and Fusion

Russia's strongest and most historic field:
- **Tokamak invention** and development
- **ITER participation** as key partner
- Plasma confinement and stability theory
- Industrial plasma applications

### Nuclear Physics

- **Heavy ion physics** (JINR is world leader)
- **Superheavy element synthesis** (elements 113-118 discovered at Dubna)
- **Neutron physics**
- **Nuclear reactors** and nuclear energy

### Condensed Matter Physics

- **Superconductivity** - Ginzburg-Landau theory, type-II superconductors
- **Superfluidity** - Kapitsa and Landau's work
- **Semiconductors** - Alferov's heterostructures
- **Graphene** - Geim and Novoselov's discovery
- **Low-temperature physics**

### Theoretical Physics

- **Landau school** tradition
- **Quantum field theory**
- **String theory** (strong contributions)
- **Mathematical physics**

### High Energy Physics

- **Particle physics** at JINR, BINP, ITEP
- **Cosmic ray physics**
- **Astrophysics** and cosmology

### Laser Physics and Quantum Electronics

- **Maser/laser development** (Basov, Prokhorov)
- **Free electron lasers** (NovoFEL)
- **Quantum optics**

## 7. International Collaborations

### CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)

**Russia's Role:**
- Observer status since 1993 (suspended March 2022)
- Russian scientists participated in:
  - CMS and ATLAS experiments (LHC)
  - ALICE experiment
  - Various fixed-target experiments

**Contributions:**
- Detector components
- Computing resources
- Scientific personnel

### ITER (International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor)

**Russia is one of 7 member parties** (along with EU, China, India, Japan, South Korea, US)

**Russian Contributions:**
- 9.1% of construction costs (in-kind)
- Superconducting conductors:
  - 22 km of Nb3Sn conductors (90 tonnes) for toroidal field coils
  - 11 km of NbTi conductors (40 tonnes) for poloidal field coils
- First wall panels (179 panels)
- Thermal testing of plasma-facing components
- Divertor testing at Efremov Institute
- Scientific expertise in tokamak physics

### JINR International Collaboration

**Member States:** 13 countries
**Associate Members:** Germany, Hungary, Italy, South Africa, Serbia

**Collaborations:**
- CERN (observer status suspended 2022)
- UNESCO (since 1997)
- BMBF (Germany)
- INFN (Italy)
- University of Turin
- EPS (European Physical Society)

### Other Collaborations

**FAIR (Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research), Germany:**
- Russia is participating in FAIR construction

**European XFEL, Germany:**
- Russian participation through Kurchatov Institute

**ESRF (European Synchrotron Radiation Facility), France:**
- Russian research groups participate

**LHC Experiments:**
- ITEP, JINR, MSU, and other institutes participate in CMS, ATLAS, ALICE

**Neutron Sources:**
- Collaboration with ILL (France)
- Spallation Neutron Source (US)

## 8. Current Challenges and Developments

### Recent Changes

**2013 Academy Reform:**
- RAS restructuring
- Creation of Federal Agency for Scientific Organizations (FASO)
- Later merged into Ministry of Science and Higher Education

**International Sanctions (2022-):**
- CERN suspended Russia's observer status
- JINR collaborations affected
- Some international partnerships paused
- European universities suspended partnerships

### Ongoing Strengths

- Strong theoretical physics tradition (Landau Institute)
- World-leading nuclear and plasma physics
- Strong mathematics foundation
- Homegrown talent development (MIPT system)
- Significant investment in major facilities (NICA, Superheavy Element Factory)

### Key Future Projects

- **NICA** (JINR) - Heavy ion collider for quark-gluon plasma research
- **T-15MD** tokamak - ITER support and domestic fusion research
- **Superheavy Element Factory** - Discovery of elements 119 and 120
- **SKIF** (Siberian Circular Photon Source) - 4th generation synchrotron
