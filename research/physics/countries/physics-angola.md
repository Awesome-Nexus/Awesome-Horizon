# Physics in Angola: Comprehensive Research Report

## Executive Summary

Angola's physics research infrastructure is in a developmental phase, with significant investments being made since the end of the civil war in 2002 to build scientific capacity. The country has established foundational institutions including the Angola Academy of Science (created 2020), FUNDECIT for research funding (2021), and multiple universities offering physics and related science programs. Physics education is primarily focused on teacher training, with growing research capabilities in nuclear physics through IAEA partnerships. International collaborations, particularly with Portugal, Brazil, South Africa, and organizations like TWAS and IAEA, play a crucial role in advancing physics research in Angola.

## 1. National Physics Societies and Associations

### 1.1 Angola Academy of Science (AAS)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Full Name** | Academia Angolana de Ciências (AAS) / Angola Academy of Science |
| **Founded** | December 2020 |
| **Type** | Private association (non-governmental) |
| **Legal Status** | Private legal entity under Angolan law |
| **Website** | Not available |

**History and Establishment:**
The Angola Academy of Science was established in December 2020 with support from the Technology Bank for LDCs and NASAC (Network of African Science Academies). It represents a significant milestone in Angola's scientific development, creating a formal structure for scientific advice to government and promotion of research excellence.

**Membership:**
- In its first membership call, 40 candidates applied for fellowship
- Membership criteria based on scientific excellence and contributions to Angolan science
- Aims to provide independent scientific advice to the government

**Key Activities:**
- Advising government on science and technology policies
- Promoting scientific research excellence
- Building scientific capacity in Angola
- International networking with other African science academies

## 5. Physics Education

### 5.1 Educational System Structure

Angola's higher education system is transitioning toward the LMD (Licence-Master-Doctorate) structure, aligning with international standards.

| Degree | Duration | Description |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Licenciatura** | 4-5 years | Bachelor's equivalent |
| **Mestrado** | 2 years | Master's degree |
| **Doutoramento** | 3-4 years | PhD/Doctorate |

### 5.2 Physics Education Programs

**Current Status:**
- Physics is primarily taught as part of teacher training programs
- Most public universities offer physics courses within science education degrees
- Dedicated physics degrees are limited but developing

**Key Physics Education Areas:**
1. **Physics Teacher Training** - Preparing secondary school physics teachers
2. **General Science Education** - Physics as component of science degrees
3. **Applied Physics** - Engineering and technology applications
4. **Nuclear Physics** - Specialized training at UAN

### 5.3 Languages of Instruction

| Level | Language |
|-------|----------|
| Undergraduate | Portuguese (primary) |
| Graduate Programs | Portuguese, some English |

### 5.4 Challenges in Physics Education

Based on recent research publications, Angola faces several challenges in physics education:

1. **Limited Specialized Faculty**: Shortage of PhD-level physics educators
2. **Infrastructure Constraints**: Laboratory equipment and facilities need development
3. **Research Integration**: Ongoing efforts to integrate research into undergraduate programs
4. **Teacher Training**: Need for more specialized physics teacher preparation

## Conclusion

Angola's physics research infrastructure is in an active developmental phase with significant recent investments:

**Key Developments:**
- Establishment of the Angola Academy of Science (2020) providing institutional framework for scientific excellence
- Creation of FUNDECIT (2021) as a dedicated research funding agency
- Reestablishment of the Nuclear Physics Applications Laboratory with IAEA support
- Opening of the Luanda Science Centre for public science engagement

**Strengths:**
- Strong government commitment to science and technology (NDP 2018-2022)
- Active international partnerships with Portugal, Brazil, South Africa, and international organizations
- Growing network of universities offering physics education
- Increasing participation in international scientific programs

**Challenges:**
- Limited number of dedicated physics research centers
- Small community of specialized physicists
- Ongoing need for infrastructure development
- Need for more advanced physics degree programs beyond teacher training

**Future Outlook:**
With continued government investment and international collaboration, Angola is positioned to develop stronger physics research capabilities. The focus on nuclear physics applications, combined with educational investments through FUNDECIT, suggests a trajectory toward greater scientific capacity in the coming decades.
