## Notable Physics Research Areas

### Key Research Strengths in Ireland

1. **Photonics and Optoelectronics**
   - Integrated photonics and optical communications
   - LED technology and visible light communications
   - Photonic integrated circuits (PICs)
   - Strong activity at Tyndall National Institute, IPIC, and multiple universities

2. **Quantum Technologies**
   - Quantum computing and quantum simulation
   - Quantum communications and cryptography
   - Quantum sensing and metrology
   - Research at Maynooth University, TCD, UCD, and Tyndall

3. **Astrophysics and Cosmology**
   - Radio astronomy (LOFAR, JWST involvement)
   - High-energy astrophysics and cosmic rays
   - Space weather research
   - Active at DIAS School of Cosmic Physics, UCC, UCD

4. **Particle Physics**
   - CERN collaborations (ATLAS, CMS, LHCb)
   - Theoretical particle physics
   - Medical applications of particle physics

5. **Condensed Matter and Materials Physics**
   - Semiconductor physics and devices
   - Nanomaterials and 2D materials
   - Spintronics and magnetism
   - Strong at Tyndall, AMBER, CRANN

6. **Plasma Physics**
   - Fusion research and plasma applications
   - Low-temperature plasmas
   - DCU National Centre for Plasma Science and Technology

7. **Computational Physics**
   - High-performance computing applications
   - Computational materials science
   - Climate and environmental modeling (ICHEC)

8. **Medical Physics**
   - Radiation therapy physics
   - Diagnostic imaging
   - Biomedical engineering
   - Research at UCD, UCC, Galway

9. **Geophysics**
   - Earth structure and seismology
   - Geothermal energy
   - DIAS School of Cosmic Physics

10. **Energy Physics**
    - Solar energy and photovoltaics
    - Battery technology
    - Sustainable aviation fuels

## International Collaborations

### CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)
- **Status**: Ireland became an Associate Member State of CERN on **22 October 2025**
- **History**: Irish scientists have collaborated with CERN since the 1960s (bubble chamber experiments)
- **Current Involvement**: ATLAS, CMS, LHCb, and ISOLDE experiments
- **Benefits**: Irish representatives participate in CERN Council meetings; Irish nationals eligible for staff positions and graduate programmes; Irish firms can bid for CERN contracts
- **Key Institutions**: UCD (LHCb and CMS experiments since 2002), MTU (ATLAS Technical Associate Institute since 2024)

### European Space Agency (ESA)
- Irish companies and researchers participate in ESA programmes
- DIAS involved in space weather and astrophysics research

### European Southern Observatory (ESO)
- Irish participation in European astronomy facilities

### LOFAR (Low-Frequency Array)
- Ireland participates in the international LOFAR radio telescope
- DIAS involved in radio astronomy research
- Prof Peter Gallagher elected Chair of LOFAR ERIC Council (2026)

### James Webb Space Telescope (JWST)
- DIAS School of Cosmic Physics contributed to JWST development
- Irish researchers awarded observing time on JWST

### European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL)
- Physics-related biological research collaborations

### International Collaborations - Research
- **Nordita** (Nordic Institute for Theoretical Physics) - collaborative research
- **Max Planck Institutes** - joint research programmes
- **MIT Media Lab** - Tyndall collaboration
- **University of Cambridge Cavendish Laboratory** - historic and ongoing collaborations

### International Industry Partnerships
- Tyndall National Institute works with 200+ industry partners worldwide
- Strong partnerships with Intel, Analog Devices, and other technology companies

## Notable Physicists from Ireland

### Nobel Laureates in Physics

| Year | Laureate | Born | Died | Contribution |
|------|----------|------|------|--------------|
| 1951 | **Ernest Thomas Sinton Walton** | 1903, Dungarvan, Co. Waterford | 1995 | "For their pioneer work on the transmutation of atomic nuclei by artificially accelerated atomic particles." Shared with John Cockcroft. First to artificially split the atom (1932). Only Irish-born Nobel laureate in Physics |

### Historic Physicists (19th Century)

| Physicist | Born | Died | Contribution |
|-----------|------|------|--------------|
| **Sir William Rowan Hamilton** | 1805, Dublin | 1865 | Mathematician and physicist. Reformulated classical mechanics (Hamiltonian mechanics), fundamental to quantum mechanics. Discovered quaternions (1843). Director of Dunsink Observatory. Arguably the most influential Irish physicist |
| **George Francis FitzGerald** | 1851, Dublin | 1901 | Theoretical physicist. Proposed the FitzGerald-Lorentz contraction to explain negative result of Michelson-Morley experiment. Introduced the term "electron". One of "The Maxwellians" who developed Maxwell's electromagnetic theory |
| **Nicholas Callan** | 1799, Dundalk, Co. Louth | 1864 | Priest and physicist. Invented the induction coil (1836) - the world's first transformer. Professor of Natural Philosophy at Maynooth College (1826-1864). Invented the Maynooth Battery |
| **John Tyndall** | 1820, Leighlinbridge, Co. Carlow | 1893 | Physicist. Explained why the sky is blue (Tyndall Effect). Pioneer in study of heat, sound, light. Demonstrated light-pipe (forerunner of optical fiber). Namesake of Tyndall National Institute |

### 20th Century Physicists

| Physicist | Born | Died | Contribution |
|-----------|------|------|--------------|
| **John Stewart Bell** | 1928, Belfast | 1990 | Theoretical physicist. Originator of Bell's Theorem (1964), fundamental to quantum entanglement and quantum information. Showed that quantum mechanics is incompatible with local hidden variable theories. Worked at CERN |
| **Lochlainn O'Raifeartaigh** | 1933, Dublin | 2000 | Theoretical physicist. Known for O'Raifeartaigh Theorem in quantum field theory. Made significant contributions to supersymmetry and group theory |
| **John J. Nolan** | 1888 | 1952 | Physicist. First Chairman of DIAS School of Cosmic Physics. Research on atmospheric electricity and cosmic rays |
| **Patrick M.S. Blackett** | 1897, London (of Irish descent) | 1974 | Nobel Prize in Physics 1948. Developed Wilson cloud chamber and discoveries in nuclear physics and cosmic rays |
