# Physics in China: A Comprehensive Overview

## Executive Summary

China is one of the world's leading nations in physics research and education, with a rich history dating back centuries and a rapidly expanding modern research infrastructure. The country has produced Nobel laureates, operates world-class research facilities, and maintains extensive international collaborations. This document provides a comprehensive overview of China's physics landscape, including national societies, university departments, research centers, major facilities, notable physicists, research strengths, and international partnerships.

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Chinese Physical Society (CPS)

**Founded:** 1932  
**Membership:** ~40,000 members  
**Headquarters:** Beijing  
**Website:** http://www.cps-net.org.cn/

The **Chinese Physical Society (CPS)** is the primary professional organization for physicists in China. Established in 1932, it is part of the China Association for Science and Technology and represents the physics community at the national level.

**Key Activities:**
- Organization of national physics conferences and seminars
- Publication of peer-reviewed scientific journals
- Promotion of physics education and public outreach
- Professional development for physicists
- Policy advisory to the government on physics-related matters

**International Affiliations:**
- Member of the **International Union of Pure and Applied Physics (IUPAP)** since 1984
- Member of the **Association of Asia Pacific Physical Societies (AAPPS)** since 1990

### Published Journals

The CPS publishes numerous academic journals covering various physics disciplines:

## 4. Major Research Facilities

### 4.1 FAST Telescope (五百米口径球面射电望远镜)

**Name:** Five-hundred-meter Aperture Spherical Telescope  
**Nickname:** Tianyan (天眼, "Sky's Eye")  
**Location:** Jinke Village, Pingtang County, Guizhou Province  
**Diameter:** 500 meters (world's largest single-dish radio telescope)  
**First Light:** September 2016  
**Fully Operational:** January 2020  
**Cost:** ¥1.2 billion (~$180 million)

**Technical Specifications:**
- Active surface with 4,450 triangular panels
- 300-meter effective illuminated aperture
- Operating frequency: 70 MHz – 3.0 GHz
- Feed cabin suspended 140 meters above the dish
- Pointing precision: 8 arcseconds

**Science Objectives:**
- Large-scale neutral hydrogen surveys
- Pulsar observations and timing arrays
- VLBI network leadership
- Interstellar molecule detection
- SETI (Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence)

**Key Achievements:**
- First discovery: Two new pulsars (August 2017)
- Over 500 new pulsars discovered by 2021
- Joined Breakthrough Listen SETI project (2016)
- Open to global scientific community since 2021

**Chief Scientist:** Nan Rendong (1945–2017), who led the project from its proposal in 1994 until his death.

### 4.2 EAST Tokamak (先进超导托卡马克实验装置)

**Name:** Experimental Advanced Superconducting Tokamak  
**Location:** Hefei, Anhui Province  
**Operated by:** Institute of Plasma Physics, Hefei Institutes of Physical Science, CAS  
**Operation Start:** 2006

**Technical Specifications:**
- First tokamak with fully superconducting magnets (both toroidal and poloidal)
- Major radius: 1.85 m
- Minor radius: 0.45 m
- Magnetic field: 3.5 T
- Plasma current: 1.0 MA
- Plasma temperature: up to 100 million °C

**Record Achievements:**
- January 2025: Sustained plasma for 1,066 seconds (world record)
- 2021: 1,056-second steady-state high-parameter plasma operation
- 2023: 403-second steady-state H-mode plasma
- 2018: Achieved ~100 million °C electron temperature

**Role:** Testbed for ITER technologies and demonstration of long-pulse fusion plasma operation.

### 4.3 Shanghai Synchrotron Radiation Facility (SSRF)

**Location:** Zhangjiang Hi-Tech Park, Shanghai Pudong  
**Operated by:** Shanghai Institute of Applied Physics (SINAP)  
**Circumference:** 432 meters  
**Energy:** 3.5 GeV  
**Operation Start:** May 2009

**Technical Features:**
- Third-generation synchrotron light source
- One of the world's most advanced medium-energy synchrotrons
- 8+ beamlines for various research applications

**Research Applications:**
- Structural biology and protein crystallography
- Materials science
- Environmental science
- Cultural heritage studies
- Cancer research

### 4.4 China Spallation Neutron Source (CSNS)

**Location:** Dongguan, Guangdong Province  
**Operation Start:** 2018

China's first and only pulsed spallation neutron source, used for:
- Materials structure research
- Condensed matter physics
- Chemistry and biology applications

### 4.5 Beijing Electron-Positron Collider II (BEPC II)

**Location:** Beijing  
**Operated by:** IHEP

- Electron-positron collider optimized for τ-charm physics
- Energy range: 1.0–2.5 GeV
- Complemented by Beijing Spectrometer III (BES III) detector

**Scientific Goals:**
- Precision measurements of charmonium and tau lepton
- Light hadron spectroscopy
- R&D for future accelerators

### 4.6 Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory (JUNO)

**Location:** Jiangmen, Guangdong Province  
**Status:** Under construction, expected completion ~2024  
**Depth:** 700 meters underground

**Scientific Goals:**
- Determine neutrino mass hierarchy
- Precise measurement of oscillation parameters
- Supernova neutrino detection
- Proton decay search
- Geoneutrino studies

**Features:**
- 20,000-ton liquid scintillator detector
- Largest liquid scintillator detector in the world
- Energy resolution target: 3% at 1 MeV

### 4.7 LHAASO (Large High Altitude Air Shower Observatory)

**Location:** Daocheng, Sichuan Province  
**Altitude:** 4,410 meters  
**Operation Start:** 2021

**Purpose:** Detection and study of high-energy gamma rays and cosmic rays

**Components:**
- 1 km² km² array of 5,195 electromagnetic particle detectors
- 1,188 muon detectors
- 3,120 water Cherenkov detectors
- Wide-field air Cherenkov telescopes

**Key Discovery:** Detection of photons with energies exceeding 1 PeV (10^15 eV) from the Milky Way.

### 4.8 Dark Matter Particle Explorer (DAMPE)

**Launch Date:** December 2015  
**Orbit:** Sun-synchronous orbit at 500 km

China's first dark matter detection satellite, designed to:
- Detect high-energy electrons and gamma rays
- Search for dark matter particle annihilation signatures
- Study cosmic ray origins and propagation

### 4.9 Proposed: Circular Electron Positron Collider (CEPC)

**Proposed Location:** Likely near Beijing  
**Circumference:** ~100 km  
**Target:** Higgs boson production and precision measurement

**Current Status:** Under discussion and design study; has faced debate about prioritization vs. other science investments.

## 5. Notable Physicists and Nobel Laureates

### 5.1 Nobel Laureates in Physics of Chinese Heritage

#### Chen-Ning Yang (杨振宁)

**Born:** October 1, 1922 (Hefei, Anhui)  
**Died:** October 18, 2025  
**Nobel Prize:** 1957 (with T. D. Lee)

**Major Contributions:**
- **Yang-Mills Theory (1954):** Non-abelian gauge theory, foundation of the Standard Model
- **Parity Non-Conservation:** Proposed violation of parity in weak interactions (experimentally confirmed by Chien-Shiung Wu)
- **Lee-Yang Theorem:** Statistical mechanics of phase transitions
- **Yang-Baxter Equation:** Mathematical physics and integrable systems

**Career:**
- Institute for Advanced Study, Princeton (1949–1965)
- Stony Brook University (1965–1999)
- Tsinghua University (honorary director, returned to China after retirement)

**Legacy:** Yang's work laid the foundation for much of modern particle physics. At least 10 Nobel laureates cited his work in their Nobel lectures.

#### Tsung-Dao Lee (李政道)

**Born:** November 24, 1926 (Shanghai)  
**Died:** August 4, 2024  
**Nobel Prize:** 1957 (with C. N. Yang), at age 30

**Major Contributions:**
- Parity violation in weak interactions
- Lee-Yang theorem in statistical mechanics
- Soliton stars and non-topological solitons
- Relativistic heavy ion physics

**Career:** Columbia University (1953–2012)

**Educational Impact:**
- CUSPEA program (China-U.S. Physics Examination and Application): Sent thousands of Chinese students to U.S. graduate programs (1979–1989)
- Established Chun-Tsung scholarships at major Chinese universities

#### Samuel C. C. Ting (丁肇中)

**Born:** January 27, 1936 (Ann Arbor, Michigan, USA)  
**Nobel Prize:** 1976

**Major Contributions:**
- Discovery of the J/ψ particle (1974), providing first evidence for charm quarks
- Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer (AMS) experiment on the International Space Station

**Career:**
- MIT (1969–present)
- CERN (1963)
- DESY, Germany (1965–1969)

**AMS Experiment:** $2 billion cosmic ray detector involving 500 scientists from 56 institutions in 16 countries, searching for antimatter and dark matter.

### 5.2 Other Eminent Chinese Physicists

#### Chien-Shiung Wu (吴健雄)

**Born:** May 31, 1912 (Liuhe, Jiangsu)  
**Died:** February 16, 1997  
**Honors:** Wolf Prize in Physics (1978, inaugural), National Medal of Science (1975)

**Major Contributions:**
- **Wu Experiment (1956):** Definitive experimental proof of parity violation in weak interactions, enabling the Nobel Prize for Lee and Yang
- Beta decay research authority
- Manhattan Project contribution (gaseous diffusion, identification of Xe-135 poisoning)
- First experimental confirmation of quantum entanglement (1949)

**Nicknames:** "First Lady of Physics," "Chinese Marie Curie," "Queen of Nuclear Research"

**Legacy:** Despite being denied the Nobel Prize (controversially), she received the first Wolf Prize in Physics and remains one of the most influential experimental physicists of the 20th century.

#### Pan Jianwei (潘建伟)

**Born:** March 11, 1970 (Dongyang, Zhejiang)  
**Current Position:** Professor and Executive Vice President, USTC

**Major Contributions:**
- Pioneer of quantum communication and quantum information in China
- Led development of world's first quantum satellite (Micius, 2016)
- Achieved satellite-to-ground quantum entanglement distribution over 1,200 km
- Developed "Zuchongzhi" quantum computers

**Honors:**
- Named "Father of Quantum" by Nature (2017)
- Time 100 Most Influential People (2018)
- Member, Chinese Academy of Sciences (youngest academician elected in 2011)

#### Zhou Guangzhao (周光召)

**Born:** 1929

**Contributions:**
- Theoretical particle physics

- Former President of Chinese Academy of Sciences
- Honorary President of USTC

#### Qian Sanqiang (钱三强)

**Contributions:**

- Founding leader of CAS Institute of Atomic Energy

#### Deng Jiaxian (邓稼先)

**Born:** 1924  
**Died:** 1986

**Contributions:**

#### Zhao Zhongyao (赵忠尧)

## 6. Physics Research Areas of Strength

### 6.1 Quantum Information Science

China is a global leader in quantum information, with particular strengths in:
- **Quantum communication:** World's first quantum satellite (Micius), Beijing-Shanghai quantum backbone network
- **Quantum computing:** "Zuchongzhi" superconducting quantum processors
- **Quantum entanglement:** Long-distance entanglement distribution records

**Leading Institutions:** USTC, Tsinghua, Peking University, IOP-CAS

### 6.2 High-Temperature Superconductivity

China has a strong tradition in superconductivity research:
- Iron-based superconductors
- Cuprate superconductors
- Topological superconductivity

**Leading Institutions:** IOP-CAS, Tsinghua, USTC

### 6.3 Particle Physics

Active participation in international experiments and domestic facilities:
- Neutrino physics (Daya Bay, JUNO)
- Tau-charm physics (BEPC II, BES III)
- Dark matter detection (DAMPE, PandaX, CDEX)
- Cosmic ray physics (LHAASO)

**Leading Institutions:** IHEP, USTC, Tsinghua, SJTU

### 6.4 Nuclear Fusion

China operates one of the world's leading fusion research programs:
- EAST tokamak (world records for long-pulse operation)
- HL-2M tokamak (Chengdu)
- Participation in ITER
- China Fusion Engineering Test Reactor (CFETR, planned)

**Leading Institution:** Institute of Plasma Physics, Hefei

### 6.5 Condensed Matter Physics

Broad strength across multiple subfields:
- Strongly correlated electron systems
- Topological materials and quantum Hall effects
- Two-dimensional materials (graphene, etc.)
- Magnetism and spintronics
- Surface and interface physics

**Leading Institutions:** IOP-CAS, Tsinghua, PKU, NJU, Fudan

### 6.6 Astrophysics and Cosmology

Rapidly growing field with major facilities:
- Radio astronomy (FAST)
- X-ray astronomy (HXMT)
- Gamma-ray astronomy (LHAASO, DAMPE)
- Cosmic ray physics
- Gravitational wave detection (planned)

**Leading Institutions:** National Astronomical Observatories, IHEP, USTC, Tsinghua

### 6.7 Accelerator Physics and Technology

Development and operation of major accelerator facilities:
- Synchrotrons (SSRF, BEPC II)
- Spallation neutron sources (CSNS)
- Free-electron lasers (under development)

**Leading Institutions:** IHEP, SINAP

## 7. International Collaborations

### 7.1 CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)

China has a long-standing cooperation agreement with CERN:
- **Formal Cooperation:** Since 1991 (agreement renewed 1997, 2004)
- **Participation:** Chinese scientists work on LHC experiments (CMS, ATLAS)
- **Contributions:** Chinese institutions contribute to detector construction and data analysis
- **Personnel:** Hundreds of Chinese researchers involved

**Chinese Institutions at CERN:**
- Institute of High Energy Physics (IHEP)
- Tsinghua University
- Peking University
- University of Science and Technology of China
- Nanjing University
- And many others

### 7.2 ITER (International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor)

China is one of the seven official member parties of ITER:
- **Joined:** 2003
- **Contribution:** 9.1% of construction costs (approximately €1.8 billion in-kind)
- **Domestic Agency:** China International Nuclear Fusion Energy Program (CNDA)

**Chinese Contributions to ITER:**
- Correction coils
- Magnet supports
- First wall components
- Shield blanket modules
- Testing on EAST tokamak to support ITER operations

**Research Links:**
- EAST serves as testbed for ITER technologies
- CFETR planned as bridge between ITER and commercial fusion

## 10. Conclusion

China has established itself as a major force in international physics research, with:

- **World-class institutions** across the full spectrum of physics disciplines
- **Cutting-edge facilities** including the world's largest radio telescope and advanced fusion reactors
- **Strong human capital** with an ever-growing pool of talented physicists
- **Strategic international partnerships** that enable participation in global megascience projects
- **Increasing influence** in setting the global research agenda

The country's physics community continues to grow in both scale and impact, with particular leadership in quantum information, high-temperature superconductivity, and fusion research. As China continues to invest heavily in basic research and large scientific facilities, its role in global physics is expected to expand further in the coming decades.
