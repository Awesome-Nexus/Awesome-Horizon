# Physics in Algeria: A Comprehensive Research Report

## Executive Summary

Algeria has a well-developed physics research and education ecosystem, anchored by major universities such as USTHB Algiers, University of Oran, and University of Constantine, alongside specialized research centers under COMENA (Algerian Atomic Energy Commission) and CDTA. The country has produced notable physicists including Nobel Laureate Claude Cohen-Tannoudji and maintains active international collaborations with CERN and other major institutions.

## 6. Physics Education

### 6.1 Educational System Structure

Algeria follows the LMD (Licence-Master-Doctorate) system:

| Degree | Duration | Description |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Licence** | 3 years (Bac+3) | Bachelor's degree - academic or professional track |
| **Master** | 2 years (Bac+5) | Master's degree - requires internal selection |
| **Doctorat** | 3 years (Bac+8) | PhD - accessible through national exam |

### 6.2 Languages of Instruction

| Level | Language |
|-------|----------|
| Undergraduate | French (primary), Arabic |
| Graduate Programs | French, English (increasingly) |
| Some Specialized Masters | English |

### 6.3 International Student Information

**Requirements for International Students:**
- High school diploma or equivalent (recognized in Algeria)
- Degree equivalence procedure for foreign diplomas
- Medical examination upon arrival
- French or English language proficiency

**Required Documents:**
- Translated and certified copies of diplomas
- Academic transcripts
- Certificate of nationality / Passport copy
- Cover letter
- Letter(s) of recommendation
- Medical certificate
- Passport photos

### 6.4 Erasmus+ Programs

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Opportunities** | Student mobility, staff exchanges, capacity building |
| **Scholarships** | ICM Mobilities, Erasmus Mundus |
| **2025 Results** | ~300 exchange projects for Algerian institutions |
| **Budget** | 5.2 billion euros for 2026 call |
