# Physics Institutions in Poland

## Overview

Poland is a major European physics nation with a rich scientific heritage dating back to the 19th century. As a member of CERN since 1991 (the first country from the former Eastern Bloc to join), Poland has established itself as a significant contributor to international particle physics research. The country hosts world-class research facilities, including the SOLARIS synchrotron and the MARIA nuclear research reactor, and maintains a strong network of university physics departments and national research institutes.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Polish Physical Society (Polskie Towarzystwo Fizyczne, PTF)

- **Founded:** 1920
- **Headquarters:** Warsaw
- **Website:** [www.ptf.net.pl](https://www.ptf.net.pl)
- **Mission:** To popularize physics and related sciences, raise the general level of physics knowledge in society, and support the development of physics in Poland
- **Activities:** 
  - Organizing scientific conferences and meetings
  - Publishing scientific journals including *Acta Physica Polonica*
  - Awarding prizes for outstanding achievements in physics
  - Representing Polish physicists nationally and internationally
  - Promoting physics education at all levels

The Polish Physical Society serves as the primary professional organization for physicists in Poland, fostering connections between physicists working in education, research, and industry. It plays a crucial role in science popularization and policy advocacy.

## 3. National Research Centers

### National Centre for Nuclear Research (Narodowe Centrum Badań Jądrowych, NCBJ)

- **Location:** Świerk-Otwock (near Warsaw) and Warsaw
- **Website:** [www.ncbj.gov.pl](https://www.ncbj.gov.pl)
- **Founded:** 2011 (merger of former Institute of Atomic Energy POLATOM and Andrzej Sołtan Institute for Nuclear Studies)
- **Employees:** Over 1,100 (including ~70 Professors, over 200 PhDs)
- **Unique Status:** The only Polish research institution operating a nuclear reactor

**Key Facilities:**
- **MARIA Research Reactor:** 
  - The only research nuclear reactor in Poland
  - High-flux pool-type reactor with 30 MW thermal power
  - Produces medical radioisotopes (covers ~10% of global supply of molybdenum-99)
  - Used for nuclear fuel research and material testing
  - Celebrated 50 years of operation in 2024
  
**Research Areas:**
- Elementary particle physics
- Nuclear physics and reactor physics
- Astrophysics and cosmology
- Hot plasma physics
- Materials engineering and radiation resistance
- Radiopharmaceuticals production (POLATOM)
- Particle accelerator development

**Major Projects:**
- **PolFEL:** Polish Free Electron Laser project
- **CERAD:** Centre for Design and Synthesis of Molecularly Targeted Radiopharmaceuticals with cyclotron
- **POLAR:** Gamma-ray burst polarization detector (in collaboration with China and Switzerland)
- **NOMATEN:** Centre of Excellence for heat-, corrosion-, and radiation-resistant materials

**International Collaborations:**
- CERN (Geneva)
- Hyper-Kamiokande experiment (Japan)
- European XFEL (Hamburg)
- Extensive cooperation with Germany, France, USA, and Switzerland

### SOLARIS National Synchrotron Radiation Centre

- **Location:** Kraków (on Jagiellonian University Campus)
- **Website:** [synchrotron.uj.edu.pl](https://synchrotron.uj.edu.pl)
- **Status:** National research center under the auspices of Jagiellonian University
- **Unique Feature:** The only synchrotron facility in Central and Eastern Europe

**Technical Specifications:**
- **Storage Ring:** 1.5 GeV energy, 96 m circumference
- **Emittance:** 6 nm rad (low-emittance design)
- **Maximum Current:** 500 mA
- **Beamlines:** 7 operational (as of 2024), with plans for dozens ultimately

**Active Beamlines:**
- **PIRX:** X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS), X-ray magnetic dichroism
- **URANOS:** Angle-resolved photoelectron spectroscopy (ARPES)
- **PHELIX:** Soft X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy and XAS
- **DEMETER:** Scanning transmission X-ray microscopy (STXM) and photoemission electron microscopy (PEEM)
- **ASTRA:** X-ray absorption spectroscopy at tender energies
- **CIRI:** Infrared absorption microscopy with imaging
- **POLYX:** X-ray microimaging and microspectroscopy (4-15 keV)

**Additional Facilities:**
- National Center for Electron Cryomicroscopy (Cryo-EM)
- **Titan Krios G3i** and **Glacios** cryo-electron microscopes

**Research Applications:**
- Biology and structural biology
- Chemistry and catalysis
- Physics and materials science
- Nanotechnology
- Medicine and pharmacology
- Geology and crystallography

**Access:** Free for researchers worldwide; beamtime allocated through peer-reviewed proposals

## 5. Notable Physicists and Nobel Laureates

### Marie Skłodowska-Curie (1867–1934)

- **Birthplace:** Warsaw, Poland
- **Nobel Prizes:**
  - **1903 Nobel Prize in Physics** (shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel) for research on radioactivity
  - **1911 Nobel Prize in Chemistry** for the discovery of radium and polonium
- **Legacy:**
  - First woman to win a Nobel Prize
  - First person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields
  - Discovered polonium (named after her native Poland) and radium
  - Coined the term "radioactivity"
  - Founded the Curie Institutes in Paris and Warsaw
  - Pioneer in mobile X-ray units during World War I
- **Honors in Poland:**
  - Maria Skłodowska-Curie National Research Institute of Oncology
  - Maria Curie-Skłodowska University in Lublin
  - The MARIA research reactor named in her honor
  - Multiple monuments and memorials

### Marian Smoluchowski (1872–1917)

- **Significance:** Pioneer of statistical physics and Brownian motion theory
- **Legacy:** Explained Brownian motion independently of Einstein; Smoluchowski equation
- **Honors:** Marian Smoluchowski Institute of Physics at Jagiellonian University named after him

### Leopold Infeld (1898–1968)

- **Contributions:** Co-author with Einstein of the paper on gravitational radiation
- **Role:** First chairman of the Scientific Council of the Institute of Physics PAN
- **Legacy:** Major figure in developing Polish theoretical physics

### Marian Danysz (1909–1983) and Jerzy Pniewski (1913–1989)

- **Contributions:** Pioneers of Polish participation in CERN
- **Discovery:** Co-discoverers of the hypernucleus (1952)
- **Legacy:** Established the first connections between Polish physics and CERN in the 1960s

### Andrzej Trautman (born 1933)

- **Field:** General relativity and gravitation theory
- **Contributions:** Important work on spinors in curved spacetime
- **Recognition:** Member of the Polish Academy of Sciences

### Aleksander Wolszczan (born 1946)

- **Discovery:** Co-discoverer of the first extrasolar planets (pulsar planets) in 1992
- **Recognition:** Member of the Polish Academy of Sciences

### Tomasz Dietl

- **Field:** Semiconductor physics and spintronics
- **Recognition:** Pioneer in diluted magnetic semiconductors; awarded the Marian Smoluchowski and Emil Warburg Prize

### Other Notable Polish Physicists:

- **Leonard Sosnowski (1905–1984):** Pioneer of semiconductor physics in Poland
- **Andrzej Kajetan Wróblewski:** High-energy physics
- **Marian Mięsowicz:** Kraków physics school, CERN pioneer
- **Stanisław Ulam (1909–1984):** Co-inventor of the Monte Carlo method; worked on Manhattan Project (later career in USA)

## 7. International Collaborations

### CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)

- **Membership:** Full member since 1991 (16th member state, first from former Eastern Bloc)
- **Pre-membership:** Observer status since 1963; collaboration since 1959
- **Participation in LHC Experiments:**
  - **ATLAS:** Silicon detectors, trigger systems, software development, physics analysis
  - **CMS:** Muon detection systems, Higgs studies, exotic particle searches
  - **ALICE:** Time projection chamber, heavy-ion physics
  - **LHCb:** Vertex detectors, RICH detectors, CP violation studies
- **Other CERN Experiments:** NA61/SHINE, COMPASS, ISOLDE, ICARUS
- **Future Projects:** Involvement in FCC (Future Circular Collider) and CLIC studies

### European Synchrotron Radiation Facility (ESRF)

- **Membership:** Joined in 2004
- **Connection:** SOLARIS synchrotron design based on MAX IV (Lund, Sweden) collaboration

### European XFEL (X-ray Free Electron Laser)

- **Partnership:** Polish scientists contributed to XFEL development
- **Project:** PolFEL (Polish Free Electron Laser) under development at NCBJ

### European Spallation Source (ESS)

- **Status:** Founding member

### Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR)

- **Status:** Partner

### Institut Laue-Langevin (ILL)

- **Membership:** Joined in 2006

### European Space Agency (ESA)

- **Membership:** Joined in 2012
- **Polish Contributions:** Space research instrumentation, satellite technology

### Other International Collaborations:

- **Hyper-Kamiokande (Japan):** NCBJ participation in detector construction and research
- **JUNO (China):** Participation in neutrino physics
- **Bilateral Agreements:** Extensive collaborations with Germany, France, USA, Switzerland, Japan, and China
