# Physics Research Centers in Cameroon

## Overview

Cameroon, often referred to as "Africa in miniature," has a developing but vibrant physics research ecosystem centered around its public universities and international collaborations. The country's physics research landscape is characterized by strong theoretical physics traditions, particularly in nonlinear dynamics and condensed matter physics, as well as growing experimental capabilities in nuclear physics, materials science, and geophysics.

## 1. University of Yaoundé I

### Faculty of Science (Faculté des Sciences)

**Location:** Yaoundé, Cameroon  
**Website:** https://facsciences.uy1.cm/en/  
**Founded:** 1962 (restructured to Yaoundé I in 1993)

#### Department of Physics

The Department of Physics at the University of Yaoundé I is the premier physics research institution in Cameroon and one of the most productive in Central Africa.

**Research Laboratories:**

| Laboratory | Research Focus | Head/Contact |
|------------|----------------|--------------|
| **Laboratory of Mechanics** | Applied mechanics, vibrations, nonlinear dynamics | Jacques Hona |
| **Laboratory of Materials Science** | Materials characterization, semiconductors, nanomaterials | Multiple PIs |
| **Laboratory of Nuclear Physics** | Nuclear and radiation physics | Research group |
| **LAMSEBP** (Laboratory of Modelling and Simulation in Engineering, Biomimetics and Prototype) | Computational physics, simulation, prototyping | Prof. Paul Woafo |
| **LEMAP** (Laboratory for Environmental Modelling and Atmospheric Physics) | Atmospheric physics, climate modeling | Appolinaire Vondou |
| **Laboratory of Mechanics, Materials and Structures** | Structural mechanics, material behavior | Marcial Baduidana |

**Programs Offered:**
- **Bachelor of Science (BSc)** in Physics (3 years)
- **Master of Science (MSc)** in Physics (2 years)
- **Doctorat de troisième cycle** (PhD equivalent) in various physics specializations

**Key Research Areas:**
- Nonlinear dynamics and chaos theory
- Condensed matter physics
- Materials science and nanotechnology
- Mechanics and vibration
- Nuclear physics and radiation protection
- Environmental and atmospheric physics
- Computational physics and modeling

### Notable Researchers

## 6. Cameroon Academy of Sciences (CAS)

**Full Name:** Académie des Sciences du Cameroun  
**Founded:** 1990 (inaugurated 1993)  
**Location:** Yaoundé, Cameroon  
**Website:** www.cas-cameroon.org  
**Type:** Non-governmental organization

### Overview

The Cameroon Academy of Sciences is the highest scientific institution in Cameroon, providing independent advice to the government and promoting scientific research excellence.

### History

- **1972:** First proposed by the Council for Higher Education and Scientific Research
- **1982:** Draft decree for creation
- **1990:** Founded during a symposium on agriculture and agricultural research in Sub-Saharan Africa in Douala
- **February 10, 1993:** Officially inaugurated by Prime Minister Simon Achidi Achu
- **1992:** Received legal status as NGO

### Leadership Structure

| Position | Current Office Holder |
|----------|----------------------|
| President | Beban Sammy Chumbow |
| 1st Vice-President | Jeanne Yonkeu Ngogang |
| 2nd Vice-President | Kofane Timoleon Crepin |
| Secretary General | Mbah David Akuro |

### Membership

- **Total Members:** 87 Fellows
- **Colleges:**
  - Biological Sciences
  - Social Sciences  
  - Mathematical and Physical Sciences
- **Criteria:** Non-discriminatory on ethnic or gender basis

### Key Activities

1. **Science Advisory:** Providing evidence-based advice to government on science and technology policy
2. **Conferences and Seminars:** Organizing scientific events
3. **Publications:** Publishing the *Journal of the Cameroon Academy of Sciences*
4. **Awards:** Prizes to young scholars and researchers
5. **Scholarships:** Grants to encourage students in sciences
6. **Contracted Research:** Projects with national and international partners

### Affiliations

- Ministry of Scientific and Technical Research
- Ministry of Higher Education
- International Science Council (ISC)
- InterAcademy Partnership (IAP)

### Cameroon Academy of Young Scientists (CAYS)

A branch of CAS focused on nurturing the next generation of Cameroonian scientists.

## 7. Research Institutes and Centers

**Support:**

- Conduct exceptional research

- Bridge academia, industry, and government

**Research Areas:**
- Mathematical Analysis
- Nonlinear Analysis
- Partial Differential Equations
- Climate Sciences
- Shape Optimization
- Control Theory
- Deep Learning and Big Data

**Applications:**
- Environmental pollution modeling
- Coastal erosion
- Urban network analysis
- Industrial problem-solving

**Statistics:**
- 200+ visitors hosted since 2017
- Activities: Summer schools, workshops, seminars, international conferences
- Research visits: Short-term (1-2 weeks) and long-term (2-4 weeks)

## 12. Challenges and Opportunities

### Challenges

1. **Limited Funding:** Research budgets remain constrained compared to international standards
2. **Equipment Access:** Limited access to advanced experimental equipment
3. **Brain Drain:** Loss of talented researchers to international institutions
4. **Infrastructure:** Inadequate research infrastructure in some institutions
5. **Internet Connectivity:** Limited bandwidth affecting computational research

### Opportunities

1. **International Collaborations:** Growing partnerships with CERN, ICTP, German institutions
2. **Diaspora Engagement:** Active Cameroonian scientific diaspora maintaining connections
3. **AIMS Network:** AIMS-Cameroon providing advanced training and research opportunities
4. **Digital Resources:** Increasing access to online journals and computational resources
5. **Government Support:** Recognition of science and technology importance for development
