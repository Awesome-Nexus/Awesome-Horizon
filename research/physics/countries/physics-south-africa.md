# Physics in South Africa

## Overview

South Africa is Africa's leading nation in physics research and education, distinguished by its hosting of the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) radio telescope and significant contributions across multiple physics disciplines. The country's physics ecosystem encompasses world-class research facilities, strong university departments, international collaborations, and Nobel laureates who began their scientific journeys on South African soil.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies

### South African Institute of Physics (SAIP)

**Founded:** July 1955  
**Status:** Non-profit organization registered under the Non-profit Organisations Act, No. 71 of 1997  
**Headquarters:** Pretoria

The **South African Institute of Physics (SAIP)** serves as the national professional body representing the physics community in South Africa. It acts as "the Voice of Physics in South Africa" with membership spanning:

- All major research and academic institutions across South Africa
- Schools and secondary educators
- Private sector physicists
- International affiliates

**Key Functions:**
- Professional development and networking for physicists
- Advocacy for physics research and education
- Organization of annual conferences and workshops
- Publication of Physics Comment and other scientific communications
- Recognition of excellence through fellowship programs and awards
- Physics for Development initiatives to nurture the next generation

The SAIP maintains international partnerships, including collaborations with the American Physical Society (APS), and elects fellows who have made significant contributions to physics research and education.

## 2. Major University Physics Departments

### University of the Witwatersrand (Wits) - School of Physics

**Location:** Johannesburg, Gauteng  
**Distinction:** The largest single Physics campus in South Africa

The **Wits School of Physics** is the premier physics institution in the country by scale, offering comprehensive undergraduate and postgraduate programs. The School has significant experimental infrastructure and engages in internationally competitive research across diverse fields.

**Research Areas:**
- **Astronomy and Astrophysics:** Cosmology, galaxy evolution, high-energy astrophysics, exploding stars
- **Collider Physics:** Participation in CERN's ATLAS experiment
- **Condensed Matter Experiment and Material Science:** Material Physics Research Institute (MPRI)
- **Nuclear and Radiation Physics:** Fundamental and applied nuclear research
- **Photonics and Quantum Optics:** Laser physics and quantum technologies
- **Physics Education:** Pedagogical research and curriculum development
- **Space Propulsion and Plasma Physics:** Advanced propulsion systems
- **Theoretical Physics:** String theory and fundamental physics

**Facilities:**
- Material Physics Research Institute (MPRI)
- Advanced laser facilities
- Nuclear physics laboratories

---

### University of Cape Town (UCT) - Department of Physics

**Location:** Cape Town, Western Cape  
**Building:** R.W. James Building, Upper Campus

UCT's Department of Physics is housed in the **R.W. James Building**, named after the legendary X-ray crystallographer who mentored two Nobel laureates. The department offers a complete physics curriculum from undergraduate through doctoral studies.

**Research Themes:**
- **High Energy Physics:** CERN ATLAS collaboration, search for Higgs boson and exclusive interactions
- **Nuclear Physics:** Radioactive ion beam physics, nuclear structure
- **Nanotechnology and Solid State Physics:** Materials characterization, nanoscale phenomena
- **Applied Physics:** Practical applications of physical principles
- **Tertiary Physics Education:** Physics pedagogy research

**Notable History:**
The department has a remarkable legacy of producing Nobel laureates, with Prof. R.W. James mentoring both **Aaron Klug** (Chemistry, 1982) and **Allan Cormack** (Physiology/Medicine, 1979) during their time at UCT.

### Stellenbosch University - Physics

**Location:** Stellenbosch, Western Cape

Stellenbosch University hosts significant physics research and is home to the **National Institute for Theoretical and Computational Sciences (NITheCS)** headquarters, officially launched in 2023.

**Research Focus:**
- Theoretical and computational physics
- Laser physics (through the Laser Research Institute)
- Materials science
- Physics education

The university has strong international linkages and contributes significantly to South Africa's research output in theoretical physics.

---

### University of the Western Cape (UWC) - Department of Physics and Astronomy

**Location:** Bellville, Cape Town, Western Cape

UWC's Department of Physics and Astronomy is a major contributor to research needs both regionally and nationally, with particular strength in applied physics and astronomy.

**Key Research Areas:**

**Materials Science/Solid State Physics:**
- Photovoltaics (solar cell research) - the only such group in the Western Cape region
- High-temperature superconductors - one of only two groups in South Africa
- Thin film research for semiconductors and coatings
- Metal-semiconductor interactions

**Nuclear Physics:**
- Nuclear structure and fundamental symmetries
- Radiation detection systems
- Applied nuclear radiation and environmental geophysics
- The only research group on radon and natural radioactivity in the Western Cape

**Astrophysics:**
- Hosts the **Centre for Radio Cosmology**
- Major contributor to SARAO/MeerKAT/SKA research and training
- 6 academic staff, 14 postdocs, 24 research students (2021)
- Research on stellar evolution, cosmology, and radio astronomy

**Electron Microscope Unit (EMU):**
- South Africa's first Field Emission TEM (Tecnai F20)
- National facility available to all researchers
- Advanced nanoscience characterization capabilities

### Rhodes University - Department of Physics and Electronics

Rhodes University has a thriving physics department with diverse research programs and strong connections to the SKA project.

## 3. National Research Centers

### iThemba Laboratory for Accelerator-Based Sciences (iThemba LABS)

**Location:** Faure (near Cape Town), Western Cape  
**Status:** National Research Facility of the National Research Foundation

**iThemba LABS** is Africa's leading accelerator-based research facility, providing particle beams for research and applications in:

**Research Programs:**
- **Nuclear Physics:** Radioactive ion beams, nuclear structure and reactions
- **Radiation Biophysics:** Biological effects of radiation
- **Materials Research:** Ion beam analysis and modification
- **Accelerator Mass Spectrometry (AMS):** Dating and tracing applications
- **Radiopharmaceuticals:** Medical isotope production for cancer diagnosis and therapy

**SA-CERN Programme:**
iThemba LABS coordinates South Africa's participation in CERN research, enabling South African scientists to participate in groundbreaking experiments in particle physics and related fields.

**Location:** Pretoria, Gauteng  
**Established:** 1945 (by Act of Parliament)

The **CSIR** is South Africa's leading scientific and technology research organization, undertaking multidisciplinary R&D across physics-related fields:

**Physics-Related Research:**
- Advanced materials and manufacturing
- Photonics and laser applications
- Space science and technology
- Defence and security technologies
- Environmental monitoring
- Nanotechnology

**Key Facilities:**
- National Laser Centre
- Advanced manufacturing facilities
- Materials characterization laboratories

### South African Nuclear Energy Corporation (Necsa)

**Location:** Pelindaba, North West Province  
**Established:** 1999 (Nuclear Energy Act)

**Necsa** is South Africa's state-owned nuclear technology organization responsible for:

**Core Activities:**
- Nuclear research and development
- Operation of the SAFARI-1 research reactor
- Production of medical radioisotopes (significant global supplier)
- Nuclear waste management
- Nuclear non-proliferation
- Materials research using neutron techniques

**Research Facilities:**
- **SAFARI-1 Research Reactor:** One of the world's most utilized research reactors
- **Materials Probe for Internal Strain Investigations (MPISI):** Neutron diffraction instrument for materials science

## 4. Major Research Facilities

### South African Radio Astronomy Observatory (SARAO)

**Status:** National Facility of the National Research Foundation  
**Headquarters:** Cape Town  
**Responsibility:** Managing all radio astronomy initiatives in South Africa

**SARAO** oversees South Africa's world-leading radio astronomy facilities:

#### MeerKAT Radio Telescope

**Location:** Karoo, Northern Cape (Meerkat National Park)  
**Description:** 64-dish radio telescope array

**Specifications:**
- 64 x 13.5-meter diameter dishes
- Frequency range: 580 MHz - 14 GHz
- Configuration: Spread over 8 km baseline
- World's most sensitive radio telescope of its kind

**Science Highlights:**
- Detection of radio filaments at the Galactic Centre
- Discovery of new pulsar populations
- Deep surveys of the southern sky
- Predecessor to SKA-Mid

#### Square Kilometre Array (SKA)

**Status:** Under construction (since December 2022)  
**South African Component:** SKA-Mid (mid-frequency array)

**SKA-Mid Specifications:**
- Location: Karoo site, combined with MeerKAT
- Antennas: 197 dishes (including existing 64 MeerKAT dishes)
- Total collecting area: ~1 square kilometer
- Construction completion: ~2028
- Partners: 16 member countries

**Scientific Goals:**
- Testing Einstein's theory of general relativity with pulsars
- Mapping the cosmic web and large-scale structure
- Detecting the Epoch of Reionization
- Search for extraterrestrial intelligence
- Cosmic magnetism studies

**Construction Milestones:**
- December 2022: Construction commencement ceremony
- July 2024: First SKA-Mid dish synchronized with MeerKAT
- July 2025: First receiver installed on SKA-Mid dish

#### Hartebeesthoek Radio Astronomy Observatory (HartRAO)

**Location:** Hartebeesthoek, Gauteng (50 km west of Johannesburg)

**History:**
- Originally built as **Deep Space Station 51 (DSS-51)** by NASA in 1961
- Supported Ranger, Surveyor, and Lunar Orbiter missions
- Received first Mars surface images from Mariner 4
- Converted to radio astronomy in 1975

**Current Operations:**
- 26-meter radio telescope (260-ton)
- Continuum radiometry, spectroscopy, pulsar timing
- Very Long Baseline Interferometry (VLBI)
- Space Geodesy program (VLBI, GNSS, SLR, DORIS)
- Associate member of European VLBI Network

**Significance:**
- Only fiducial geodetic site in Africa
- Supports international space missions on contract basis
- Training facility for university students

## 5. Notable Physicists

### William Lawrence Bragg (1890-1971)

**Birthplace:** Adelaide, South Australia (parents from England)  
**South African Connection:** Born in Adelaide when his father was Professor at the University of Adelaide; the Bragg family has deep South Australian connections

**Achievement:** 
- **Nobel Prize in Physics (1915)** - shared with his father, William Henry Bragg
- "For their services in the analysis of crystal structure by means of X-rays"
- Youngest-ever Nobel laureate in science (age 25)

**Legacy in South Africa:**
- The Bragg Institute (now part of ANSTO in Australia) and the Bragg Gold Medal honor his legacy
- South Australian connections through the Electoral district of Bragg

**Note:** While Bragg was not South African, his father held the Elder Chair of Mathematics and Physics at the University of Adelaide from 1885, making the family story part of the broader Commonwealth scientific heritage.

---

### Allan MacLeod Cormack (1924-1998)

**Birthplace:** Johannesburg, South Africa  
**Education:**
- B.Sc. in Physics, University of Cape Town (1944)
- M.Sc. in Physics (Crystallography), UCT (1945)
- Research student at St. John's College, Cambridge (1947-1949)

**Career:**
- Lecturer in Physics, UCT (1950-1957)
- Professor of Physics, Tufts University, USA (1957-1995)

**Achievement:**
- **Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine (1979)** - shared with Godfrey Hounsfield
- "For the development of computer assisted tomography (CT scanning)"

**The Discovery:**
Cormack's groundbreaking work on CT scanning began at UCT and Groote Schuur Hospital in 1956. He developed the mathematical theory for reconstructing images from X-ray absorption data, publishing foundational papers in 1963-1964. Though initially ignored, his work became the theoretical basis for modern medical CT scanners.

**Legacy:**
- Posthumously awarded the Order of Mapungubwe (2002)
- UCT Physics Department honors his memory
- Inspired generations of South African medical physicists

---

### Sir Aaron Klug (1926-2018)

**Birthplace:** Å½elva, Lithuania  
**South African Connection:** Brought to Durban, South Africa at age 2; raised and educated in South Africa

**Education:**
- Durban High School
- University of the Witwatersrand (pre-medical, then chemistry/physics)
- M.Sc. in Physics, University of Cape Town (1946-1948)

**Achievement:**
- **Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1982)**
- "For his development of crystallographic electron microscopy and his structural elucidation of biologically important nucleic acid-protein complexes"

**Career Path:**
- Worked with Prof. R.W. James at UCT on X-ray crystallography
- 1851 Exhibition Scholarship to Cambridge (1949)
- Birkbeck College, London with J.D. Bernal and Rosalind Franklin
- MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology, Cambridge (1962)
- President of the Royal Society (1995-2000)

**Legacy:**
- UCT Physics Department memorializes his contributions
- Demonstrated the pathway from South African education to global scientific leadership

## 6. Physics Research Areas of Strength

### Radio Astronomy and Astrophysics

South Africa is a global leader in radio astronomy, driven by:
- MeerKAT operations and SKA construction
- HartRAO geodetic and VLBI capabilities
- Strong university research groups (UWC, Rhodes, Wits, UCT)
- Training programs for African radio astronomers

**Key Research Topics:**
- Pulsar timing and astrophysics
- Cosmic magnetism
- Galaxy evolution
- Cosmology and large-scale structure
- Epoch of Reionization studies

### Nuclear Physics

South Africa maintains significant nuclear physics capabilities:
- Radioactive ion beam physics at iThemba LABS
- Fundamental symmetry tests
- Nuclear astrophysics
- Applications in medicine and materials
- Neutrino physics (nEXO experiment participation)

### High Energy Physics

Through the SA-CERN program:
- ATLAS experiment participation (Wits, UCT, UP, UJ consortium)
- Higgs boson discovery contributions
- Search for new physics beyond the Standard Model
- Detector development and computing

### Condensed Matter and Materials Science

Strong programs at multiple universities:
- High-temperature superconductors (UWC)
- Photovoltaics and solar cell development
- Nanotechnology and nanomaterials
- Thin film characterization
- Semiconductor physics

### Quantum Science

Growing strength in quantum technologies:
- Quantum Research Group at UKZN
- Quantum optics and photonics at Wits
- Quantum computing research

### Physics Education Research

South African universities contribute internationally to:
- Physics pedagogy in diverse educational contexts
- Access and equity in physics education
- Use of simulations and technology in teaching
- Conceptual understanding studies

### Space Science and Geodesy

- HartRAO space geodesy program
- VLBI for Earth rotation and reference frames
- Collaborations with SANSA
- Space weather monitoring

## 7. International Collaborations

### CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)

**SA-CERN Programme:**
- Established formal collaboration framework
- South African scientists participate in:
  - **ATLAS Experiment:** High-energy proton-proton collisions
  - **ALICE Experiment:** Heavy-ion physics
  - **ISOLDE:** Radioactive ion beam facility
- Joint appointments and student exchanges
- Technology transfer and capacity building

**Impact:**
- South African researchers contributed to Higgs boson discovery (2012)
- Breakthrough Prize in Fundamental Physics (2025) for ATLAS collaboration
- Training of next-generation particle physicists

## Summary

South Africa's physics landscape is characterized by:

1. **World-class facilities:** MeerKAT, iThemba LABS, HartRAO, and the emerging SKA
2. **Strong university base:** Comprehensive physics departments across the country
3. **International leadership:** In radio astronomy and selected areas of nuclear physics
4. **Nobel legacy:** Three Nobel laureates with South African connections
5. **Pan-African role:** Training hub for physics across the continent
6. **Diverse research:** From fundamental physics to applications in health and energy

As the SKA comes online and international collaborations deepen, South Africa is positioned to remain Africa's physics leader and an increasingly significant player in global physics research.
