# Physics in Belgium: A Comprehensive Overview

Belgium has a distinguished history in physics research, dating back to the 19th century with pioneering work in statistics, astronomy, and optics. As a founding member of CERN and home to the legendary Solvay Conferences, Belgium continues to play a significant role in international physics research, particularly in particle physics, cosmology, condensed matter physics, and nuclear research.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Belgian Physical Society (BPS - Société Physique de Belgique / Belgische Natuurkundige Vereniging)

**Founded:** 1921 (evolved from earlier scientific societies)  
**Headquarters:** Brussels  
**Website:** [belgianphysicalsociety.be](https://belgianphysicalsociety.be/)

The Belgian Physical Society serves as the national professional organization for physicists in Belgium. As a member of the European Physical Society (EPS), the BPS promotes physics research, education, and the support of physicists across the country.

**Key Activities:**
- Organization of the annual **BPS Conference**, bringing together physicists from across Belgium
- Publication of the **Belgian Journal of Physics** (official journal of the BPS)
- **BPS Young Minds** - activities for early-career physicists (undergraduate to postdoc)
- Organization of the **International Physics Competition (IPhyC)** for students
- Belgian-Dutch Nuclear Summer School programs

**Societal Structure:**
The society operates bilingually, serving both Flemish (Dutch-speaking) and Walloon (French-speaking) communities, reflecting Belgium's linguistic duality.

## 3. National Research Centers

### 3.1 SCK CEN - Belgian Nuclear Research Centre

**Location:** Mol, Belgium  
**Founded:** 1952  
**Website:** [sckcen.be](https://www.sckcen.be/en)

SCK CEN is Belgium's largest nuclear research center, with over 70 years of experience in nuclear applications research and approximately 750 employees.

**Core Research Areas:**
- Nuclear reactor safety and design
- Medical radioisotope production
- Nuclear waste management and disposal
- Material aging analysis
- Nuclear decommissioning

**Major Facilities:**

#### BR1 (Belgian Reactor 1)
- Belgium's first research reactor (1956)
- Air-cooled graphite reactor
- Currently used for irradiation, instrument calibration, and training

#### BR2 (Belgian Reactor 2)
- One of the world's most powerful research reactors
- Produces **25-65% of global demand** for molybdenum-99 (medical isotope for cancer diagnosis)
- Used for material testing under intense radiation

#### BR3 (Belgian Reactor 3)
- Europe's first pressurized water reactor
- Europe's first dismantled PWR - serves as training ground for nuclear decommissioning

#### VENUS Reactor
- Zero-power flexible experimental facility
- Testing reactor for new designs and calculations
- Currently being converted for Accelerator Driven Systems research

#### HADES Underground Lab
- Located **225 meters underground**
- Research facility for radioactive waste disposal in deep clay
- Operated by EURIDICE (SCK CEN and ONDRAF/NIRAS partnership)

#### MYRRHA (Under Development, 2035-2040)
- **Multi-purpose hYbrid Research Reactor for High-tech Applications**
- World's first research reactor driven by a particle accelerator
- Lead-bismuth cooled system
- Applications include:
  - Nuclear waste transmutation
  - Medical radioisotope production
  - Research on new reactor concepts
  - Fundamental physics research

## 4. Major Research Facilities

### MYRRHA Facility (Under Construction)

The MYRRHA project represents one of Belgium's most ambitious research infrastructure investments:

- **Accelerator-driven system** combining a particle accelerator with a subcritical reactor
- **Lead-bismuth eutectic coolant**
- Research applications spanning nuclear physics, materials science, and medical isotopes
- Expected operation: 2035-2040
- Currently in Phase 1 (MINERVA) implementing the accelerator component

### BR2 Research Reactor

One of the world's most powerful research reactors:
- High-flux materials testing
- Medical isotope production (global significance)
- Fuel and materials research for nuclear industry

### IceCube Neutrino Observatory

Belgian groups at VUB and other institutions participate in this South Pole neutrino telescope, studying high-energy neutrinos from cosmic sources.

### Gravitational Wave Research Infrastructure

Belgium is a partner in developing the **Einstein Telescope**, the next-generation gravitational wave observatory:
- Belgian groups contribute to detector technology
- Research into essential technologies for future gravitational wave detection

## 5. Notable Physicists and Nobel Laureates

### 5.1 Nobel Laureates in Physics

#### François Englert (b. 1932)

**Nobel Prize in Physics 2013** (shared with Peter Higgs)

- **Institution:** Université libre de Bruxelles (ULB)
- **Discovery:** Brout-Englert-Higgs mechanism for spontaneous symmetry breaking
- **Contribution:** Explained how gauge bosons acquire mass through interaction with the Higgs field
- **Recognition:**
  - Wolf Prize in Physics (2004, with Brout and Higgs)
  - J.J. Sakurai Prize (2010)
  - Francqui Prize (1982)
  - Ennobled as Baron by King Albert II (2013)

**Note:** His collaborator Robert Brout (1928-2011) would have shared the Nobel Prize had he lived; Brout is recognized as co-discoverer of the mechanism.

---

### 5.2 Pioneering Physicists

#### Georges Lemaître (1894-1966)

**"Father of the Big Bang Theory"**

- **Institution:** Catholic University of Louvain (UCLouvain predecessor)
- **Major Contributions:**
  - First formulation of the **Big Bang theory** ("hypothesis of the primeval atom," 1931)
  - Derivation of what is now known as the **Hubble-Lemaître law** (1927, two years before Hubble)
  - Connection between general relativity and the expanding universe
  - Pioneering work in **physical cosmology**

**Recognition:**
- Eddington Medal (1953, inaugural recipient)
- Francqui Prize (1934)
- President of Pontifical Academy of Sciences (1960-1966)
- **Hubble-Lemaître Law** - IAU officially renamed Hubble's law in 2018 to recognize Lemaître's priority

**Legacy:** The fifth European ATV spacecraft was named **Georges Lemaître ATV** in his honor.

---

#### Robert Brout (1928-2011)

- **Institution:** Université libre de Bruxelles (ULB)
- **Co-discoverer:** Brout-Englert-Higgs mechanism (1964, with Englert)
- **Origin:** American-born, became Belgian citizen
- **Also known for:** Early work on cosmic inflation (1978, with Englert and Gunzig)
- **Awards:** Wolf Prize (2004), Sakurai Prize (2010)

---

#### Conny Aerts (b. 1966)

- **Institution:** KU Leuven
- **Field:** Asteroseismology and stellar astrophysics
- **Recognition:**
  - Leading expert in asteroseismology
  - Major contributions to space missions (CoRoT, Kepler, TESS, PLATO)
- Pioneered the use of stellar oscillations to study the internal structure of stars

---

### 5.3 Historical Figures

#### Adolphe Quetelet (1796-1874)

- **Founder:** Brussels Observatory (1832)
- **Contributions:**
  - Pioneer in social physics and statistics
- First to apply the normal distribution beyond error analysis
  - Developed the concept of the "average man" (l'homme moyen)
- **Legacy:** Founded the quantitative approach to social sciences; influential in astronomy and meteorology

---

#### Théophile De Donder (1872-1957)

- **Institution:** ULB
- **Contributions:**
  - Founder of **irreversible thermodynamics**
  - Work on **thermodynamics of chemical reactions**
  - Developed the concept of **affinity** in chemical thermodynamics

---

#### Jules Bordet (1870-1961)

- **Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 1919**
- Discovery of complement system in immunology
- Not strictly a physicist, but his work exemplifies Belgian scientific excellence

## 7. International Collaborations

### 7.1 CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)

**Belgium is a founding member of CERN** (established 1954).

**Belgian Contributions:**
- Active participation in **ATLAS** and **CMS** experiments at the LHC
- Belgian physicists contributed to the **Higgs boson discovery** (2012)
- Theoretical physics support for CERN programs
- Engineering and detector construction contributions

**Belgian Institutions at CERN:**
- ULB - ATLAS experiment participation
- VUB - CMS experiment participation
- UCLouvain - Phenomenology and theory

### 7.2 The Solvay International Institutes

**Founded:** 1912-1913 by Ernest Solvay  
**Location:** Brussels  
**Website:** [solvayinstitutes.be](https://solvayinstitutes.be/)

The Solvay Institutes are among the most prestigious scientific institutions in the world:

**History:**
- Established following the historic **1911 Solvay Conference on Physics**
- Founded by Belgian industrialist and philanthropist **Ernest Solvay** (1838-1922)
- Mission: "To enlarge and deepen the understanding of natural phenomena"

**The Solvay Conferences:**
Invitation-only conferences that shaped 20th-century physics:
- **1911:** "Radiation and the Quanta" - First quantum conference
- **1927:** "Electrons and Photons" - Famous for Bohr-Einstein debates on quantum mechanics
- **Regular cycle:** Physics (odd years), Chemistry (alternating years), Biology (new series since 2024)

**Notable Participants (historical):**
- Albert Einstein, Niels Bohr, Marie Curie, Max Planck
- Werner Heisenberg, Erwin Schrödinger, Paul Dirac
- Hendrik Lorentz (first chair), Ernest Rutherford
- 17 Nobel Laureates attended the 1927 conference alone

**UNESCO Recognition:**
The Solvay Conference archives (1910-1962) were added to UNESCO's **Memory of the World International Register** in 2023.

### 7.3 European Research Infrastructure Consortia (ERIC)

Belgium participates in major European research infrastructures:

- **ESRF** (European Synchrotron Radiation Facility) - Grenoble, France
- **ILL** (Institut Laue-Langevin) - Neutron source, France
- **ELETTRA** - Synchrotron, Italy
- **ELI** (Extreme Light Infrastructure) - Laser facilities

### 7.4 Gravitational Wave Collaborations

- **Virgo Collaboration** - European gravitational wave detector (Italy)
- **Einstein Telescope** - Next-generation underground gravitational wave observatory (proposed for Netherlands/Belgium/Italy)
- **LIGO Scientific Collaboration** - US-based gravitational wave detection

### 7.5 Space Research Collaborations

Belgium participates in major space missions through:
- **ESA (European Space Agency)** - Founding member
- **PLATO mission** - Asteroseismology and exoplanet hunting
- **CHEOPS, ARIEL** - Exoplanet characterization

### 7.6 Other Major Collaborations

- **IceCube Neutrino Observatory** (South Pole)
- **Pierre Auger Observatory** (Cosmic rays, Argentina)
- **SKA (Square Kilometre Array)** - Radio astronomy

## 9. Physics Education in Belgium

### Undergraduate Programs

Belgian universities offer comprehensive physics education at the bachelor's level, typically 3-year programs leading to a professional bachelor's degree.

### Graduate Programs

- **Master's Programs:** 1-2 year programs with specializations in:
  - Theoretical Physics
  - Particle Physics
  - Astrophysics and Cosmology
  - Condensed Matter Physics
  - Nuclear Physics
  - Biophysics

### Doctoral Training

- **PhD programs** typically 4 years
- Funded through research assistant positions (FWO/FNRS fellowships)
- Strong international orientation
- All Belgian PhD programs are fully aligned with the Bologna Process

### Notable Features

- **Bilingual education:** Many programs available in Dutch (Flanders), French (Wallonia), or English
- **International collaboration:** Joint programs with neighboring countries (Netherlands, France, Germany)
- **Research-focused:** Strong integration of education and research
