# Physics in New Zealand

## Overview

New Zealand has a distinguished history in physics, most notably as the birthplace of Ernest Rutherford, the "father of nuclear physics" and Nobel laureate who pioneered our understanding of atomic structure. Today, New Zealand maintains a vibrant physics community through its national institute, world-class university departments, and specialized research centers. The country has particular strengths in photonics, quantum technologies, materials science, and astrophysics, with research conducted through the Centres of Research Excellence (CoREs) framework.

## 8. Notable Physicists

### Nobel Laureates from New Zealand

| Year | Laureate | Field | Contribution |
|------|----------|-------|--------------|
| **1908** | **Ernest Rutherford** | Chemistry (for physics research) | Investigations into the disintegration of elements and radioactive substances; discovery of atomic nucleus; "father of nuclear physics" |

### Ernest Rutherford (1871-1937)

**Key Achievements:**
- Discovered the concept of radioactive half-life (1899)
- Coined terms "alpha" and "beta" radiation
- Discovered the atomic nucleus through gold foil experiment (1911)
- First to artificially transmute one element into another (1917)
- Postulated the existence of the proton (1920)
- Predicted the neutron (1920, later discovered by Chadwick in 1932)

**Education:**
- Nelson College, New Zealand
- Canterbury College (now University of Canterbury)
- Cavendish Laboratory, Cambridge University

**Career:**
- Macdonald Professor of Physics, McGill University (1898-1907)
- Langworthy Professor, University of Manchester (1907-1919)
- Cavendish Professor, Cambridge University (1919-1937)

**Recognition:**
- Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1908)
- President of the Royal Society (1925-1930)
- Baron Rutherford of Nelson (1931)
- Buried in Westminster Abbey

### Roy Kerr (b. 1934)

**Major Contribution:**
- Discovered the exact solution of Einstein's field equations describing rotating black holes (1963)
- The "Kerr metric" is fundamental to understanding astrophysical black holes
- Shaw Prize in Astronomy (2021)

### Dan Walls (1943-1999)

**Major Contributions:**
- Pioneer in quantum optics and cavity quantum electrodynamics
- Co-authored seminal textbook "Quantum Optics"
- The Dodd-Walls Centre is named in his honor
