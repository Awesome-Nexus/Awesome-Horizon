# Physics Institutions and Research in Malaysia

## Overview

Malaysia has a dynamic physics ecosystem that has evolved significantly since the establishment of its first physics department at the University of Malaya in 1961. The country's physics research is characterized by a unique "maker ethos"—a pragmatic approach to science that emphasizes innovation, resourcefulness, and adaptation of technology to local needs. With a diverse research landscape spanning from fundamental physics to applied technologies, Malaysia has established itself as a notable player in Southeast Asian scientific research.

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Malaysian Institute of Physics (Institut Fizik Malaysia, IFM)

**Founded**: January 29, 1973  
**Headquarters**: Department of Physics, University of Malaya, Kuala Lumpur  
**President**: Prof. Tou Teck Yong (2021–present)  
**Website**: [https://ifm.org.my/](https://ifm.org.my/)

**Overview**: The IFM is the sole representative body for physicists and those involved in physics-related work in Malaysia. It was established to address the need for a unified professional organization to represent the growing physics community in the early 1970s.

**Membership Growth**:
- Initial membership (1973): 34 members
- Current membership (2024): ~1,530 members (550 active)
- Honorary Fellows: 9
- Fellows: 54

**Key Objectives**:
- Promotion of the advancement of physics
- Promotion of activities bringing together members to cooperate in education, training, and practice of physics
- Elevation of the professional status of physicists

**Major Activities**:
- **Persidangan Fizik Kebangsaan (PERFIK)**: National physics conference held annually since 1975 (formerly National Physics Symposium/NAPS)
- International Conference on Frontiers in Physics (1997, 1998, 2005, 2009, 2013, 2017)
- Asia-Pacific Physics Conference (APPC 1992, 2019)
- Training for Asian Physics Olympiad (APhO) and International Physics Olympiad (IPhO) since 2002

**Divisions and Subgroups**:
- Plasma Physics Division
- Medical Physics Division
- Optics & Photonics Subgroup
- Theoretical & Computational Physics Subgroup
- Astronomy & Astrophysics Subgroup
- Industrial Physics Subgroup (established 2021)

**Affiliations**:
- Founding member of Association of Asia Pacific Physical Societies (AAPPS)
- Founding member of Asia Pacific Center for Theoretical Physics (APCTP)
- Associate council member of Asia Oceania Forum for Synchrotron Radiation Research (AOFSRR)
- Founding member of Confederation of Scientific and Technological Associations in Malaysia (COSTAM)

**Publications**:
- *Jurnal Fizik Malaysia* (JFM): Quarterly research journal, indexed by Clarivate Analytics ESCI
- *Berita Fizik*: Quarterly newsletter
- *Majalah Fizik*: Biannual magazine with popular articles

## 2. Major University Physics Departments

### University of Malaya (UM) - Department of Physics

**Founded**: 1961 (first physics department in Malaysia)  
**Location**: Faculty of Science, 50603 Kuala Lumpur  
**Website**: [http://www.fizik.um.edu.my/](http://www.fizik.um.edu.my/)

**Overview**: The Department of Physics at UM is Malaysia's oldest and most established physics department, with a strong tradition in both teaching and research. The IFM is headquartered here.

**Research Groups**:

| Research Group | Focus Areas |
|----------------|-------------|
| Quantum and Laser Sciences Group | Quantum optics, laser-plasma interactions, photonic structures, quantum computing, ultrafast laser physics |
| Condensed Matter Physics | Materials characterization, semiconductor physics, superconductivity |
| Applied Physics | Industrial applications, instrumentation |

**Key Facilities**:
- Femtosecond laser laboratory
- Photonics research laboratory
- Materials characterization facilities
- High-performance computing cluster

**Notable Faculty**:
- Prof. Dr. C.H. Raymond Ooi (Quantum and Laser Sciences)
- Prof. Kurunathan Ratnavelu (former IFM President, Theoretical Physics)

### Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia (UKM) - Department of Applied Physics

**Founded**: 1970  
**Location**: Faculty of Science and Technology, 43600 UKM Bangi, Selangor  
**Website**: [https://www.ukm.my/fst/](https://www.ukm.my/fst/)

**Overview**: UKM was established to address the shortage of ethnic Malay technical experts and has developed strong research programs in applied physics. It is the only public university where Malay is the primary language of instruction.

**Research Areas**:

- Superconducting materials
- Nanomaterials
- Nuclear physics applications

**Notable Faculty**:
- Prof. Dato' Dr. Roslan Abd Shukor - High-temperature superconductors, materials physics
- Prof. Dr. Azman Jalar - Semiconductor physics
- Prof. Chin-Hua Chia - Materials science

## 3. National Research Centers and Laboratories

### Malaysian Nuclear Agency (Agensi Nuklear Malaysia, ANM)

**Founded**: September 19, 1972 (as Centre for Application of Nuclear Malaysia/CRANE)  
**Status**: Public organization under Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation (MOSTI)  
**Location**: Bangi, 43000 Kajang, Selangor  
**Website**: [https://www.nuclearmalaysia.gov.my/eng/](https://www.nuclearmalaysia.gov.my/eng/)

**Historical Names**:
- 1972: Centre for Application of Nuclear Malaysia (CRANE)
- 1983: Tun Ismail Atomic Research Centre (PUSPATI)
- 1994: Malaysian Institute for Nuclear Technology Research (MINT)
- 2006: Malaysian Nuclear Agency

**Major Facilities**:

| Facility | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| TRIGA PUSPATI Reactor (RTP) | Pool-type research reactor, 1 MW, first criticality 1982; only nuclear reactor in Malaysia |
| Gamma Irradiation Plant | Industrial and research irradiation |
| Cyclotron Facility | For radioisotope production |
| Radioactive Waste Management Center | National facility for radioactive waste handling |

**Research Areas**:
- Plant mutation breeding using chronic gamma irradiation
- Advanced non-destructive testing
- Radiopharmaceutical development
- Food irradiation and preservation
- Environmental radioactivity monitoring
- Nuclear reactor technology research

---

### Malaysian Space Agency (MYSA)

**Status**: Agency under Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation (MOSTI)  
**Location**: Banting, Selangor  
**Website**: [https://www.mysa.gov.my/](https://www.mysa.gov.my/)

**Research Areas**:

**International Collaborations**:

- CTA (Cherenkov Telescope Array) membership

## 4. Notable Physics Research Areas

### Quantum Technology and Laser Physics

Malaysia has developed significant expertise in quantum optics and laser physics, particularly at the University of Malaya:

- Quantum optics and quantum information
- Ultrafast laser physics
- Laser-plasma interactions
- Nonlinear optics
- Quantum computing applications

### Condensed Matter and Materials Physics

A major strength across Malaysian universities:

- High-temperature superconductors (UKM)
- Semiconductor physics and devices
- Nanomaterials and nanotechnology
- 2D materials research
- Thin film technology

### Medical Physics

Growing field with applications in healthcare:

- Radiation therapy
- Medical imaging
- Radiopharmaceutical development (Malaysian Nuclear Agency)
- Radiation protection and dosimetry

### Nuclear Physics and Technology

Centered at the Malaysian Nuclear Agency:

- Reactor physics
- Radiation applications in agriculture and industry
- Radioisotope production
- Nuclear safety and regulation

### Astrophysics and Space Science

Coordinated by MYSA and CAAR:

- Radio astronomy
- Space weather
- Optical astronomy
- Space technology development

### Plasma Physics

Historical strength with international collaborations:

- Fusion plasma research
- Industrial plasma applications
- Laser-produced plasmas
- Atmospheric plasmas

### Geophysics

Particularly strong at USM and UPM:

- Seismology
- Applied geophysics for natural resources
- Environmental geophysics
- Climate physics

## 8. Notable Physicists from Malaysia

### Mazlan Othman (b. 1951)

**Recognition**: Malaysia's first astrophysicist and most famous physicist internationally  
**HPI (Historical Popularity Index)**: 50.87 (highest among Malaysian physicists)

**Career Highlights**:
- Pioneered Malaysia's participation in space exploration
- Helped create astrophysics curriculum at the national university
- Director General of ANGKASA (Malaysian National Space Agency)
- Director of United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs (UNOOSA), Vienna (2007–2014)
- UNESCO Ambassador for Space Science

**Legacy**: Built public awareness and understanding of astronomy and space issues in Malaysia

---

### Thong Saw Pak

**Recognition**: First President of IFM (1973–1975), founding father of Malaysian physics

**Career Highlights**:
- First Chair of Physics Department at University of Malaya (Kuala Lumpur campus)
- Spent time in China in the late 1950s as a postdoctoral researcher
- Advocated for physics as central to industrialization
- Led the formation of the Malaysian Institute of Physics
- Taught and trained physicists from developing countries (Egypt, India, Indonesia, Nigeria, Pakistan, Sierra Leone)

## Future Directions

### Strategic Priorities (2025-2030)

1. **Quantum Technology Development**: Building on existing strengths in quantum optics
2. **Space Science Expansion**: Continued development through MYSA
3. **Nuclear Research**: New reactor facilities and medical isotope production
4. **Synchrotron Access**: Enhanced collaboration with regional facilities
5. **Industrial Physics**: Strengthening university-industry linkages
6. **International Collaboration**: Expanded CERN and ITER participation

### Challenges

- Brain drain of talented physicists to other countries
- Need for increased research funding
- Building critical mass in specialized areas
- Enhancing public understanding of physics
- Balancing fundamental and applied research priorities
