# Physics in Mexico: A Comprehensive Overview

Mexico is a major physics research nation in Latin America with a rich tradition of scientific inquiry dating back to the early 20th century. The country boasts world-class research institutions, internationally recognized physicists, and significant contributions to fields ranging from astrophysics and cosmology to high-energy physics and materials science.

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Sociedad Mexicana de Física (SMF)

The **Mexican Physical Society (Sociedad Mexicana de Física)** is the premier professional organization for physicists in Mexico. Founded to promote physics research, education, and collaboration, the SMF organizes conferences, publishes journals, and represents Mexican physicists internationally.

**Key Activities:**
- Annual National Physics Meetings
- Publication of scientific journals
- Awards and recognition programs
- Outreach and science communication

### Mexican Academy of Sciences (Academia Mexicana de Ciencias)

Founded by **Guillermo Haro** in 1960, the Mexican Academy of Sciences promotes scientific research and collaboration across all disciplines, including physics.

### Colegio Nacional

**El Colegio Nacional** is Mexico's most prestigious honorary academy. Several distinguished physicists have been members, including Guillermo Haro, who became a member at age 40—the youngest person ever elected at that time.

## 2. Major University Physics Departments

### National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM)

UNAM is Mexico's largest and most prestigious university, with multiple institutes conducting world-class physics research.

#### Physics Institute (Instituto de Física - IFUNAM)

- **Location:** Mexico City (Ciudad Universitaria campus)
- **Website:** https://www.fisica.unam.mx
- **Description:** The first and largest physics institute in Mexico, IFUNAM conducts research across all major areas of physics and is at the forefront of research and academic development in the country.

**Research Departments:**
- Condensed Matter Physics
- High Energy Physics
- Nuclear Physics
- Plasma Physics
- Applied Physics
- Quantum Optics

#### Institute of Nuclear Sciences (Instituto de Ciencias Nucleares - ICN)

- **Location:** Mexico City
- **Website:** https://www.nucleares.unam.mx
- **Founded:** 1968
- **Description:** One of UNAM's premier research institutes focusing on nuclear sciences, with strong programs in high-energy physics, gravitation, and mathematical physics.

**Key Research Areas:**
- High Energy Physics (experimental and theoretical)
- Gravitation and Field Theories
- Mathematical Physics
- Plasma Physics
- Nuclear Chemistry

**High Energy Physics Department:**
- 14 researchers working on frontier theoretical and experimental research
- Major focus on Standard Model physics, cosmology, and beyond
- Strong detector development program

#### Institute of Astronomy (Instituto de Astronomía - IA-UNAM)

- **Locations:** Mexico City and Ensenada, Baja California
- **Website:** https://astronomia.unam.mx
- **Description:** Mexico's premier center for astronomical research, with two major campuses conducting cutting-edge astrophysical research.

**Research Areas:**
- Extragalactic astronomy
- Stellar astrophysics
- Solar physics
- Astronomical instrumentation
- Cosmology

### Center for Research and Advanced Studies (CINVESTAV)

- **Full Name:** Centro de Investigación y de Estudios Avanzados del Instituto Politécnico Nacional
- **Founded:** April 17, 1961
- **Founders:** President Adolfo López Mateos, Jaime Torres Bodet, Antonio Ortiz Mena, and **Arturo Rosenblueth**
- **Website:** https://www.cinvestav.mx

**Overview:**
CINVESTAV is a Mexican non-governmental scientific research institution affiliated with the National Polytechnic Institute (IPN). It operates 10 campuses across Mexico and is consistently ranked among the top research institutions in Latin America.

**Campuses:**

2. **Coapa** (South Mexico City)
3. **San Borja** (Mexico City)
4. **Guadalajara** (Jalisco)
5. **Irapuato** (Guanajuato)
6. **Mérida** (Yucatán)
7. **Querétaro** (Querétaro)
8. **Saltillo** (Coahuila)
9. **Monterrey** (Nuevo León)
10. **Ciudad Victoria** (Tamaulipas)

**Physics-Related Departments:**
- **Physics** (Zacatenco) - One of the leading research institutions in Mexico with international reputation
- **Applied Physics** (Mérida)
- **Mathematics**
- **Materials** (Querétaro)

**Physics Department Highlights:**
- Long experience in high energy physics research (GAE Group)
- Strong programs in condensed matter physics

## 4. Major Research Facilities

### Gran Telescopio Milimétrico Alfonso Serrano (LMT/GTM)

- **Type:** Single-dish millimeter-wave radio telescope
- **Location:** Sierra Negra volcano, Puebla, Mexico
- **Altitude:** 4,850 meters (15,910 feet)
- **Diameter:** 50 meters
- **Collecting Area:** 1,960 m²
- **Wavelength Range:** 0.85 - 4 mm
- **Inaugurated:** June 17, 2011
- **Named After:** Alfonso Serrano Pérez-Grovas (project initiator)

**Partnership:**
- **Mexico (70%):** INAOE
- **United States (30%):** University of Massachusetts Amherst

**Description:**
The LMT is the world's largest single-aperture telescope in its frequency range. Located on the fifth-highest peak in Mexico, this binational project represents the largest and most complex scientific instrument constructed in Mexico.

**Science Goals:**
- Star formation studies (penetrating interstellar dust)
- Protoplanetary disks and exoplanet formation
- Solar system planetesimals
- Evolved stars
- Molecular clouds
- Active galactic nuclei (AGNs)
- High-redshift galaxies
- Galaxy clusters
- Cosmic microwave background

**Instrumentation:**
- **TolTEC:** Three-band imaging polarimeter (1.1, 1.4, 2.1 mm)
- **SEQUOIA:** 32-pixel focal-plane array (85-116 GHz)
- **Redshift Search Receiver (RSR):** Ultra-wide-band receiver

### High Altitude Water Cherenkov Observatory (HAWC)

- **Type:** Gamma-ray and cosmic ray observatory
- **Location:** Sierra Negra volcano, Puebla, Mexico
- **Altitude:** 4,100 meters
- **Completed:** Spring 2015
- **Detectors:** 300 water Cherenkov detectors (plus outrigger array)

**Collaboration:**
- 15 US institutions
- 12 Mexican institutions
- Funding from NSF (US), DOE (US), and CONACyT (Mexico)

**Participating Mexican Institutions:**
- UNAM
- INAOE
- CINVESTAV
- BUAP
- Universidad de Guanajuato
- Universidad Michoacana

**Science Goals:**
- Study origin of cosmic rays
- Detect ultra-high-energy gamma rays
- Study particle acceleration in extreme environments
- Search for dark matter
- Test Lorentz invariance
- Multi-messenger astronomy

**Major Results:**
- First measurement of cosmic-ray spectrum (2017)
- Detection of gamma rays from the Sun at TeV energies (2023)
- Observation of ultrahigh-energy gamma rays from Galactic Center (2024)

### Guillermo Haro Observatory (OAGH)

- **Location:** Cananea, Sonora, Mexico
- **Operated by:** INAOE
- **Named After:** Guillermo Haro

**Telescopes:**
- 2.12-meter telescope
- 16-inch telescope for atmospheric extinction measurement

### Tonantzintla Observatory

- **Location:** Tonantzintla, Puebla
- **Founded:** 1942 by **Luis Enrique Erro**
- **Historical Significance:** Birthplace of modern Mexican astronomy
- **Instrument:** Historic Schmidt camera (famous for Herbig-Haro object discoveries)

**Historical Collection:**
- ~15,000 astro-photographic plates
- Major constellations: Orion, Taurus, Cancer, Gemini, Scorpio, Sagittarius, Ursa Major, Ursa Minor

### Gran Telescopio Canarias (GTC) Participation

Mexico participates in the Gran Telescopio Canarias ( world's largest optical-infrared telescope) through:
- INAOE
- Instituto de Astronomía UNAM

## 5. Notable Physicists

### Historical Figures

#### Manuel Sandoval Vallarta (1899-1977)

- **Legacy:** Pioneer of Mexican physics
- **Contribution:** Discovered the "east-west effect" in cosmic rays (1930s), demonstrating that cosmic rays are charged particles
- **Note:** Worked at MIT; foundational figure for Mexican physics

#### Luis Enrique Erro (1897-1955)

- **Fields:** Astronomy, education, politics
- **Achievement:** Founded the Tonantzintla Observatory (1942)
- **Legacy:** Established modern astronomical research in Mexico
- **Named After:** Planetario Luis Enrique Erro (Mexico City), lunar crater Erro

#### Guillermo Haro Barraza (1913-1988)

- **Field:** Astronomy
- **Achievements:**
  - Discovered Herbig-Haro objects (with George Herbig)
  - Founded INAOE (1971)
  - Founded Mexican Academy of Sciences (1960)
  - First Mexican elected to Royal Astronomical Society (1959)
  - Youngest member of El Colegio Nacional (age 40)
- **Named After:** Guillermo Haro Observatory, galaxy Haro 11

#### Arturo Rosenblueth (1900-1970)

- **Fields:** Physiology, cybernetics
- **Achievement:** Pioneer of cybernetics; co-author of "Behavior, Purpose and Teleology" (1943) with Norbert Wiener
- **Legacy:** First director of CINVESTAV (1961)

### Contemporary Physicists

#### Miguel Alcubierre Moya (b. 1964)

- **Field:** Theoretical physics, numerical relativity
- **Institution:** UNAM (Instituto de Ciencias Nucleares)
- **Famous For:** Alcubierre warp drive (1994) - a speculative faster-than-light propulsion concept within general relativity
- **Position:** Former Director of ICN-UNAM (2012-2020)
- **Education:** UNAM (Licentiate, MSc), Cardiff University (PhD with Bernard Schutz)

#### Jacob David Bekenstein (1947-2015)

- **Birth:** Mexico City
- **Fields:** Theoretical physics, black hole thermodynamics
- **Major Contributions:**
  - First to propose that black holes have entropy (1972)
  - Bekenstein bound (information limit in finite space)
  - Black hole thermodynamics
  - TeVeS (relativistic MOND theory)
- **Awards:** Wolf Prize in Physics (2012), Einstein Prize (2015)
- **Note:** Born in Mexico; career primarily in Israel and US

#### Carlos Silvestre Frenk (b. 1951)

- **Birth:** Mexico City
- **Fields:** Cosmology, astrophysics
- **Institution:** Durham University (UK) - Ogden Professor of Fundamental Physics
- **Major Contributions:**
  - Cold dark matter (CDM) theory
  - Navarro-Frenk-White (NFW) profile for dark matter halos
  - Virgo Consortium for cosmological simulations
- **Education:** UNAM (BSc), University of Cambridge (PhD)
- **Awards:** Fellow of the Royal Society (2004), Gruber Prize in Cosmology (2011), Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society (2014)

#### Silvia Torres-Peimbert (b. 1940)

- **Field:** Astronomy
- **Achievement:** Student of Guillermo Haro; prominent Mexican astronomer
- **Research:** Chemical composition of gaseous nebulae, stellar evolution

#### Manuel Peimbert

- **Field:** Astronomy
- **Achievement:** Student of Guillermo Haro; leading figure in nebular astrophysics
- **Research:** Chemical evolution of galaxies, cosmology

## 7. International Collaborations

### CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)

Mexico has a long-standing relationship with CERN dating back to the 1980s.

**History:**
- Individual Mexican physicists participated in UA1 experiment (1980s)
- Formal agreement signed in 1998 between CONACYT and CERN
- Protocol established in 2013; extended in 2018 (20th anniversary celebrated)

**Participating Experiments:**
- **ALICE:** Heavy-ion collisions, quark-gluon plasma
- **CMS:** General-purpose LHC detector
- **NA62:** Kaon physics
- **AMS:** Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer
- **ISOLDE:** Nuclear physics facility

**Mexican Institutions in CERN Collaborations:**
- UNAM
- CINVESTAV
- BUAP
- INAOE

**Capacity Building:**
- CONACyT grants for students to train at CERN
- Research toward doctorates in accelerator technologies
- CERN servers donated to Universidad de Chiapas (2015)

### HAWC Collaboration

Binational US-Mexico collaboration operating the HAWC observatory.

### Pierre Auger Observatory

Argentina-based ultra-high-energy cosmic ray observatory with Mexican participation (ICN-UNAM).

### ALICE Experiment at LHC

Strong Mexican participation through ICN-UNAM, studying quark-gluon plasma.

### LMT/GTM Collaboration

Binational Mexico-US project between INAOE and University of Massachusetts.

### Large Binocular Telescope (LBT)

Mexican participation through INAOE.

### Gran Telescopio Canarias (GTC)

Mexican participation through INAOE and IA-UNAM.

## Summary

Mexico's physics landscape is characterized by:

1. **Strong institutional foundations** - UNAM, CINVESTAV, and INAOE form the backbone of physics research
2. **World-class facilities** - LMT and HAWC are international flagship projects
3. **Distinguished researchers** - From historical figures like Haro and Erro to contemporary scientists like Alcubierre
4. **International integration** - Active participation in CERN, major observatories, and international collaborations
5. **Research breadth** - Excellence across high-energy physics, astrophysics, optics, and condensed matter
6. **Regional leadership** - Mexico serves as a hub for Latin American physics collaboration

The country continues to invest in physics research and education, maintaining its position as a major Latin American scientific power and an increasingly important player in global physics research.
