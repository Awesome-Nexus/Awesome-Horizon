# Physics in Serbia

## Overview

Serbia has a rich and distinguished tradition in physics research and education, dating back to the 19th century. The country maintains a robust physics ecosystem with strong university departments, national research institutes of international significance, active participation in major international collaborations (particularly CERN), and notable contributions to various fields of physics. Following the establishment of the Science Fund of the Republic of Serbia in 2019, the country's research funding system has undergone significant modernization, creating new opportunities for scientific advancement.

## 4. Notable Research Areas

### High Energy Physics and Particle Physics
- **CERN Collaborations**: Serbia has full membership in CERN. Major involvement in CMS (Compact Muon Solenoid) and ATLAS experiments since 1997
- Research at Institute of Physics Belgrade and Vinča Institute on detector hardware, data analysis, and theoretical interpretations
- Notable contribution to the Higgs boson discovery and subsequent precision measurements

### Condensed Matter Physics
- Strong focus at Institute of Physics Belgrade (national Center of Excellence in this field)
- Research on novel materials, nanostructures, quantum materials
- Vinča Institute: CEXTREME LAB - materials for extreme conditions

### Nuclear Physics
- Historical strength dating to Yugoslav nuclear program
- Current focus: applied nuclear physics, radiopharmaceuticals, nuclear safety
- Vinča Institute: unique radiopharmaceutical production facility (only GMP-certified facility in Balkans)

### Atomic, Molecular and Optical Physics
- Atomic collision processes at Institute of Physics Belgrade
- Laser physics and photonics
- Quantum optics research

### Plasma Physics
- Research at University of Novi Sad, University of Niš, and Vinča Institute
- Applications in materials processing, fusion research connections

### Astrophysics and Astronomy
- Stellar evolution and variability
- Solar physics
- Celestial mechanics
- Astrobiology (laboratory at University of Niš)

### Physics Education Research
- Active research groups at all major university physics departments
- Focus on physics pedagogy, conceptual understanding, teacher training

### Interdisciplinary Physics
- Biophysics and medical physics
- Environmental physics
- Complex systems and statistical physics

## 7. International Collaborations

### CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)
- **Status**: Full member state since 2019 (after provisional associate membership)
- **Participating Institutions**: Institute of Physics Belgrade, Vinča Institute of Nuclear Sciences, Faculty of Physics Belgrade, Faculty of Sciences Novi Sad
- **Experiments**: CMS (major involvement since 1997), ATLAS, DELPHI (historical)
- **Contributions**: Detector hardware (ECAL), physics analysis, computing, engineering
