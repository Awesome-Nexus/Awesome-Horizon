## Key Observations

### Strengths:
1. **North Africa** has the most developed physics education infrastructure
2. **South Africa** (covered in other documents) and **North Africa** lead in research output
3. **East Africa** shows strong growth in physics education
4. **French-speaking Africa** has well-structured LMD (Licence-Master-Doctorat) systems

### Challenges:
1. **Limited online presence** for many institutions in conflict-affected regions
2. **Resource constraints** in Central and parts of West Africa
3. **Brain drain** affecting research capacity in many countries
4. **Infrastructure gaps** in laboratory equipment across much of the continent

### Opportunities:
1. **Growing investment** in STEM education across Africa
2. **International partnerships** increasing (Erasmus+, Intra-Africa Academic Mobility)
3. **Focus on applied physics** for local challenges (renewable energy, health, agriculture)
4. **Astronomy initiatives** expanding (HESS, SKA-related programs)
