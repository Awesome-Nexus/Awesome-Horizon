## Overview

The Middle East has emerged as a significant region for physics research, with world-class facilities like SESAME in Jordan, leading research universities like KAUST in Saudi Arabia and the Weizmann Institute in Israel, and growing collaborations with international organizations like CERN. This report documents the major physics research institutions, their capabilities, and collaborative networks across the region.

## SESAME Synchrotron - Jordan

### What is SESAME?

**SESAME** (Synchrotron-light for **E**xperimental **S**cience and **A**pplications in the **M**iddle **E**ast) is a 2.5 GeV synchrotron light source located in Jordan, near Amman. It represents one of the most significant scientific infrastructure developments in the Middle East.

### Key Facts

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Location** | Allan, Balqa Governorate, Jordan |
| **Official Opening** | May 16, 2017 |
| **Energy** | 2.5 GeV |
| **Type** | Third-generation synchrotron light source |
| **Staff** | International team from member countries |
| **Users** | Over 2,000 researchers from the region |

### Member Countries

SESAME currently has **8 Member Countries**:

1. **Cyprus**
2. **Egypt**
3. **Iran**
4. **Israel**
5. **Jordan** (host country)
6. **Pakistan**
7. **Palestine**
8. **Turkey**

### Observer Countries and Organizations

Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, Greece, Italy, Japan, Kuwait, Portugal, Russian Federation, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, UAE, UK, USA, CERN, and the European Union hold Observer status.

### Beamlines in Operation (2024-2025)

| Beamline | Purpose | Status |
|----------|---------|--------|
| BM08-XAFS/XRF | X-ray Absorption Fine Structure / X-ray Fluorescence | Operational since 2018 |
| BM02-IR | Infrared Spectromicroscopy | Operational since 2018 |
| ID09-MS/XPD | Materials Science / Powder Diffraction | Operational since 2020 |
| ID10-BEATS | Tomography (EU-funded) | Operational since Feb 2024 |
| ID11L-HESEB | Soft X-ray (Helmholtz-SESAME) | Operational since Feb 2024 |
| ID11R-TXPES | Turkish soft X-ray PhotoElectron Spectroscopy | Under commissioning |

### Scientific Output

- **145+ peer-reviewed publications** (as of 2025)
- Average journal impact factor: **5.6**
- **20%** of papers in journals with impact factor > 7
- **1,158 proposals** received from 39 countries
- **423 experiments** granted beam time

### Unique Features

- World's **first large accelerator complex fully powered by renewable energy** (since 2019)
- Carbon-neutral accelerator laboratory
- Science diplomacy instrument bringing together countries with diverse political systems
- Training hub for regional scientists

### Research Areas

- Biology and medical sciences
- Materials science
- Physics and chemistry
- Cultural heritage studies
- Environmental science
- Agriculture

### Funding

Total investment: ~**US$135 million**
- Funded by member countries, EU, IAEA, Germany, Italy, Switzerland
- Equipment donations from Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Switzerland, UK

## Major Physics Centers by Country

### Israel

#### Weizmann Institute of Science

**Location:** Rehovot, Israel  
**Founded:** 1934 (as Daniel Sieff Research Institute)  
**Website:** [www.weizmann.ac.il/physics](https://www.weizmann.ac.il/physics)

The Weizmann Institute is one of the world's premier research institutions, ranked #2 globally for research quality by Nature Index in 2019.

##### Physics Research Areas

| Department | Research Focus | Key Faculty |
|------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Astrophysics** | Stellar explosions, gravitational wave counterparts, exoplanets, ULTRASAT space mission | Prof. Avishay Gal-Yam, Prof. Eran Ofek |
| **Condensed Matter** | Quantum mechanics, superconductivity, topological phases | Prof. Erez Berg, Prof. Yuval Oreg |
| **Particle & Astroparticle** | ATLAS experiment, dark matter, detector development | Prof. Ehud Duchovni, Prof. Eilam Gross |
| **Plasma & Nuclear** | Plasma physics, heavy-ion collisions | Prof. Victor Malka, Prof. Alexander Milov |
| **Biological & Soft Matter** | Emergent properties in physical/biological systems | Prof. Ariel Amir, Prof. Elisha Moses |

##### Major Physics Projects

- **ULTRASAT**: Wide-field UV space telescope (launched 2024)
- **W-FAST, LAST, MAST**: Ground-based observatories
- **ATLAS Experiment**: CERN LHC collaboration
- **XENON**: Dark matter search
- **WeizQC**: Israel's first quantum computer (2022)

##### Notable Achievements

- 6 Nobel laureates associated with the Institute
- Development of WEIZAC (1955) - Middle East's first computer
- String theory foundations (Veneziano amplitude, 1967)
- MOND theory (Modified Newtonian Dynamics, 1983)
- World Wide Web technologies

#### Technion - Israel Institute of Technology

**Location:** Haifa, Israel  
**Website:** [physics.technion.ac.il](https://physics.technion.ac.il)

The Technion is one of Israel's leading technical universities with strong physics research programs in:
- Condensed matter physics
- Particle physics
- Astrophysics
- Biophysics

## Conclusion

The Middle East has developed into a region with significant physics research capabilities, anchored by:

1. **SESAME** - The region's flagship international research facility, promoting scientific collaboration across political boundaries

2. **World-class universities** - KAUST, Weizmann Institute, and others conducting cutting-edge research

3. **Growing CERN engagement** - From full membership (Israel) to associate membership (Turkey) and expanding collaborations (UAE, Iran)

4. **Astronomy renaissance** - Led by Morocco's Oukaïmeden Observatory and Israel's ULTRASAT mission

5. **Regional collaboration** - Science diplomacy through shared facilities like SESAME and cross-border research partnerships

The region continues to invest heavily in research infrastructure, with particular strengths in energy research (solar, nuclear), particle physics, and astronomy. These developments position the Middle East as an increasingly important contributor to global physics research.
