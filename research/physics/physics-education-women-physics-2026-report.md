## 1. Physics Education Statistics (Global)

### 1.1 Overview

**International Union of Pure and Applied Physics (IUPAP)**
- **URL:** https://www.iupap.org/
- Established: 1922 in Brussels
- Current Membership: 60 country members
- Mission: Assist in worldwide development of physics, foster international cooperation, and help apply physics toward solving problems of concern to humanity

### 1.2 Physics Education Data Sources

**AIP Statistical Research (American Institute of Physics)**
- **URL:** https://www.aip.org/statistics
- The preeminent source of data on education and employment in physics, astronomy and physical sciences
- Conducting surveys since 1941
- Formal statistical research unit established in 1962
- Staff: ~10 full-time survey researchers and analysts

**National Center for Science and Engineering Statistics (NCSES)**
- **URL:** https://ncses.nsf.gov/
- U.S. federal statistical agency providing data on science and engineering enterprise
- Part of the National Science Foundation

### 1.3 Key Statistics

**Physics Doctorates (United States)**
- Source: AIP Statistical Research
- Data available from 1900-present on number of physics PhDs granted
- Typical starting salaries for new physics PhDs vary by sector:
  - Private sector potentially permanent positions: Highest median salary
  - Over 70% of all postdocs filled in academic institutions

**Physics Faculty Demographics (US)**
- Women among physics faculty data tracked by AIP
- Increasing internationalization: About half of USPTO patents awarded to foreign inventors (2020)

## 2. Women in Physics (2024-2025)

### 2.1 IUPAP Women in Physics Working Group (WG5)

**URL:** https://www.iupap.org/who-we-are/internal-organization/working-groups/wg5-women-in-physics/

- **Created:** 1999 (resolution of Atlanta, Georgia General Assembly)
- **Mission:** Survey the present situation of women physicists, report to Council and Liaison Committees, suggest means to improve the situation

**International Conference on Women in Physics (ICWIP)**
- Held every three years since 2002
- **Past Conferences:**
  | Year | Location | Host Country |
  |------|----------|--------------|
  | 2002 | Paris, France | France |
  | 2005 | Rio de Janeiro | Brazil |
  | 2008 | Seoul | Korea |
  | 2011 | Stellenbosch | South Africa |
  | 2014 | Waterloo | Canada |
  | 2017 | Birmingham | UK (with guest speaker Malala Yousafzai) |
  | 2020 | Melbourne (virtual) | Australia |
  | 2023 | Virtual event | India (July 10-14, 2023) |

- **Attendance:** Over 92 different countries and 1300+ delegates have attended ICWIPs
- **Outcomes:** Many new national bodies on women in physics created; regional meetings established

**International Day of Women in Physics (IDWIP)**
- **Date:** February 11th (annually)
- Aims to celebrate women in physics of the past, support women in physics now, inspire future women in physics

**Global Surveys:**
- 2002: Global survey of female physicists
- 2006: Follow-up survey
- 2009-10: Third survey (included men for first time)
- 2018: Latest survey conducted with ten other ISC Unions as part of "Gender Gap in Science project"

### 2.2 APS Women in Physics Programs

**URL:** https://www.aps.org/programs/women/

**Programs include:**
- Conference for Undergraduate Women in Physics (CUWiP)
- Women in Physics workshops
- Statistics and reports on women in physics
- Career development resources

### 2.3 IOP (Institute of Physics) Women in Physics

**URL:** https://www.iop.org/careers/working-physics/being-physicist

**Key Initiatives:**
- **Limit Less:** Campaign to support young people to change the world and fulfill their potential by doing physics
- **Girls in Physics:** Programs specifically targeting girls' participation
- **Broken Bias:** Addressing unconscious bias in physics

### 2.4 L'Oréal-UNESCO For Women in Science

**URL:** https://www.unesco.org/en/prizes/women-science

**Statistics:**
- **Since 1998:** 132 laureates recognized
- **Nobel Prize Connection:** 7 laureates subsequently received Nobel Prizes
- **Total Supported:** 4,400+ women researchers
- **Geographic Coverage:** 140+ countries

**Program Components:**
1. **International Awards:** 5 outstanding women scientists annually (one per region)
   - Africa and the Arab States
   - Asia and the Pacific
   - Europe
   - Latin America and the Caribbean
   - North America

2. **International Rising Talents:** 15 promising young women scientists annually (doctoral or post-doctoral level)

3. **National and Regional Young Talents Programmes:** Implemented by L'Oréal subsidiaries worldwide

### 2.5 Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA)

**URL:** https://marie-sklodowska-curie-actions.ec.europa.eu/

**Overview:**
- European Union's reference programme for doctoral education and postdoctoral training
- **30th Anniversary:** 2026 marks 30 years of MSCA

**Key Features:**
- Fosters research across borders, sectors and disciplines
- Budget: Part of Horizon Europe programme
- 2024 Call: Record number of applications and grants
- 2025 Budget: €652 million invested for 65+ projects

### 2.6 Percentage of Women in Physics by Region

Based on ICWIP surveys and reports:

**Regions with Highest Representation:**
- Latin America: Higher than global average
- Parts of Southeast Asia: Increasing participation
- Middle East: Growing numbers in some countries

**Ongoing Challenges:**
- Underrepresentation in senior faculty positions globally
- Gender pay gaps persist
- Lower representation in certain subfields (e.g., nuclear physics, high-energy physics)

### 2.7 Notable Women Physicists - Recent Awards

**L'Oréal-UNESCO 2024 Laureates (Physics-related fields):**
- Awards given across all five regions for outstanding contributions

**IUPAP Awards:**
- Various commissions recognize women physicists at different career stages
- Awards presented at major IUPAP sponsored conferences

## 3. Physics Olympiad Statistics

### 3.1 International Physics Olympiad (IPhO)

**URL:** https://ipho-new.org/

**Overview:**
- **Established:** 1967 (Warsaw, Poland by Prof. Czesław Ścisłowski)
- **Participation:** Maximum 5 students + 2 team leaders per country
- **Format:** 9-10 day competition
- **Current Membership:** 90+ participating countries/territories

**IPhO 2024 - Iran**
- **Host:** Isfahan, Iran
- **Dates:** July 2024
- **Venue:** Isfahan University of Technology, Sheikh Baha'i Grand Hall
- **Significance:** Second time Iran hosted (first was 38th IPhO in 2007)
- Iran has also hosted: Astronomy, Computer, and Biology Olympiads

**IPhO 2025**
- **Host:** Japan (planned)

**Competition Structure:**
- Theoretical examination
- Experimental examination
- Tests highest level of knowledge, critical thinking, problem solving
- Hands-on skills in theoretical and experimental physics

### 3.2 Asian Physics Olympiad (APhO)

- Regional competition for Asian countries
- Held annually
- Multiple participating countries across Asia-Pacific region

### 3.3 Country Rankings (Historical Performance)

**Traditionally Strong Countries:**
- China
- United States
- Russia
- South Korea
- Taiwan
- Vietnam
- India
- Iran

**Participation Statistics:**
- Over 90 countries/territories currently participate in IPhO
- Growth from 13 countries in early years to 90+ today

## 4. Developing World Physics Initiatives

### 4.1 African School of Physics (ASP)

**URL:** https://africanschoolofphysics.org/

**Overview:**
- **Established:** 2010 (first edition)
- **Frequency:** Biennial event
- **Founding Partners:** ICTP and world's top research institutes/laboratories

**ASP 2024 - Morocco**
- **Host:** Cadi Ayyad University, Marrakesh, Morocco
- **Dates:** July 7-21, 2024
- **Participants:** 143 students (93 in person + 50 online)
- **Countries Represented:** 28 countries
- **Gender Balance:** ~50% women participants
- **Topics:** Nuclear and particle physics, astrophysics and cosmology, accelerator physics, high-performance computing, quantum information

**Additional ASP 2024 Components:**
- High school teacher training: 80 teachers from Morocco
- High school outreach: 1000+ pupils in Marrakesh region (April 15-19, 2024)
- Policy forum for African policymakers in physics education and research

**Future Events:**
- **2025:** Fourth African Conference on Fundamental and Applied Physics - Togo
- **2026:** Ninth African School of Physics - Kenya

**Impact:**
- Alumni network for continued collaboration
- Former students becoming lecturers at future schools
- Career development support (e.g., Dr. Mounia Laassiri - postdoc at Brookhaven National Laboratory)

### 4.2 Abdus Salam International Centre for Theoretical Physics (ICTP)

**URL:** https://www.ictp.it/

**Overview:**
- **Location:** Trieste, Italy
- **Founded:** 1964 by Nobel Laureate Abdus Salam
- **Operated under:** UNESCO and IAEA auspices
- **Mission:** Foster theoretical physics and mathematical science in developing countries

**2025 Strategic Plan:**
- Historic role: "science is common heritage"
- Continues to assist worldwide development of physics

**Educational Programs:**
1. **Postgraduate Diploma Programme:**
   - One-year intensive program
   - Fields: High Energy Physics, Cosmology, Condensed Matter Physics, Mathematics, Earth System Physics

2. **PhD Programs:**
   - Joint programs with international universities
   - Focus on students from developing countries

3. **Schools and Workshops:**
   - 2026: ICTP School on Synchrotron Light Sources (January 12-23, 2026)
   - LAAAMP co-organized events

**Impact Stories:**
- Research breakthroughs in neutrino oscillations, climate modeling
- Advances in quantum technologies and artificial intelligence
- Training of thousands of scientists from developing countries

### 4.3 SESAME (Synchrotron-light for Experimental Science and Applications in the Middle East)

**URL:** https://www.sesame.org.jo/

**Overview:**
- **Location:** Allan, Jordan
- **Type:** Third-generation synchrotron light source
- **Members:** Bahrain, Cyprus, Egypt, Iran, Israel, Jordan, Pakistan, Palestinian Authority, Turkey

**Key Milestones:**
- 2017: First beam circulation
- 2018: Official laboratory opening
- 2018: First users hosted
- 2019: World's first large accelerator complex fully powered by renewable energy
- 2022: First light for Materials Science (MS) beamline
- 2023: BEATS beamline inauguration
- 2024: Turkish TXPES beamline inaugurated (first beamline built by a member)

**Beamlines:**
- XAFS/XRF (X-ray Absorption Fine Structure/X-ray Fluorescence)
- IR (Infrared Spectromicroscopy)
- MS (Materials Science)
- HESEB (Helmholtz-SESAME Beamline)
- BEATS (BEAmline for Tomography)
- TXPES (Turkish X-ray Photoemission Spectroscopy)

**Strategic Plan 2024-2028:**
- Foster excellent science and technology in Middle East and Mediterranean
- Enable world-class research
- Build regional scientific capacity

### 4.4 CERN openlab

**URL:** https://openlab.cern/

**Overview:**
- **Established:** 2001 (23+ years of operation)
- **Mission:** Accelerate computing for science through collaboration between CERN, ICT companies, and research centers
- **Current Phase:** Eighth phase (2024-2026)

**Partners:**
- Leading ICT companies
- Research organizations worldwide

**Focus Areas:**
- Multidisciplinary collaboration
- Scientific computing advancement
- Technology development beyond high-energy physics
- Societal impact on global scale

### 4.5 African Strategy for Fundamental and Applied Physics (ASFAP)

**Report:** January 2025
- Comprehensive strategy for physics development in Africa
- Focus on fundamental and applied physics
- Emphasizes African-led initiatives with international partnerships

### 4.6 Scholarship Programs for Developing Countries

**ICTP Programs:**
- Full scholarships for diploma program (tuition, housing, meals)
- Travel grants for students from developing countries
- Research fellowships

**OWSD (Organization for Women in Science for the Developing World):**
- PhD fellowships for women scientists from developing countries
- Early career fellowships

**L'Oréal-UNESCO National Fellowships:**
- Available in 140+ countries
- Focus on supporting young women researchers

**SEED Funding (SESAME):**
- Supports researchers from member countries
- Travel and accommodation grants
