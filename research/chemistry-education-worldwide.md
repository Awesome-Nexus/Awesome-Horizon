### ACS-Approved Chemistry Programs

The American Chemical Society (ACS) approves bachelor's degree programs that meet rigorous standards for curriculum, faculty, and facilities.

#### About ACS Approval
- **Purpose:** Ensures comprehensive education preparing students for professional careers
- **Website:** https://www.acs.org/education/policies/acs-approved-programs.html
- **Benefits:**
  - Rigorous curriculum covering all chemistry subdisciplines
  - Laboratory experience with modern instrumentation
  - Access to undergraduate research
  - Professional networking opportunities

#### Finding ACS-Approved Programs
The ACS maintains a searchable database of over 700 approved programs:
- **Search Tool:** https://www.acs.org/education/policies/acs-approved-programs.html
- **Coverage:** Programs in all 50 US states and international institutions
- **Degree Tracks:** Chemistry, biochemistry, chemical engineering, materials chemistry

#### Key Requirements for ACS Approval
1. Comprehensive curriculum in all major chemistry areas
2. Minimum laboratory hours with modern instrumentation
3. Qualified faculty actively engaged in research
4. Adequate library and information resources
5. Opportunities for undergraduate research
6. Safety training and protocols

### U.S. National Chemistry Olympiad (USNCO)

#### Overview
The USNCO is a multi-tiered chemistry competition for high school students, sponsored by the American Chemical Society since 1984.

**Official Website:** https://www.acs.org/education/olympiad.html

#### Competition Structure

| Level | Description | Timing |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Local Exam** | 60 multiple-choice questions (110 minutes) | February-March |
| **National Exam** | 3 parts: Multiple Choice, Free Response, Lab Practical | April |
| **Study Camp** | 2-week intensive training for top 20 students | June |
| **International** | IChO competition with 4 selected team members | July |

#### 2026 Important Dates
- **October 6, 2025:** Student registration opens
- **February 27 - March 16, 2026:** Local Exam
- **April 10-19, 2026:** National Exam
- **May 31 - June 13, 2026:** Study Camp
- **July 10-19, 2026:** International Chemistry Olympiad (Tashkent, Uzbekistan)

#### Eligibility Requirements
- U.S. citizens or legal permanent residents (for National Exam)
- Enrolled in grades 9-12
- Under 20 years old on July 1 of competition year
- Maximum 2 students per school per local section

#### Study Resources
- **USNCO Coaching Program:** https://www.acs.org/education/olympiad/prepare-for-exams/coaching-program.html
- **Past Exams:** Available on ACS website
- **Tutorials:** Topic-specific preparation materials
- **Local Section Coordinators:** https://www.usncolookup.acs.org/

### Summer Research Programs (REU)

#### NSF Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU)

The National Science Foundation supports undergraduate research through REU programs at universities nationwide.

**Official Information:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/initiatives/reu/students

#### Program Features
- **Duration:** Typically 10 weeks (summer)
- **Stipend:** $5,000-$7,000+ depending on program
- **Housing:** Often provided or subsidized
- **Travel:** Support for travel to/from program site
- **Eligibility:** U.S. citizens or permanent residents

#### Notable Chemistry REU Programs

| Institution | Focus Areas | Application Deadline |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Texas A&M University** | Sustainability, green chemistry, materials | February 15 |
| **University of Minnesota** | All chemistry disciplines | February 2 |
| **University of Arkansas** | Sustainable chemistry, materials | February 15 |
| **MIT** | Various chemistry research areas | Varies |
| **University of California, Berkeley** | Chemical biology, materials | Varies |

#### How to Apply
1. Search REU Site Directory: https://www.nsf.gov/crssprgm/reu/
2. Prepare materials: transcript, resume, personal statement, letters of recommendation
3. Apply through NSF ETAP or directly to programs
4. **Typical Deadline:** January-March for summer programs

## INTERNATIONAL CHEMISTRY OLYMPIAD (IChO)

### History and Overview

The International Chemistry Olympiad (IChO) is the premier global competition for high school chemistry students.

**Official Website:** https://www.icho-official.org/

#### Founding and Development
- **Founded:** 1968 in Prague, Czechoslovakia
- **Founding Countries:** Czechoslovakia, Poland, Hungary (3 countries)
- **First Expansion:** Western countries joined starting 1975-1980
- **Current Participation:** ~90 countries, 360+ students annually

#### Competition Structure

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Format** | Theoretical Exam (5 hours) + Practical Exam (5 hours) |
| **Points** | Theoretical: 60 points, Practical: 40 points (Total: 100) |
| **Team Size** | 4 students + 2 mentors per country |
| **Medal Distribution** | Gold: Top 12%, Silver: Next 22%, Bronze: Next 32%, Honorable Mention: Top 10% of non-medalists |

### National Qualifying Rounds

Countries select their IChO teams through various national competition structures:

#### United States
- **Program:** USNCO (U.S. National Chemistry Olympiad)
- **Coordinator:** American Chemical Society
- **Process:** Local Exam → National Exam → Study Camp → IChO Team

#### United Kingdom
- **Program:** UK Chemistry Olympiad
- **Coordinator:** Royal Society of Chemistry
- **Website:** https://edu.rsc.org/

#### China
- **Process:** Provincial competitions → National Olympiad in Chemistry (CChO) → National Training Camp → IChO Team

#### India
- **Program:** Indian National Chemistry Olympiad (INChO)
- **Coordinator:** Homi Bhabha Centre for Science Education (HBCSE)

#### Other Countries
Most participating nations have similar multi-tier selection systems involving regional and national competitions.

### Training Camps

#### International Approach
- **Duration:** Typically 1-2 weeks of intensive training
- **Content:** Advanced organic, inorganic, physical chemistry; laboratory skills
- **Format:** Lectures, problem-solving sessions, laboratory practice
- **Goal:** Prepare students for IChO competition level

#### USNCO Study Camp (Example)
- **Location:** Typically hosted by a US university
- **Duration:** 2 weeks in June
- **Participants:** Top 20 National Exam scorers
- **Activities:** Lectures, lab work, exams; selection of 4 IChO team members

### ACS Safety Guidelines

#### Overview
The American Chemical Society provides comprehensive safety resources for chemistry laboratories.

**Main Safety Page:** https://www.acs.org/about/governance/committees/chemical-safety.html

#### Key Documents

| Resource | Description | Link |
|----------|-------------|------|
| **ACS Guidelines for Chemical Laboratory Safety in Secondary Schools** | Comprehensive safety guidelines for high school chemistry labs | [Download PDF](https://www.acs.org/content/dam/acsorg/about/governance/committees/chemicalsafety/publications/acs-safety-guidelines-academic.pdf) |
| **Laboratory Safety Manual** | General laboratory safety practices | [Access](https://www.acs.org/content/dam/pldp/center/lab-safety/publications/laboratory-safety.pdf) |

#### RAMP Process
The ACS recommends using RAMP for all laboratory activities:
1. **R**ecognize hazards
2. **A**ssess the risks of the hazards
3. **M**inimize and manage the risks of the hazards
4. **P**repare for the unexpected and emergencies

### Chemical Disposal Guidelines

#### General Principles
1. **Reduce:** Minimize chemical waste through good inventory management
2. **Reuse:** Share unused chemicals when possible
3. **Recycle:** Reclaim solvents and other materials
4. **Dispose:** Use proper disposal methods for remaining waste

#### ACS Waste Management Resources
- **Chemical Inventory Management:** Track chemicals to minimize waste
- **Waste Segregation:** Separate compatible waste streams
- **Satellite Accumulation:** Proper on-site waste storage
- **Disposal Documentation:** Maintain records of waste disposal

#### Flinn Scientific Disposal Guides
- **SDS Database:** Contains disposal information for each chemical
- **Waste Disposal Procedures:** Step-by-step disposal instructions
- **Neutralization Procedures:** For acid/base waste
- **Heavy Metal Waste:** Special handling procedures
