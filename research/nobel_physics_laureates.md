# Nobel Prize in Physics Laureates (1901-2025)

## Overview

The Nobel Prize in Physics has been awarded **119 times** from 1901 to 2025, honoring **230 laureates** representing **229 individuals** (John Bardeen won twice).

## Notable Facts

1. **John Bardeen** is the only person to win the Nobel Prize in Physics twice (1956 for the transistor effect, 1972 for superconductivity theory).

2. **William Lawrence Bragg** remains the youngest Physics laureate at age 25 (1915), sharing the prize with his father William Henry Bragg.

3. **Arthur Ashkin** was the oldest laureate at age 96 when awarded in 2018.

4. **Marie Curie** is the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields (Physics in 1903, Chemistry in 1911).

5. The **United States** dominates with 97 laureates (about 42% of all Physics laureates).

6. **Germany** and the **United Kingdom** are tied for second with 26 laureates each (approximately 11% each).

7. Only **5 women** have won the Physics Nobel out of 229 individual laureates (~2.2%).

8. The prize has been awarded 119 times from 1901 to 2025, with 6 years skipped.

9. **Bell Labs** has produced more Physics Nobel laureates than any other single institution.

10. The 2024 prize was notable for being awarded for work in **machine learning and artificial neural networks**, marking a significant expansion of what is considered "physics."
