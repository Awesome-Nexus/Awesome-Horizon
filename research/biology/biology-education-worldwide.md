#### AP Biology Curriculum

The AP Biology course is a college-level program offered by the College Board for high school students.

**Course Structure:**
- 8 Units covering major biology topics
- Emphasis on scientific inquiry and laboratory investigations
- 3-hour exam with multiple-choice and free-response sections

**Key Topics:**
1. Chemistry of Life
2. Cell Structure and Function
3. Cellular Energetics
4. Cell Communication and Cell Cycle
5. Heredity
6. Gene Expression and Regulation
7. Natural Selection
8. Ecology

**Resources:**
- Official AP Biology Course: https://apstudents.collegeboard.org/courses/ap-biology
- AP Biology Lab Manual: https://www.collegeboard.org/

#### MCAT Preparation for Pre-Med

The Medical College Admission Test (MCAT) is required for admission to most medical schools in the United States and Canada.

**MCAT Exam Sections:**
| Section | Content Focus | Score Range |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Chemical & Physical Foundations | General chemistry, organic chemistry, physics, biochemistry | 118-132 |
| Biological & Biochemical Foundations | Biology, biochemistry, general chemistry, organic chemistry | 118-132 |
| Psychological, Social & Biological Foundations | Psychology, sociology, biology | 118-132 |
| Critical Analysis & Reasoning Skills | Reading comprehension, critical thinking | 118-132 |

**Pre-Med Course Requirements:**
- One year General Chemistry with lab
- One year Organic Chemistry with lab
- One year Biology with lab
- One year Physics with lab
- One year English/Writing
- Biochemistry (recommended)
- Psychology and Sociology (recommended)

**Competitive Scores:**
- Competitive for medical school: 509+ (127+ per section)
- Top medical schools: 515+ (128-129+ per section)

**Resources:**
- AAMC MCAT: https://www.aamc.org/exams/mcat/
- MSAR (Medical School Admission Requirements): https://students-residents.aamc.org/

#### NSF-Funded REU Programs

The National Science Foundation (NSF) Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU) program supports undergraduate research across the United States.

**Program Features:**
- 10-week summer research programs
- Stipends typically $500-600/week
- Housing and travel assistance often provided
- Available in all areas of biology and related fields

**How to Apply:**
- Search REU Sites: https://www.nsf.gov/funding/initiatives/reu
- BIO REU Sites: https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/bio-reu-sites
- Apply directly to individual REU Sites

**Eligibility:**
- US citizens or permanent residents
- Currently enrolled undergraduates
- Students from underrepresented groups especially encouraged
