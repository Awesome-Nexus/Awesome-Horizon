## 1. Russian Academy of Sciences (RAS) Structure

### Overview
The **Russian Academy of Sciences (RAS)** is Russia's national academy, founded in 1724 by Peter the Great. It comprises the national academy, a network of scientific research institutes, libraries, publishing units, and hospitals. As of 2025, RAS has approximately 1,960 living Russian members and about 445 foreign members.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Founded** | February 8, 1724 |
| **Headquarters** | Leninsky Prospekt 14, Moscow |
| **President** | Gennady Krasnikov (since September 2022) |
| **Website** | [www.ras.ru](https://www.ras.ru) / [new.ras.ru/en](https://new.ras.ru/en) |
| **Total Institutions** | ~1,008 institutions and units |
| **Total Employees** | ~125,000 (47,000 researchers) |

### RAS Territorial Branches

| Branch | Location | Established | Research Centers |
|--------|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Siberian Branch (SB RAS)** | Novosibirsk (Akademgorodok) | 1957 | Novosibirsk, Tomsk, Krasnoyarsk, Irkutsk, Yakutsk, Ulan-Ude, Kemerovo, Tyumen, Omsk |
| **Ural Branch (UB RAS)** | Yekaterinburg | 1932 | Yekaterinburg, Perm, Chelyabinsk, Izhevsk, Orenburg, Ufa, Syktyvkar |
| **Far East Branch (FEB RAS)** | Vladivostok | - | Vladivostok, Blagoveshchensk, Khabarovsk, Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk, Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, Magadan |
| **St. Petersburg Branch** | St. Petersburg | 2023 | St. Petersburg |

### RAS Physics-Related Scientific Divisions

| Division | Focus Areas |
|----------|-------------|
| **Physical Sciences** | Fundamental physics, condensed matter, optics |
| **Mathematical Sciences** | Mathematical physics, computational physics |
| **Earth Sciences** | Geophysics, atmospheric physics, space physics |

## 3. Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR)

### Overview
The **Joint Institute for Nuclear Research** is an international intergovernmental organization established in 1956 in Dubna, Russia. It is one of the world's leading international research centers for nuclear physics.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Founded** | March 26, 1956 |
| **Location** | Dubna, Moscow Oblast (110 km north of Moscow) |
| **Director General** | Grigory V. Trubnikov (since 2021) |
| **Staff** | ~5,500 total; 1,200 researchers (1,000+ PhDs) |
| **Website** | [www.jinr.ru](https://www.jinr.ru/main-en/) |

### Member States (2025)

| Full Members |
|-------------|
| Armenia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Cuba, Egypt, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Moldova, Mongolia, Russia, Uzbekistan, Vietnam |

| Associate Members |
|-------------------|
| Germany, Hungary, Italy, Serbia, South Africa |

### JINR Laboratories

| Laboratory | Focus | Key Facilities |
|------------|-------|----------------|
| **BLTP** - Bogoliubov Laboratory of Theoretical Physics | Quantum field theory, nuclear theory, condensed matter theory | - |
| **VBLHE** - Veksler and Baldin Laboratory of High Energy Physics | Heavy ion physics, spin physics, elementary particles | Nuclotron, Synchrophasotron, NICA |
| **DLNP** - Dzhelepov Laboratory of Nuclear Problems | Neutrino physics, astrophysics, particle physics | Phasotron, Synchrocyclotron |
| **FLNR** - Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions | Superheavy element synthesis, nuclear reactions | U400, U400M, DC-280 Cyclotrons, SHE Factory |
| **FLNP** - Frank Laboratory of Neutron Physics | Neutron physics, condensed matter physics | IBR-2 pulsed reactor, IREN |
| **MLIT** - Meshcheryakov Laboratory of Information Technologies | Computing, grid infrastructure, data processing | HybriLIT, CICC |
| **LRB** - Laboratory of Radiation Biology | Radiation therapy, radiobiology | - |

### Major Achievements
- 1957: Prediction of neutrino oscillation (Bruno Pontecorvo)
- Discovery of superheavy elements 113-118 (Nihonium, Flerovium, Moscovium, Livermorium, Tennessine, Oganesson)
- Named elements: Dubnium (105), Flerovium (114), Moscovium (115), Oganesson (118)

### Key Facilities
- **Nuclotron**: First superconducting synchrotron in the world (7 GeV)
- **NICA**: Heavy ion collider under construction
- **IBR-2**: High-flux pulsed fast-neutron reactor
- **SHE Factory**: Superheavy Element Factory (opened 2019)
