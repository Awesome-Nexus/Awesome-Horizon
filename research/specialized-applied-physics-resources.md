### 5.2 Major Fusion Research Facilities

#### ITER - International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor
- **Website:** https://www.iter.org/
- **Location:** Saint-Paul-lès-Durance, France
- **Description:** World's largest tokamak fusion experiment
- **Members:** China, EU, India, Japan, Korea, Russia, USA
- **Goal:** Demonstrate fusion power production at power plant scale
- **Status:** Under construction

#### Wendelstein 7-X
- **Location:** Greifswald, Germany
- **Operator:** Max Planck Institute for Plasma Physics (IPP)
- **Website:** https://www.ipp.mpg.de/w7x
- **Description:** World's largest stellarator fusion device
- **Features:**
  - First plasma: December 2015
  - Continuous operation capability
  - Superconducting magnets
  - Basis for power plant concepts

#### JT-60SA
- **Location:** Naka, Japan
- **Operator:** National Institutes for Quantum Science and Technology (QST), JAEA
- **Description:** Superconducting tokamak, largest in Japan
- **Purpose:** Support ITER and DEMO development

#### NSTX-U - National Spherical Torus Experiment Upgrade
- **Location:** Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory, USA
- **Description:** Spherical tokamak for fusion research
- **Status:** Undergoing upgrades

#### JET - Joint European Torus
- **Location:** Culham, UK
- **Operator:** UKAEA
- **Description:** Former largest tokamak, achieved 59 MJ fusion energy record

#### KSTAR - Korea Superconducting Tokamak Advanced Research
- **Location:** Daejeon, South Korea
- **Description:** Superconducting tokamak with advanced plasma control

#### EAST - Experimental Advanced Superconducting Tokamak
- **Location:** Hefei, China
- **Description:** Chinese superconducting tokamak
