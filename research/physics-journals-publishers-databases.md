## 2. SPECIALIZED DATABASES & RESOURCES

### 2.1 Major Academic Search Databases

#### Web of Science (Clarivate)
**Website:** https://www.webofscience.com/
**Description:** Premier multidisciplinary citation database covering sciences, social sciences, arts, and humanities.
- Coverage: 1900-present
- Physics coverage: All major physics journals
- Features: Citation tracking, h-index calculation, impact factor data
- Access: Subscription required

#### Scopus (Elsevier)
**Website:** https://www.scopus.com/
**Description:** Large abstract and citation database of peer-reviewed literature.
- Coverage: 1788-present
- Documents: 80+ million records
- Physics coverage: Extensive physics and engineering
- Features: Citation analysis, author profiles, journal metrics
- Access: Subscription required

#### Google Scholar
**Website:** https://scholar.google.com/
**Description:** Free search engine for scholarly literature.
- Coverage: Broad, including preprints, theses, books, and articles
- Features: Citation counts, author profiles, alerts
- Physics coverage: Excellent coverage including arXiv preprints
- Access: Free

#### Semantic Scholar
**Website:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/
**Description:** AI-powered academic search engine.
- Coverage: 200+ million papers across all disciplines
- Features: AI-generated paper summaries, citation context, research feeds
- Physics coverage: Strong coverage including arXiv
- Access: Free with optional account

### 2.2 Physics-Specific Databases

#### arXiv
**Website:** https://arxiv.org/
**Description:** Free distribution service and open-access archive for scholarly articles.
- Founded: 1991 (by Paul Ginsparg at LANL)
- Papers: Nearly 2.4 million scholarly articles
- Physics Coverage:
  - **astro-ph**: Astrophysics (Galaxies, Cosmology, Earth/Planetary, High Energy, Instrumentation, Solar/Stellar)
  - **cond-mat**: Condensed Matter (Disordered Systems, Materials, Mesoscale/Nano, Quantum Gases, Soft Matter, Statistical Mechanics, Strongly Correlated Electrons, Superconductivity)
  - **gr-qc**: General Relativity and Quantum Cosmology
  - **hep-ex**: High Energy Physics - Experiment
  - **hep-lat**: High Energy Physics - Lattice
  - **hep-ph**: High Energy Physics - Phenomenology
  - **hep-th**: High Energy Physics - Theory
  - **math-ph**: Mathematical Physics
  - **nucl-ex**: Nuclear Experiment
  - **nucl-th**: Nuclear Theory
  - **physics**: General Physics (Accelerator, Applied, Atomic, Biological, Chemical, Classical, Computational, Data Analysis, Fluid Dynamics, Geophysics, History, Instrumentation, Medical, Optics, Plasma, Space)
  - **quant-ph**: Quantum Physics
- Access: Free
- Note: Articles are not peer-reviewed by arXiv

#### INSPIRE-HEP
**Website:** https://inspirehep.net/
**Description:** Information system for High Energy Physics.
- Maintained by: CERN, DESY, Fermilab, SLAC
- Coverage: 1+ million papers, 1.2M authors, 30M citations
- Specializes in: Particle physics, astrophysics, related fields
- Features: Author disambiguation, citation analysis, job listings
- Access: Free
- History: Successor to SPIRES-HEP (Stanford)

#### NASA ADS (Astrophysics Data System)
**Website:** https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/
**Description:** Digital library for astronomy and astrophysics.
- Maintained by: Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory under NASA contract
- Coverage: 15+ million records
- Content: Astronomy, astrophysics, physics publications, all arXiv e-prints
- Features: Full-text search, citation tracking, author profiles
- Access: Free
- History: Founded in 1993, one of the first digital libraries

### 2.3 Emerging & Alternative Databases

#### Dimensions
**Website:** https://www.dimensions.ai/
**Description:** Modern research data platform linking publications, grants, patents.
- Coverage: 200+ million publications
- Features: Links publications to funding, patents, clinical trials
- Access: Free limited search; subscription for full features

#### Lens.org

**Description:** Open global innovation and research platform.
- Coverage: 200+ million scholarly records, 100+ million patents
- Features: Links scholarly works to patents (PatCite), biological sequences (PatSeq)
- Data sources: Microsoft Academic, PubMed, Crossref, OpenAlex
- Access: Free

## 6. IMPACT FACTORS & RANKINGS

### Journal Citation Reports (JCR)
**Website:** https://jcr.clarivate.com/
**Provider:** Clarivate Analytics
**Description:** Official source of journal impact factors.
- Published annually (typically June)
- Includes: Journal Impact Factor (JIF), 5-year JIF, Immediacy Index, Cited Half-life
- Categories: Physics, Applied; Physics, Atomic, Molecular & Chemical; Physics, Condensed Matter; Physics, Fluids & Plasmas; Physics, Mathematical; Physics, Multidisciplinary; Physics, Nuclear; Physics, Particles & Fields
- Access: Subscription required

### Scimago Journal Rank (SJR)
**Website:** https://www.scimagojr.com/
**Provider:** Scimago Lab
**Description:** Portal for journal and country scientific indicators.
- Metrics: SJR indicator, H-index, Citable documents
- Coverage: 30,000+ journals
- Features: Subject area and category rankings
- Access: Free

### CiteScore (Elsevier)
**Website:** https://scopus.com/sources
**Provider:** Elsevier/Scopus
**Description:** Alternative journal metric based on Scopus data.
- Calculation: Citations in 4 years / Documents in 4 years
- Updated: Monthly
- Access: Free (limited)

### Eigenfactor & Article Influence
**Website:** http://www.eigenfactor.org/
**Description:** Alternative metrics using network analysis.
- Eigenfactor Score: Measures total importance of journal
- Article Influence Score: Measures average influence per article
- Access: Free
