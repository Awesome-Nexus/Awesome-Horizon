## Key Observations

1. **Ethiopia** has the most developed physics ecosystem with an active Physical Society, extensive ISP support, and strong diaspora engagement through EPSNA.

2. **Kenya** has significant international collaboration through CERN partnerships and computational physics initiatives.

3. **Tanzania** shows strong growth in STEM outreach, particularly through robotics programs funded by international partners.

4. **Uganda** has multiple strong physics departments with notable women in physics leadership (Dr. Saphina Biira).

5. **Rwanda** hosts the premier regional physics research institute (ICTP-EAIFR), positioning it as a hub for fundamental research.

6. **Burundi** has limited documented physics infrastructure but maintains university programs.

7. **Somalia** is rebuilding physics education after prolonged conflict, with Somali National University leading efforts.
