# Major Physics Research Centers in Japan

## Executive Summary

Japan is home to world-class physics research institutions spanning fundamental particle physics, nuclear physics, plasma/fusion research, astronomy, condensed matter physics, and applied physics. This document provides comprehensive information about the major research centers, universities, and facilities across Japan.

---

## 1. National Research Institutions

### 1.1 RIKEN (Institute of Physical and Chemical Research)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Full Name** | RIKEN - Institute of Physical and Chemical Research |
| **Japanese Name** | 理化学研究所 (Rikagaku Kenkyūsho) |
| **Type** | National Research and Development Agency |
| **Founded** | 1917 (initially as private foundation) |
| **Headquarters** | Wako, Saitama, Japan |
| **Website** | https://www.riken.jp/en/ |
| **Status** | Japan's largest comprehensive research institution |

**Overview:**
RIKEN is Japan's premier comprehensive research institution, renowned for high-quality research across diverse scientific disciplines. Under the 5th Mid- to Long-Term Plan (2025-), RIKEN operates with five research domains:
- Mathematical, Computational and Information Science Domain
- Life Science Domain
- Sustainability Science Domain
- Physical Science Domain
- Pioneering Sciences Domain

**Major Physics-Related Research Centers:**

| Center | Focus Area | Location |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Center for Emergent Matter Science (CEMS) | Condensed matter physics, quantum materials | Wako, Saitama |
| Center for Computational Science (R-CCS) | Supercomputing (Fugaku supercomputer) | Kobe, Hyogo |
| Interdisciplinary Theoretical and Mathematical Sciences Program (iTHEMS) | Theoretical physics, mathematics | Wako, Saitama |
| Nishina Center for Accelerator-Based Science | Nuclear physics, RI Beam Factory | Wako, Saitama |
| Center for Quantum Computing | Quantum information science | Wako, Saitama |

**Notable Facilities:**
- **Fugaku Supercomputer**: World's fastest supercomputer (as of 2020-2023)
- **RI Beam Factory (RIBF)**: World-leading heavy ion accelerator facility
- **SPring-8**: Synchrotron radiation facility (jointly operated with JASRI)

### 1.5 NIFS (National Institute for Fusion Science)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Full Name** | National Institute for Fusion Science |
| **Japanese Name** | 核融合科学研究所 (Kakuyūgō Kagaku Kenkyūsho) |
| **Type** | National Research Institute (under NINS) |
| **Founded** | May 29, 1989 |
| **Location** | Toki, Gifu Prefecture, Japan |
| **Website** | https://www.nifs.ac.jp/en/ |
| **Status** | World-leading fusion research institute |

**Overview:**
NIFS pursues everlasting energy from seawater through fusion research. It operates as an inter-university research institute leading the world's fusion research.

**Major Research Units:**

| Unit | Research Focus |
|------|----------------|
| **Large Helical Device (LHD) Project** | Heliotron fusion confinement experiments |
| **Plasma Turbulence Processes** | Plasma turbulence and transport |
| **Plasma Quantum Processes** | Quantum processes in plasmas |
| **Fusion Engineering** | Reactor engineering, materials |
| **Theory and Data Analysis** | Plasma theory, simulation |

**Major Facilities:**

| Facility | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Large Helical Device (LHD)** | World's largest superconducting heliotron device (operated 1998-2025) |
| **LHD Experiment** | Longest-running continuous fusion experiment (completed Dec 2025) |
| **Compact Helical System (CHS)** | Smaller confinement device |
| **Rokkasho Research Center** | Fusion materials, tritium handling |

**Key Achievements:**
- Longest plasma confinement time in a stellarator device
- Extensive contributions to ITER project
- Development of helical confinement concept
