# Physics in Spain: A Comprehensive Overview

Spain is a major European physics research nation with a rich scientific heritage dating back to the early 20th century. The country has developed a robust ecosystem of universities, research centers, and international collaborations that place it at the forefront of several cutting-edge fields in physics. This document provides a detailed overview of Spain's physics landscape.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Real Sociedad Española de Física (RSEF)

The **Spanish Royal Physics Society (RSEF)** is the premier professional organization for physicists in Spain.

**History and Foundation:**
- Founded on **January 23, 1903** as the Spanish Royal Society of Physics and Chemistry (RSEFQ) at the Universidad Central de Madrid
- Split into independent physics and chemistry societies in **1980**
- First president after separation: Carlos Sánchez del Río y Sierra (1980-1984)
- Granted royal charter by King Alfonso XIII in **1928**

**Mission and Objectives:**
- Promote and develop basic knowledge of physics and its applications
- Encourage scientific research and teaching of physics at all educational levels
- Foster international relations with Iberoamerican physics societies
- Represent Spanish physics in European and global contexts

**Structure:**
- **Divisions** (created 2017): Condensed Matter Division and other specialized areas
- **Specialized Groups**: Focus on specific research areas
- **Local Sections**: Regional groupings across Spain
- **Foreign Section**: For international members

**International Affiliations:**
- Member of the **European Physical Society**
- Member of the **Federation of Iberoamerican Physics**
- Member of the **Confederation of Scientific Societies of Spain (COSCE)**
- Cooperative agreements with the **American Physical Society** and **Portuguese Physical Society**

**Publications:**
- *Revista Española de Física*: Articles of general interest and society news
- *Boletín Informativo*: Monthly newsletter for members
- Previously published *Anales de Física* (merged into European Physical Journal in 1998)

**Major Events:**
- **RSEF Physics Biennial**: Biannual conference bringing together the Spanish physics community
- XXXIX Biennial held in Donostia/San Sebastián (July 15-19, 2024)

**Awards (with Fundación BBVA):**
- RSEF Medal (highest honor)
- Best Doctoral Thesis Awards
- Multiple prizes recognizing research excellence

**Current President:** Luis Viña Liste (since 2021)

## 2. Major University Physics Departments

### Universidad Autónoma de Madrid (UAM)

The **Autonomous University of Madrid** hosts one of Spain's strongest physics departments.

**Key Research Centers:**

#### IFIMAC - Condensed Matter Physics Center
- **"María de Maeztu" Excellence Research Unit** since 2014
- World-renowned research in:
  - Advanced Materials
  - First Principles Simulations and Modelling
  - Nanophysics
  - Nano and Quantum Optics
  - Soft Condensed Matter and Biophysics
- Offers junior group leader positions for young scientists
- Provides up to €200,000 research funding for selected positions

**Physics Department Highlights:**
- Strong focus on condensed matter physics
- Collaboration with the Institute for Theoretical Physics (IFT-CSIC/UAM)
- Active in quantum materials and nanoscience research

### Universidad Complutense de Madrid (UCM)

The **Complutense University of Madrid** is one of Spain's oldest and most prestigious universities with a rich physics tradition.

**Historical Significance:**
- Formerly Universidad Central de Madrid
- Where **Blas Cabrera** (father of Spanish physics) held the chair of Electricity and Magnetism (1905)
- Home to the historic Laboratorio de Investigaciones Físicas

**Current Research Strengths:**
- Condensed matter physics
- Particle physics
- Theoretical physics
- Biophysics

### Universitat de Barcelona (UB)

The **University of Barcelona** has a distinguished physics department with broad research expertise.

**Key Research Areas:**
- Quantum optics and quantum information
- Statistical physics
- Particle physics
- Astrophysics

**Notable Collaboration:**
- Faculty members serve as Associated Personnel at **IFAE**
- Agreement with IFAE dating back to 1992

### Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona (UAB)

The **Autonomous University of Barcelona** is the primary academic partner of IFAE.

**Key Features:**
- IFAE located on UAB campus in Bellaterra
- Strong partnership in high-energy physics research
- Joint doctoral programs in physics
- Home to the **Institute of Cosmos Sciences (ICCUB)**

### Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC)

The **Polytechnic University of Catalonia** has significant strengths in applied physics.

**Key Partnerships:**
- Co-founder of **ICFO** (Institute of Photonic Sciences)
- Joint Master's in Photonics program
- Joint PhD programs in photonics
- Strong engineering physics orientation

### University of Granada (UGR)

The **University of Granada** hosts significant physics research, particularly in astrophysics.

**Research Strengths:**
- Theoretical physics
- Particle physics
- Astrophysics (close ties with IAA-CSIC)

### Other Notable University Physics Departments

- **Universidad de Valencia (UV)**: Strong in particle physics (IFIC-CSIC/UV joint center)
- **Universidad de Zaragoza**: Astrophysics and high-energy physics
- **Universidad del País Vasco (UPV/EHU)**: Materials physics, quantum optics
- **Universidad de Salamanca**: Theoretical physics, astrophysics
- **Universidad de Alicante**: Astrophysics, condensed matter
- **Universidad de Cádiz**: Applied physics

## 3. National Research Centers

### ICFO - Institute of Photonic Sciences

**Overview:**
- **Established:** 2002
- **Location:** Castelldefels, Barcelona (Mediterranean Technology Park)
- **Type:** CERCA research center, member of Barcelona Institute of Science and Technology (BIST)
- **Staff:** 400+ researchers
- **Research Groups:** 26 groups in 60+ laboratories
- **Director:** Oriol Romero-Isart

**Research Areas:**

1. **Nonlinear Photonics**
   - Ultrafast optics
   - Attosecond science
   - Extreme nonlinear optics

2. **Quantum Photonics**
   - Quantum information technologies
   - Quantum communication systems
   - Quantum sensors

3. **Nanophotonics**
   - Nanophotonic devices
   - 2D materials (graphene, TMDs)
   - Nano-cavities and nano-antennas

4. **Biophotonics**
   - Super-resolution microscopy (STORM, STED)
   - Medical optics
   - Biomedical imaging

**Major Facilities:**
- Nanophotonics Fabrication Lab
- Super-Resolution Light Microscopy & Nanoscopy (SLN) Facility
- Advanced Engineering Lab
- Attoscience facility with UV to mid-IR laser sources

**Education Programs:**
- Master in Photonics (MSc) - joint with UPC, UAB, UB
- Erasmus Mundus Master Course (EUROPHOTONICS)

**Spin-off Companies:**

**Awards:**
- Optica Diversity & Inclusion Advocacy Recognition (2021)

### IFAE - Institute for High Energy Physics

**Overview:**
- **Established:** July 16, 1991
- **Location:** UAB Campus, Bellaterra, Barcelona
- **Type:** Public Consortium between Generalitat de Catalunya and UAB
- **Status:** Severo Ochoa Center of Excellence (2012, 2017, 2024)
- **Staff:** 100+ researchers (postdocs, PhD students, permanent staff)

**Research Areas:**

1. **Particle Physics (High Energy Physics)**
   - ATLAS experiment at CERN LHC
   - Neutrino physics (T2K, NOvA experiments)
   - Beyond Standard Model searches
   - Higgs boson studies

2. **Astrophysics and Cosmology**
   - Gamma-ray astronomy (MAGIC telescopes)
   - Dark energy research (DESI)
   - Gravitational wave astronomy (LIGO, Virgo collaborations since 2024)
   - Cherenkov Telescope Array (CTA)

3. **Applied Physics**
   - Medical imaging (X-ray detectors)
   - Quantum computing

**Major Projects:**

**ATLAS at CERN:**
- Member since 1992
- Contributed to Higgs boson discovery (2012)
- Developed 3D silicon pixel sensors for Inner Detector
- Operating Tier-1 computing center at PIC
- Leading detector upgrades for High-Luminosity LHC

**MAGIC Telescopes:**
- Located at Roque de los Muchachos Observatory, La Palma
- Operating since 2004
- First detection of very-high-energy gamma rays from a gamma-ray burst (2019)
- IFAE led camera design and construction

**CTA - Cherenkov Telescope Array:**
- Co-leading construction of Large-Sized Telescopes (LSTs)
- Ten times more sensitive than MAGIC

**Facilities:**
- Microelectronics Clean Room (unique in Spain for chip packaging)
- Instrumentation Clean Room
- Shielded Room for detector testing
- Quantum Computing Technologies Lab (3 dilution refrigerators)
- Mechanical workshop with CNC machines
- Data center

## 4. Major Research Facilities

### Observatories and Telescopes

#### Roque de los Muchachos Observatory (La Palma, Canary Islands)

**Location:** La Palma, Canary Islands
**Status:** One of the world's premier astronomical observatories

**Major Telescopes:**

**Gran Telescopio Canarias (GTC):**
- **Type:** 10.4m segmented mirror optical-infrared telescope
- **Status:** World's largest single-aperture optical telescope
- **Operation:** Since 2007
- **Managed by:** Instituto de Astrofísica de Canarias (IAC)

**MAGIC Telescopes:**
- **Type:** Two 17m diameter Imaging Atmospheric Cherenkov Telescopes
- **Energy Range:** 50 GeV - 50 TeV
- **Operation:** Since 2004 (MAGIC-I), 2009 (stereoscopic)
- **Managed by:** MAGIC Collaboration (IFAE is key member)

**Cherenkov Telescope Array (CTA) - North:**
- **Type:** Next-generation gamma-ray observatory
- **Sensitivity:** 10x better than MAGIC
- **Location:** Under construction at Roque de los Muchachos
- **First Light:** LST-1 achieved 11σ detection of Crab Nebula

**Other Major Telescopes:**
- William Herschel Telescope (4.2m)
- Isaac Newton Telescope
- Nordic Optical Telescope
- Liverpool Telescope
- Swedish Solar Telescope

#### Calar Alto Observatory (Almería)

**Managed by:** IAA-CSIC (jointly with Max-Planck Institute)

**Telescopes:**
- 3.5m telescope (largest in continental Western Europe)
- 2.2m telescope
- 1.23m telescope
- CARMENES spectrograph for exoplanet hunting

#### Sierra Nevada Observatory (Granada)

**Managed by:** IAA-CSIC

**Telescopes:**
- 1.5m telescope
- 0.9m telescope

### Computing and Data Facilities

#### PIC - Port d'Informació Científica

**Overview:**
- **Founded:** 2003 (by IFAE and CIEMAT)
- **Location:** UAB Campus, Bellaterra
- **Status:** Spain's sole LHC Tier-1 data center
- **Role:** World Wide LHC Computing Grid (WLCG) Tier-1 center

**Capabilities:**
- Processes petabytes of LHC data annually
- 24/7 operation with top-tier reliability
- MAGIC Data Center (300 TB/year)
- Supports ATLAS, CMS, LHCb experiments

## 5. Notable Physicists

### Historical Figures

#### Blas Cabrera y Felipe (1878-1945)

**Recognition:** "Father of Spanish Physics"

**Key Achievements:**
- Founded modern experimental physics in Spain
- Pioneer in magnetism and magnetic properties of matter
- Director of Laboratorio de Investigaciones Físicas (1910)
- President of Real Sociedad Española de Física y Química (1916)
- Member of 6th Solvay Conference Scientific Committee (1930) - sponsored by Einstein and Marie Curie
- Foreign member of French Academy of Sciences (1928)
- Published 110 works between 1910-1934
- Modified Curie-Weiss law for magnetic susceptibility
- Introduced special relativity to Spain (1912)

**Legacy:**
- His work laid foundation for MRI technology
- Influenced development of quantum mechanics
- Host to Albert Einstein during 1923 Spain visit

#### Miguel A. Catalán (1894-1957)

**Achievements:**
- Spectroscopist who discovered multiplets in Mn I spectrum
- Leading figure at Instituto Nacional de Física y Química
- Co-founder with Cabrera of modern Spanish physics institutions

### Contemporary Physicists

#### Pablo Jarillo-Herrero (b. 1976)

**Affiliation:** MIT (Spanish origin from Valencia)

**Key Achievement:**
- Pioneer in **twistronics** - study of twisted bilayer graphene
- Discovered superconductivity in magic-angle graphene (2018)
- Wolf Prize in Physics (2020)
- Buckley Prize (2020)
- Lise Meitner Medal (2021)

## 6. Physics Research Areas of Strength

### Photonics and Quantum Optics

Spain has emerged as a **global leader in photonics**, primarily through ICFO:

**Strengths:**
- Quantum photonics and quantum information
- Nanophotonics and 2D materials
- Attosecond science and ultrafast optics
- Biophotonics and medical imaging
- Nonlinear photonics

**Applications:**
- Quantum technologies
- Solar energy harvesting
- Medical diagnostics
- Optical communications

### High-Energy Physics

Spain has a **strong tradition in particle physics**:

**Strengths:**
- LHC physics (ATLAS, CMS, ALICE collaborations)
- Neutrino physics (T2K, NOvA)
- Flavor physics (LHCb)
- Heavy-ion physics (quark-gluon plasma)
- Detector technology development

### Gravitational Wave Astronomy

Spain is positioning itself as a **major player in gravitational wave science**:

**REDONGRA Network:**
- 20+ research groups across Spain
- Coordination of national efforts
- Participation in LIGO, Virgo, LISA, Einstein Telescope

**Research Areas:**
- Data analysis for ground-based detectors
- Space-based gravitational wave detection (LISA)
- Numerical relativity
- Multi-messenger astronomy

### Astrophysics and Cosmology

**Key Strengths:**
- Gamma-ray astronomy (MAGIC, CTA)
- Exoplanet research (CARMENES)
- Dark energy studies (DESI)
- Solar physics (Solar Orbiter)
- Star formation and stellar evolution

### Condensed Matter Physics

**Major Centers:** IFIMAC (UAM), various CSIC institutes

**Research Areas:**
- Quantum materials
- 2D materials (graphene, TMDs)
- Superconductivity
- Magnetism and spintronics
- Strongly correlated electron systems

### Quantum Technologies

**Growing National Priority:**
- Quantum computing (IFAE, ICFO)
- Quantum communication
- Quantum sensors
- Catalonia Quantum Academy network

### Medical Physics

**Applications:**
- Advanced X-ray detectors (IFAE)

## Summary and Future Outlook

Spain's physics ecosystem demonstrates:

1. **Strong institutional framework** with well-funded research centers (ICFO, IFAE, IAA-CSIC)
2. **Excellence recognition** through multiple Severo Ochoa centers
3. **International leadership** in photonics, high-energy physics, and astrophysics
4. **Growing presence** in gravitational wave astronomy and quantum technologies
5. **Robust educational pipeline** with strong university physics departments
6. **Active international collaboration** at CERN, ESA, and other major facilities

**Emerging Priorities:**
- Quantum technologies development
- Einstein Telescope preparation
- LISA mission contributions
- Advanced detector technologies
- Multi-messenger astronomy

Spain continues to strengthen its position as a major European physics research nation, with particular strengths in photonics, particle physics, and astrophysics.
