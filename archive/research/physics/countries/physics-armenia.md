# Physics Research Centers and Universities in Armenia

## Executive Summary

Armenia has a rich tradition in physics research dating back to the early 20th century, with significant developments during the Soviet era and continued growth in the post-independence period. The country boasts several world-class research institutions, universities with strong physics programs, and notable international collaborations, particularly in areas such as astrophysics, elementary particle physics, laser physics, and theoretical physics.

### Historical Development

The training of mathematical physicists at Yerevan State University began in **1922**. Key milestones include:

- **1933**: Establishment of the Physics and Mathematics Faculty with separate departments
- **1959**: Separation of the Faculty of Physics from the Faculty of Physics and Mathematics; first dean was Norayr Kocharyan, Corresponding Member of the Academy of Sciences
- **1975**: With assistance from Academician Victor Hambardzumyan, the Faculty of Radiophysics separated from the Faculty of Physics; founder and first dean was Professor Yu. L. Vardanyan
- **2022**: Formation of the **YSU Institute of Physics** by merging the Faculty of Physics and Faculty of Radiophysics to address the need for modern physics specialists

### Key Research Areas

- Elementary particle physics
- Nuclear physics
- Cosmic ray physics and astrophysics
- Theoretical physics
- Condensed matter physics
- Radiobiology
- Isotope investigation and production

### Major Achievements

#### Early Years
- Discovery of protons and neutrons in cosmic rays
- First evidence of particles with masses between muons and protons

#### Cosmic Ray Division (CRD)
- Discovery of sharp knee in light components of primary cosmic rays
- Detection of highest energy protons accelerated on the Sun
- Creation of **Aragats Space Environmental Center (2000)** for solar-terrestrial connection studies

#### Accelerator Physics
- Operation of 6 GeV electron synchrotron (1967-1991)
- Significant results in hadronic properties of photons, nucleon resonance structures

#### Gamma-Ray Astronomy
- Development of stereoscopic approach in Very High Energy gamma-ray astronomy using IACT
- Participation in HEGRA, MAGIC (Canary Islands), and H.E.S.S. (Namibia)

### S. Matinyan Center for Theoretical Physics

#### Research Focus
- Physics of elementary particles
- Quantum field theory
- Quantum gravity and string theory
- Condensed matter physics
- Statistical physics
- Interdisciplinary fields (biophysics, mathematical physics)

## 6. Institute of Chemical Physics (Named after A.B. Nalbandyan)

### Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Founded** | 1960 (as Laboratory), 1975 (as Institute) |
| **Named After** | Academician A.B. Nalbandyan (1993) |
| **Website** | [https://ichph.am/](https://ichph.am/) |

### Research Areas

| Area | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Chemical Kinetics | Theory of chemical transformations, chain reactions |
| Combustion | Combustion and ignition processes in gases and condensed phases |
| Self-propagating High-temperature Synthesis (SHS) | Production of refractory inorganic compounds |
| Catalysis | Biomimetic catalysts, nanocatalysis |
| Nanomaterials | Nanosized powders, nanophotonics |

### Key Achievements

- Development of kinetic method of radical freezing with EPR spectroscopy
- Discovery of chain carriers (peroxyl radicals) in degenerate branched chain reactions
- First synthesis of transition metal hydrides during combustion
- Over **1,600 scientific articles** published
- **17 monographs** published
