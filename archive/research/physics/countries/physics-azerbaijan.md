# Physics Institutions and Resources in Azerbaijan

## Executive Summary

Azerbaijan has a well-established physics research and education infrastructure, with roots dating back to the Soviet era. The country's physics landscape is anchored by the **Azerbaijan National Academy of Sciences (ANAS) Institute of Physics**, founded in 1945, and several major universities including **Baku State University**, **Azerbaijan Technical University**, and **Khazar University**. The nation maintains active international collaborations, notably with **CERN** since 1997, and participates in high-energy physics research through the **ATLAS experiment**.

## 2. Azerbaijan National Academy of Sciences - Institute of Physics

### Overview
- **Full Name:** G.M. Abdullayev Institute of Physics
- **Founded:** 1945
- **Location:** 131 H. Javid Avenue, Baku, AZ-1143
- **Website:** http://www.physics.gov.az/
- **Current Status:** Since 2023, operates under the Ministry of Science and Education of Azerbaijan

### Leadership
- **Director:** Academician Nazim Mammadov (Academician-Secretary of the Department of Physical-Mathematical and Technical Sciences)

### Research Structure

The Institute is organized into four main sectors:

| Sector | Laboratories | Staff | Key Research Areas |
|--------|--------------|-------|-------------------|
| **General Sector** | 9 laboratories + 1 department | 128 employees (3 academicians, 15 doctors of science, 50 PhDs) | Fundamental physics research |
| **Innovation Sector** | 17 laboratories | ~200 researchers | Applied physics, ATLAS/CERN collaboration |
| **Interdisciplinary Sector** | 9 laboratories | - | Cross-disciplinary research |
| **Information Sector** | Multiple departments | - | Scientific computing, library, publishing |

### Major Research Areas

1. **Condensed Matter Physics**
   - Semiconductor physics
   - Solid-state physics
   - Nanoelectronics

2. **Optoelectronics**
   - Physical and technical foundations of optoelectronics
   - Electromagnetic wave sources and receivers
   - Quantum electronics

3. **Nuclear and High Energy Physics**
   - Elementary particle physics
   - CERN ATLAS collaboration
   - Grid computing for high-energy physics

4. **Energy Research**
   - Renewable energy conversion
   - Energy converters and photovoltaic systems

### National GRID Information and Computing Centre

- **Established:** 2008
- **Partners:** CERN, JINR (Joint Institute for Nuclear Research), AzScienceNet
- **Infrastructure:**
  - High-productive blade Supermicro servers
  - IBM blade servers
  - Cloud computing and storage systems
- **Purpose:** Processing big data for CERN collaborations and high-performance computing

### Publications
- **Journal:** "AJP Fizika" (Azerbaijan Journal of Physics) - published in both Azerbaijani and English
- **Frequency:** Quarterly
- **Coverage:** All areas of physics research
