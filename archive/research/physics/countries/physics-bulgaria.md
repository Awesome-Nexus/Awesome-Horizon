#### Institute for Nuclear Research and Nuclear Energy (INRNE)

**Address:** 72 Tsarigradsko Chaussee Blvd., 1784 Sofia

**Research Areas:**
- Theory of elementary particles and string theory
- Experimental high energy physics
- Gamma-astrophysics at ultra-high energies
- Nuclear reactions and structure
- Neutron physics and nuclear reactors
- Dosimetry and radiation safety
- Radioecology and environmental monitoring
- Radiochemistry

**Key Facilities:**
- Research reactor IR-T
- National Cyclotron Center (under construction)
- Radioactive waste repository (transferred to State Enterprise in 2006)

**Educational Programs:**
- PhD in Theoretical and Mathematical Physics
- PhD in Nuclear Physics
- PhD in Neutron Physics and Nuclear Reactors
- PhD in Physics of High Energies
- PhD in Radiochemistry

**International Collaborations:**
- CERN (CMS, ALICE experiments)
- JINR Dubna
- ASACUSA collaboration at CERN

**Website:** www.inrne.bas.bg

#### Georgi Nadjakov Institute of Solid State Physics (ISSP)

**Address:** 72 Tsarigradsko Chaussee Blvd., 1784 Sofia

**Research Directions:**
- Microelectronics and acoustoelectronics
- Physics of superconductors and low temperatures
- Optics and spectroscopy
- Laser physics
- Physics of condensed matter
- Physics of atoms, molecules, and plasma

**Museum of History of Physics:**
- Founded by Georgi Nadjakov
- EPS Historic Site since 2014
- Permanent exhibition on Georgi Nadjakov's work

**Website:** www.issp.bas.bg

#### Institute of Astronomy with National Astronomical Observatory

**Address:** 72 Tsarigradsko Chaussee Blvd., 1784 Sofia

**Observatories:**
1. **National Astronomical Observatory - Rozhen**
   - Location: Rhodope Mountains, 1750 m altitude
   - Opened: 1981
   - Telescopes:
     - 2m Ritchey-Chretien-Coude reflector (Carl Zeiss Jena)
     - 50/70 cm Schmidt camera
     - 60 cm Cassegrain telescope
     - 15 cm Lyot coronagraph (solar)
     - 1.5m ASA AZ1500 robotic telescope (2023)

2. **Astronomical Observatory - Belogradchik**
   - Location: 650 m altitude, northwest Bulgaria
   - Established: 1961
   - 60 cm Cassegrain telescope

**Research Departments:**
- Sun and Solar System
- Stars and Stellar Systems
- Galaxies and Cosmology

**Doctoral Programs:**
- Astrophysics and Stellar Astronomy
- Astronomy and Celestial Mechanics
- Heliophysics

**International Projects:**
- LOFAR (Low-Frequency Array)
- OPTICON network
- STELLAR consortium (EU Horizon 2020)
- Wide-Field Plate Database

**Website:** astro.bas.bg

## 6. International Collaborations

### 6.1 CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)

**Membership:** Full Member State since June 18, 1999

**Bulgarian Contribution:**
| Experiment | Role | Bulgarian Institutions |
|------------|------|----------------------|
| **CMS (Compact Muon Solenoid)** | Major contributor since 1999; Hadron Calorimeter (HCAL); Resistive Plate Chambers (RPC); Gas Electron Multiplier (GEM) | INRNE, Sofia University |
| **ALICE** | Official member since 2021; FoCal detector development | INRNE, Sofia University, Plovdiv University |
| **ATLAS** | Participation through JINR groups | Various |
| **ISOLDE** | Nuclear physics experiments | INRNE |
| **PADME** | Search for new light particles; key proponent | INRNE |

**Key Facts:**
- Bulgaria became CERN's 20th Member State in 1999
- Cooperation began in the 1980s with L3 experiment at LEP
- ~30 experimental high-energy physicists at INRNE
- ~35 theoretical physicists at INRNE
- ~10 theoretical and 9 experimental physicists at Sofia University

### 6.2 Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR), Dubna

**Status:** Founding member state (1956)

**Collaboration Areas:**
- Theoretical physics
- Experimental nuclear physics
- Particle physics
- Heavy ion physics
- Neutron physics

**Notable Achievements:**
- Bulgarian scientists trained at JINR since 1956
- Plenipotentiary Representative tradition
- Active in Dubna-based experiments
