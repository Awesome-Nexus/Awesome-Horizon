# Physics in Bangladesh: A Comprehensive Research Report

## Executive Summary

Bangladesh has a rich legacy in physics education and research, dating back to the early 20th century. The country boasts prestigious institutions like the University of Dhaka (where Satyendra Nath Bose made his groundbreaking contributions), robust research organizations such as the Bangladesh Atomic Energy Commission, and active professional societies like the Bangladesh Physical Society. This report documents the major universities, research institutions, notable physicists, and physics-related activities in Bangladesh.

## 5. Notable Bangladeshi Physicists

### 5.1 Satyendra Nath Bose (1894-1974)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Born** | January 1, 1894, Calcutta, India |
| **Died** | February 4, 1974 |
| **Affiliation** | University of Dhaka (1921-1945) |

**Major Contributions:**
- Developed **Bose-Einstein statistics** (1924)
- Discovered the concept of **bosons** (particles named after him by Paul Dirac)
- Laid foundation for **Bose-Einstein condensate**
- Worked with Albert Einstein on quantum theory

**Legacy at Dhaka University:**
- Joined as Reader in Physics in 1921
- Established new departments, laboratories, and libraries
- Made Head of Department in 1926 (on Einstein's recommendation)
- Served as Dean of Faculty of Science until 1945

**Honors:**
- Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS, 1958)
- Padma Vibhushan (1954)
- National Professor of India (1959-1974)

---

### 5.2 Jamal Nazrul Islam (1939-2013)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Born** | February 24, 1939, Jhenaidah |
| **Died** | March 16, 2013 |
| **Institution** | University of Chittagong |

**Education:**
- BSc (Hons) from Calcutta University
- BA/MA from Trinity College, Cambridge
- PhD (1964) and DSc (1982) from Cambridge

**Career:**
- Worked at Cambridge Institute of Theoretical Astronomy (1967-1971)
- California Institute of Technology (1971-1972)
- University of Washington (1972-1973)
- King's College London (1973-1974)
- City University London (1978-1984)
- Returned to Bangladesh in 1984
- Founded Research Centre for Mathematical and Physical Sciences (RCMPS) at CU in 1989

**Research Areas:**
- General relativity and black holes
- Mathematical cosmology
- Quantum field theory in curved spacetime
- Rotating fields in general relativity

**Major Publications:**
- *The Ultimate Fate of the Universe* (Cambridge, 1983)
- *Rotating Fields in General Relativity* (Cambridge, 1985)
- *An Introduction to Mathematical Cosmology* (Cambridge, 1992)
- *Krishna Bibor* (Black Hole) in Bengali

**Awards:**
- Bangladesh Academy of Sciences Gold Medal (1985)
- Ekushey Padak (2000)
- Bangladesh National Award for Science and Technology (1998)
- Razzak-Shamsun Lifetime Achievement Award (2011)
- Independence Award (2025, posthumous)
- Fellow of Third World Academy of Sciences (1985)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|

| **Type** | National physics competition |
| **Website** | [www.bdpho.org](https://www.bdpho.org) |
| **Facebook** | [Bangladesh Physics Olympiad](https://www.facebook.com/BdPhO/) |

### Competition Structure

**Categories:**
- Category A: Class 5-6
- Category B: Class 7-8
- Category C: Class 9-10
- Category D: Class 11-12

### Selection Process

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Regional Round** | Open to all students; held at designated centers across the country |
| **National Round** | Top performers from regional round; rigorous written exam |
| **Training Camp** | Intensive training for selected students; theoretical lectures and problem-solving |
| **International Competitions** | Team selection for IPhO and APhO |

### Recent Statistics
- **14th BdPhO (2024)**: ~10,000 students participated in regional round; 1,130 selected for national round at AIUB
- **16th BdPhO (2026)**: National round held in Chittagong

- **National Team Coach**: Prof. Dr. M Arshad Momen

### International Achievements

## 11. Challenges and Opportunities

### Challenges
1. Limited research funding
2. Brain drain to developed countries
3. Limited access to cutting-edge equipment
4. Need for stronger industry-academia collaboration
5. Gender disparity in physics (though improving)

### Opportunities
1. Growing government interest in science and technology
2. Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant creating demand for nuclear physicists
3. Renewable energy sector growth
4. Increasing international collaborations
5. Bangladesh Physics Olympiad nurturing young talent

## 13. Conclusion

Bangladesh has established a solid foundation in physics education and research over the past century. From Satyendra Nath Bose's pioneering work in quantum statistics to contemporary research in nuclear physics, condensed matter, and cosmology, Bangladeshi physicists have made significant contributions. The country's network of universities, research institutions like BAEC, professional societies like BPS, and the growing Bangladesh Physics Olympiad ecosystem provide a comprehensive framework for physics development. With continued investment in research infrastructure and international collaboration, Bangladesh is positioned to make even greater contributions to the global physics community.
