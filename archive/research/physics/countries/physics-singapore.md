# Physics Institutions and Research in Singapore

## Overview

Singapore has emerged as one of Asia's leading research hubs for physics, with strategic investments in quantum technologies, 2D materials, photonics, and advanced computing. The city-state's physics ecosystem is characterized by strong government support through agencies like A*STAR and the National Research Foundation, world-class universities, and a growing network of international collaborations.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Institute of Physics Singapore (IPS)

The **Institute of Physics Singapore** is the professional body representing physicists in Singapore. It serves as the primary organization for:

- **Professional Development**: Organizing seminars, workshops, and conferences for physics professionals
- **Student Engagement**: Supporting physics students through the NUS Sigma Pi Sigma Chapter and student chapters at various institutions
- **Outreach Activities**: Promoting physics education and public understanding of science
- **Industry Connections**: Bridging academia and industry for applied physics applications

The IPS works closely with the Institute of Physics (UK) and other international physics societies to facilitate knowledge exchange and professional networking.

## 2. Major University Physics Departments

### National University of Singapore (NUS) - Department of Physics

**Founded**: 1904 (originally as part of the Straits Settlements Medical School)

**Location**: 2 Science Drive 3, Blk S12, Level 2, Singapore 117551

**Overview**: The Department of Physics at NUS has transformed from primarily a teaching department into a research-intensive institution. It was ranked 25th in the QS World University Rankings for Physics and Astronomy (2017-2018).

**Major Research Areas**:
1. **Physics of Nanoscience** - Low-dimensional nanostructures, nanoelectronics, quantum dots
2. **Condensed Matter and Advanced Materials** - Fuel cells, hydrogen storage, spintronics, multiferroics
3. **Biological Physics** - Single molecule manipulation, force and fluorescence, complex fluids
4. **Physics of Nonlinear and Complex Systems** - Computational methods, thermal transport
5. **Atomic and Molecular Physics** - Nonlinear optics, laser spectroscopy
6. **Computational and Theoretical Physics** - String theory, cosmology, particle physics
7. **Quantum Information** - Quantum cryptography, quantum computation
8. **Graphene and 2D Materials** - 2D electron systems, van der Waals materials

**Key Facilities**:
- Centre for Ion Beam Applications (CIBA)
- Centre for Superconducting Magnetic Materials (CSMM)
- Surface Science Laboratory
- Laser Spectroscopy Laboratory
- High-performance computing clusters

**Faculty**: ~55 active research staff
**Graduate Students**: ~150 PhD and MSc students
**Annual Research Funding**: Exceeds S$15 million

**Notable Historical Note**: Nobel Laureate Paul A.M. Dirac visited the department, and his photo from that lecture is displayed in the departmental meeting room.

**Location**: 50 Nanyang Avenue, Singapore 639798

**Overview**: The Division of Physics and Applied Physics at NTU's School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences (SPMS) hosts a young and dynamic team of over 40 active faculty members engaged in cutting-edge research.

**Programs Offered**:

## 3. National Research Centers

### Centre for Quantum Technologies (CQT)

**Founded**: December 2007
**Location**: Block S15, 3 Science Drive 2, Singapore 117543 (NUS campus)
**Director**: Prof. José Ignacio Latorre (since July 2020)
**Founding Director**: Prof. Artur Ekert (2007-2020)

**Overview**: CQT is Singapore's flagship national research center for quantum technologies and one of the largest dedicated quantum research centers in the world. In January 2025, CQT was elevated to a **flagship national research center** under Singapore's National Quantum Strategy.

**Scale**:
- 260+ staff and students
- Research groups at NUS, NTU, SUTD, and A*STAR
- Research nodes across Singapore's quantum ecosystem

**Research Areas**:

**Computer Science**:
- Quantum algorithms
- Quantum networks
- Communication complexity
- Query complexity
- Interactive proofs and quantum games

**Interdisciplinary Theory**:
- Quantum entanglement
- Quantum computation and cryptography
- Quantum information theory
- Cold fermions

**Experimental Labs**:
- Quantum optics (entangled photon sources, quantum interfaces)
- Cold atoms and ions (atom chips, ion traps, optical lattices)
- Quantum cryptography and quantum hacking

**Key Achievements**:
- Published in top journals: Nature, Science, Physical Review Letters
- Multiple quantum technology spin-offs
- Pioneer in quantum key distribution research
- Home to world-leading quantum cryptography research

**Education**:
- Joint graduate studies program with NUS
- QCamp summer school for pre-university students
- National Quantum Scholarships Scheme (100 PhD + 100 Master's over 5 years)

**Website**: [www.quantumlah.org](https://www.quantumlah.org)

### National Quantum Office (NQO)

**Established**: April 2022
**Hosted by**: A*STAR
**Executive Director**: Mr. Ling Keok Tong

**Overview**: The NQO is Singapore's national platform to drive the development and implementation of the National Quantum Strategy. It coordinates quantum RIE (Research, Innovation, and Enterprise) activities across the country.

**National Programs Overseen**:

1. **National Quantum-Safe Network (NQSN)** - Quantum-safe communication technologies
2. **National Quantum Computing Hub (NQCH)** - Quantum software and applications
3. **National Quantum Federated Foundry (NQFF)** - Quantum device fabrication
4. **National Quantum Sensor Programme (NQSP)** - Launched August 2024
5. **National Quantum Processor Initiative (NQPI)** - Quantum processor design and manufacturing

**Website**: [nqo.sg](https://nqo.sg)

## 6. Physics Research Areas of Strength

### Quantum Technologies

Singapore is globally recognized as a leader in quantum research, with particular strength in:

**Quantum Cryptography and Communications**:
- World's first entanglement-based quantum cryptography protocol (Ekert, 1991)
- National Quantum-Safe Network (NQSN+) - nationwide quantum-safe network deployment
- Satellite-based quantum key distribution (SpeQtral)
- Space-QUEST quantum communication experiments

**Quantum Computing**:
- National Quantum Computing Hub
- Research across multiple platforms (ion traps, neutral atoms, superconducting qubits)
- Partnership with Quantinuum to host Helios quantum computer (2026)
- Focus on middleware and hybrid quantum-classical integration

**Quantum Sensing**:
- National Quantum Sensor Programme (launched August 2024)
- Applications in position, navigation, timing (PNT)
- Biomedical sensing and imaging
- Remote sensing

### 2D Materials and Graphene

**Key Strengths**:
- World's first isolation of graphene (Geim & Novoselov, Manchester, foundational work)
- Home to Nobel Laureate Sir Kostya Novoselov
- Centre for Advanced 2D Materials (CA2DM)
- Institute for Functional Intelligent Materials (IFIM)
- Van der Waals heterostructures research

**Applications**:
- Next-generation electronics
- Flexible displays
- Sensors and detectors
- Energy storage and conversion

### Photonics and Optics

**Research Areas**:
- Nonlinear optics
- Quantum optics

- Optical communications

### Condensed Matter Physics

**Strengths**:

### Biological Physics and Soft Matter

**Research Areas**:

### Computational and Theoretical Physics

## Summary

Singapore has established itself as a world-class hub for physics research, particularly in quantum technologies and 2D materials. With:

- **3 major universities** with strong physics programs
- **National Research Centre of Excellence** (CQT)
- **Dedicated National Quantum Office** coordinating strategy
- **S$300 million+ current investment** in quantum technologies
- **Nobel Laureate leadership** (Prof. Sir Kostya Novoselov)
- **Extensive international collaborations** spanning CERN, Europe, US, and Asia-Pacific

Singapore's physics ecosystem demonstrates how strategic investment, international partnerships, and talent development can create a vibrant research environment despite geographic and demographic constraints. The nation's focus on quantum technologies, materials science, and photonics positions it at the forefront of the "second quantum revolution" and next-generation technologies.
