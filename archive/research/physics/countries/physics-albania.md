# Physics in Albania: Comprehensive Research Report

## Overview

Albania has a developing physics research and education infrastructure with a network of public universities offering physics programs, specialized research institutes, and growing international collaborations, particularly with CERN. This report documents the physics landscape in Albania including universities, research centers, societies, notable physicists, and educational programs.

## 2. Research Institutes and Centers

### 2.1 Institute of Applied Nuclear Physics (Instituti i Fizikës Bërthamore të Zbatuar - IFBZ)

**Location:** Tirana, Albania  
**Address:** Rr. Muhamet Allushi, ex-Kinostudio, Tirana  
**Phone:** +355 4 245 1371  
**Website:** [https://unitir.edu.al/en/instituti-i-fizikes-berthamore/](https://unitir.edu.al/en/instituti-i-fizikes-berthamore/)

**History:**
- Established in 1971 as the Institute of Nuclear Physics
- Initially under the University of Tirana
- Later became part of the Academy of Sciences
- In 2007, re-established as Center of Applied Nuclear Physics
- In 2013, returned to University of Tirana as Institute of Applied Nuclear Physics

**Departments:**
1. Department of Radiation Protection and Monitoring Networks
2. Department of Radiometry and Radiochemistry
3. Department of Instrumental Analytical Methods

**Staff:** 23 employees (17 academic staff, 6 non-teaching)
- 4 Professors
- 2 Associate Professors
- 3 Doctors of Science
- 8 Masters of Science

**Director:** Prof. Assoc. Dr. Brunilda Daci

**Research Areas:**
- Applied nuclear physics
- Radiation protection
- Environmental radioactivity monitoring
- Nuclear analytical methods

## 9. Challenges and Opportunities

### Current Challenges

1. **Declining Enrollment:** Physics programs face decreasing student numbers
2. **Resource Constraints:** Limited funding for modern laboratory equipment
3. **Brain Drain:** Young physicists often leave for better opportunities abroad
4. **Infrastructure:** Need for upgraded research facilities

### Opportunities

1. **CERN Collaboration:** Major step forward for Albanian physics research
2. **International Networking:** Growing connections with European institutions
3. **Balkan Regional Cooperation:** Active participation in regional initiatives
4. **Digital Education:** Expanding online learning and data science applications

---

## 10. Conclusion

Albania's physics landscape is characterized by a network of public universities offering physics education across the country, with the University of Tirana and Polytechnic University of Tirana leading research efforts. The establishment of the National Institute of Physics and the recent CERN collaboration mark significant milestones for Albanian physics. While challenges remain, the growing international partnerships and dedicated physicists are positioning Albania for greater contributions to global physics research.

The participation in the CMS experiment at CERN represents a transformative moment for Albanian physics, providing access to world-class research infrastructure and international scientific networks. Continued support for physics education and research will be crucial for sustaining this momentum.
