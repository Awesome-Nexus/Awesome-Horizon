# Physics in Thailand: A Comprehensive Overview

Thailand has emerged as a leading research hub for physics in Southeast Asia, with significant investments in synchrotron radiation, nuclear technology, astronomy, and quantum technologies. The country has developed strong research infrastructure through strategic government funding and international collaborations, positioning itself as a regional center for scientific excellence in ASEAN.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Thai Physics Society (TPS)

The **Thai Physics Society** is the premier professional organization representing physicists in Thailand.

- **Founded**: 1978
- **Mission**: To promote physics research, education, and applications in Thailand
- **Activities**:
  - Organizes the annual **Siam Physics Congress** (SPC), the largest physics conference in Thailand
  - Publishes the *Thai Journal of Physics*
  - Promotes physics education and public outreach
  - Facilitates networking among physicists nationwide
- **Membership**: Open to physicists, physics educators, and physics students
- **Website**: http://thaiphysoc.org/

### Nuclear Society of Thailand

- **Focus**: Promoting nuclear science and technology applications
- **Activities**: Conferences, workshops on nuclear physics and radiation safety
- **Collaboration**: Works closely with TINT and the Office of Atoms for Peace

## 3. National Research Centers

### Synchrotron Light Research Institute (SLRI)

**Founded**: 1996 (operations began 2003)

**Status**: Public Organization under Ministry of Higher Education, Science, Research and Innovation

**Location**: Nakhon Ratchasima, 250 km from Bangkok

**Overview**: SLRI operates Thailand's synchrotron light source, a major research facility serving academic and industrial users throughout Thailand and ASEAN.

**Major Facility - Siam Photon Source (SPS)**:
- **Type**: 1.2 GeV electron storage ring
- **Origin**: Originally built in Japan by SORTEC corporation, donated to Thailand in 1996
- **Redesigned**: For 1.2 GeV with Double Bend Achromat (DBA) lattice
- **Operation**: Since 2003
- **Users**: Over 2,500 projects served since 2003

**Technical Specifications**:
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Energy | 1.2 GeV |
| Current | 150 mA (typical) |
| Operational Beamlines | 10 |
| Horizontal Emittance | 57.10 nm rad |
| Vertical Emittance | 4.45 pm rad |

**Insertion Devices**:
- Undulator (U60)
- Superconducting Wavelength Shifter (SWLS 6T)
- Multipole Wiggler (MPW 2.2T)

**Research Applications**:
- Materials science
- Life sciences and protein crystallography
- Environmental science
- Cultural heritage studies
- Industrial quality control

**Future Development**:
- **Siam Photon Source II (SPS-II)**: Planned 3 GeV facility, expected operation by 2034

**International Collaboration**:
- Close collaboration with SPring-8 (Japan)
- Member of the global light source community

**Website**: https://www.slri.or.th/

### Thailand Institute of Nuclear Technology (TINT)

**Founded**: December 2006 (operational since December 25, 2006)

**Status**: Public Organization under Ministry of Higher Education, Science, Research and Innovation

**Location**: Ongkharak District, Nakhon Nayok Province (headquarters); Bangkok facilities

**Overview**: TINT is Thailand's primary nuclear research and development organization, responsible for peaceful applications of nuclear technology.

**Research Areas**:
1. **Medical and Public Health** - Radiopharmaceutical production
2. **Agricultural Applications** - Plant mutation breeding, sterile insect technique
3. **Material and Industrial Applications** - Non-destructive testing
4. **Environmental Applications** - Isotope hydrology, pollution tracking
5. **Advanced Technology** - Fusion research, SMR studies

**Major Facilities**:

#### Thai Research Reactor-1/Modification 1 (TRR-1/M1)
- **Type**: TRIGA Mark III pool-type research reactor
- **Power**: 1.2 MW (upgraded from original 1 MW)
- **First Criticality**: October 27, 1962 (original), 1977 (after modification)
- **Fuel**: Low-enriched uranium (LEU) TRIGA fuel
- **Applications**:
  - Neutron activation analysis
  - Radioisotope production (I-131, Sm-153, etc.)
  - Neutron radiography
  - Education and training

#### Thailand Tokamak-1 (TT-1)
- **Type**: Small research tokamak (upgraded from China's HT-6M)
- **First Hydrogen Plasma**: 2023
- **Purpose**: Fusion plasma research
- **Consortium**: CPaF (Consortium of Plasma and Fusion) with 19 Thai partners

#### Other Facilities**:
- Gamma Irradiation Plant
- Cyclotron (30 MeV) for isotope production
- Isotope Hydrology Laboratory
- Clean Room facilities
- Radioactive Waste Management Center

**Radiopharmaceutical Production**:
- Tc-99m (200-250 doses/week)
- Ga-68 (10-15 doses/week for PSMA, 5-10 doses/week for DOTATATE)
- I-131 (20 curies/week)
- F-18 FDG (200-250 doses/week)

**Future Plans**:
- New multi-purpose research reactor at Ongkharak Nuclear Research Centre (ONRC)
- Small Modular Reactor (SMR) feasibility studies
- ITER participation coordination

**Website**: https://www.tint.or.th/
