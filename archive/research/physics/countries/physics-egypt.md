# Physics in Egypt: A Comprehensive Overview

Egypt has established itself as a leading physics nation in the Middle East and Africa, with a rich scientific heritage dating back to antiquity and a modern research ecosystem encompassing major universities, national research centers, and international collaborations. This document provides a comprehensive overview of physics institutions, notable physicists, research strengths, and international partnerships in Egypt.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Egyptian Physical Society (EPS)

The Egyptian Physical Society (EPS) serves as the primary professional organization for physicists in Egypt. Founded to promote the advancement of physics research and education, the society plays a crucial role in:

- **Organizing scientific conferences** and workshops on various physics disciplines
- **Publishing scientific journals** to disseminate research findings
- **Facilitating networking** among Egyptian physicists and international colleagues
- **Advocating for physics education** at all levels
- **Promoting public understanding** of physics and its applications

The EPS maintains affiliations with international physics organizations including the European Physical Society and the American Physical Society, enabling Egyptian physicists to participate in global scientific discourse.

### Other Professional Organizations

- **Egyptian Mathematical Society** - Collaborates with theoretical physicists
- **Egyptian Astronomical Society** - Focuses on astrophysics and space science
- **Egyptian Materials Research Society** - Addresses materials science and solid-state physics

## 2. Major University Physics Departments

Egypt boasts a comprehensive network of public and private universities with strong physics programs. The following institutions represent the pillars of physics education and research in the country.

### 2.1 Cairo University (جامعة القاهرة)

**Founded:** 1908  
**Location:** Giza, Greater Cairo  
**Website:** [cu.edu.eg](https://cu.edu.eg)

Cairo University is Egypt's premier public university and the second oldest institution of higher education after Al-Azhar. The **Faculty of Science** houses the Department of Physics, offering comprehensive programs in:

- **Classical and Quantum Mechanics**
- **Electromagnetism and Electrodynamics**
- **Thermodynamics and Statistical Physics**
- **Nuclear Physics**
- **Solid State Physics**
- **Optics and Photonics**
- **Astrophysics**

The university has produced numerous distinguished physicists and has research collaborations with institutions worldwide. With over 200,000 students, it is one of the largest universities globally and maintains strong rankings across African and Arab university rankings.

**Notable Physics-Related Alumni:**
- Ahmed Zewail (Nobel Laureate in Chemistry 1999)
- Mohamed ElBaradei (Nobel Peace Prize 2005)
- Various NASA scientists and space researchers

### 2.2 Alexandria University (جامعة الإسكندرية)

**Founded:** 1938 (as Farouk I University)  
**Location:** Alexandria  
**Website:** [alexu.edu.eg](https://www.alexu.edu.eg)

Alexandria University is the second largest university in Egypt with approximately 180,000 students. The **Faculty of Science** includes a Physics Department with particular strengths in:

- **Electrical Engineering Physics**
- **Nuclear Engineering** (Yahya El Mashad was a prominent alumnus)
- **Materials Science**
- **Environmental Physics**

The university has expanded internationally with branches in Chad (2010) and South Sudan (2014), exporting Egyptian scientific education to the African continent.

**Notable Physics-Related Alumni:**
- Ahmed Zewail (B.Sc. and M.Sc. in Chemistry, with strong physics foundation)
- Yahya El Mashad (Nuclear scientist, headed Iraqi nuclear program)
- Mohamed Hashish (Father of the abrasive water jet cutter)

### 2.3 Ain Shams University (جامعة عين شمس)

**Founded:** 1950  
**Location:** Abbassia, Cairo  
**Website:** [asu.edu.eg](https://www.asu.edu.eg)

Ain Shams University is the third-oldest non-sectarian public Egyptian university, located on the site of the former Zafarana Palace. The university comprises multiple physics-related departments:

**Faculty of Science - Physics Department:**
- Theoretical Physics
- Experimental Physics
- Applied Physics

**Faculty of Engineering - Engineering Physics Department:**
- Semiconductor Physics
- Solar Cell Technology
- Nanoelectronics
- 3D Detectors

The university has a strong international presence, with faculty members regularly publishing in high-impact journals and participating in international conferences. The physics program emphasizes:

- High-quality scientific research
- Continuous development to keep abreast of scientific progress
- Application of mathematical tools to analyze experimental results
- Integration of theoretical and practical aspects

**Research Areas:**
- High Energy Physics and Cosmology (through the Center for Theoretical Physics)
- Materials Science and Nanotechnology
- Solar Energy and Photovoltaics
- Radiation Physics

### 2.4 Capital University (formerly Helwan University) (جامعة العاصمة)

**Founded:** 1980  
**Location:** Helwan, Greater Cairo  
**Website:** [helwan.edu.eg](https://www.helwan.edu.eg)

Capital University is the youngest of the three major governmental universities in Cairo, encompassing some of Egypt's oldest faculties. The **Faculty of Science** includes physics programs with focus on:

- Applied Physics
- Materials Science
- Electronics

The university has 50 research centers and strong programs in applied sciences, making it an important hub for physics research with practical applications.

### 2.5 Mansoura University (جامعة المنصورة)

**Founded:** 1972  
**Location:** Mansoura, Nile Delta  
**Website:** [mans.edu.eg](https://www.mans.edu.eg)

Mansoura University is one of Egypt's largest universities, renowned for its medical programs but also maintaining a strong **Faculty of Science** with physics research. Notable physics-related facilities include:

- **Mansoura University Nanotechnology Center** - Focuses on nanoscale physics and materials
- **Scientific Computing Center (SCC)** - Provides computational physics resources
- **Glass Research Group** - Studies glass physics and materials

The university gained international recognition when a dinosaur species (*Mansourasaurus*) was named after it, discovered by university paleontologist Hesham Sallam.

### 2.6 Assiut University (جامعة أسيوط)

**Founded:** 1957  
**Location:** Assiut, Upper Egypt  
**Website:** [aun.edu.eg](http://www.aun.edu.eg)

Assiut University was the first university established in Upper Egypt, serving as a crucial center for science education in southern Egypt. The **Faculty of Science** offers physics programs that have produced many researchers who contribute to Egypt's scientific development.

The university includes specialized institutes such as the **South Egypt Cancer Institute (SECI)**, which involves medical physics applications.

### 2.7 Al-Azhar University (جامعة الأزهر)

**Founded:** c. 970/972 (university status 1961)  
**Location:** Cairo  
**Website:** [azhar.edu.eg](https://www.azhar.edu.eg)

While primarily known as the world's premier institution for Islamic learning, Al-Azhar University expanded to include secular faculties in 1961, including science and engineering. The Faculty of Science offers physics education integrated with Islamic scholarship.

Historically, Al-Azhar was a center for:
- **Islamic Astronomy** - Studying celestial phenomena for religious calendar purposes
- **Mathematical Physics** - Including contributions from scholars like Sibt al-Maridini (1423–1495), an Arab physicist, mathematician, and astronomer

### 2.8 Private Universities with Physics Programs

#### American University in Cairo (AUC)
**Founded:** 1919  
**Website:** [aucegypt.edu](https://www.aucegypt.edu)

AUC offers American-style physics education with programs in:
- Physics (Bachelor of Science)

- Space Sciences and Astronomy

The university has produced notable alumni in science and technology and maintains strong research facilities.

#### German University in Cairo (GUC)
**Founded:** 2002  
**Website:** [guc.edu.eg](https://www.guc.edu.eg)

GUC offers German-accredited physics-related programs through its:
- Faculty of Information Engineering & Technology

The university operates under German educational standards and has partnerships with universities in Germany, facilitating student and researcher exchanges.

#### British University in Egypt (BUE)
**Founded:** 2005  
**Website:** [bue.edu.eg](https://www.bue.edu.eg)

BUE offers UK-accredited degrees with physics components in engineering and applied sciences programs.

## 3. National Research Centers

### 3.1 Egyptian Atomic Energy Authority (EAEA) (هيئة الطاقة الذرية المصرية)

**Founded:** 1955  
**Location:** Inshas, 60 km northeast of Cairo  
**Website:** [eaea.gov.eg](http://www.eaea.gov.eg)

The Egyptian Atomic Energy Authority is the cornerstone of nuclear research in Egypt, leading national R&D in basic and applied peaceful nuclear research. Egypt was the second African nation (after South Africa) to build a nuclear reactor.

The EAEA is organized into four major research centers:

#### 3.1.1 Nuclear Research Center (NRC)
The primary facility for nuclear physics research, housing the research reactors and associated laboratories.

#### 3.1.2 Hot Laboratory and Waste Management Center (HLWMC)
Handles radioactive materials and manages nuclear waste disposal.

#### 3.1.3 National Centre for Radiation Research and Technology (NCRRT)
Focuses on applications of radiation technology in:
- Medical physics and radiation therapy
- Food irradiation
- Industrial applications
- Environmental protection

#### 3.1.4 National Centre for Nuclear Safety and Radiation Control (NCNSRC)
Ensures nuclear safety standards and radiation protection regulations.

The EAEA is a member of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) and participates in numerous international collaborations.

### 3.2 National Research Centre (NRC) (المركز القومي للبحوث)

**Founded:** 1956  
**Location:** Dokki, Giza  
**Website:** [nrc.sci.eg](https://www.nrc.sci.eg)

The National Research Centre is the largest institution affiliated with Egypt's Ministry of Scientific Research, employing over 4,700 scientists. Among its 14 institutes, the **Physics Research Institute** is particularly significant, containing 6 departments:

- **Materials Physics**
- **Nuclear Physics**
- **Laser Physics**
- **Semiconductor Physics**
- **Theoretical Physics**
- **Applied Physics**

The Physics Research Institute conducts cutting-edge research in:
- Nanotechnology and nanomaterials
- Laser applications
- Semiconductor devices
- Renewable energy materials
- Radiation physics

### 3.3 Zewail City of Science, Technology and Innovation (مدينة زويل للعلوم والتكنولوجيا والابتكار)

**Founded:** 2011 (concept proposed 1999)  
**Location:** 6th of October City, Giza  
**Website:** [zewailcity.edu.eg](https://www.zewailcity.edu.eg)

Named after Nobel Laureate Ahmed Zewail, Zewail City represents Egypt's commitment to world-class scientific research and education. The institution comprises a university, research institutes, and a technology park.

**Research Institutes include:**

#### Institute for Basic Sciences (IBS)
- **Center for Fundamental Physics (CFP)** - Conducts research in theoretical and experimental physics, including:
  - High Energy Physics
  - Quantum Mechanics
  - Condensed Matter Physics
  - Mathematical Physics

#### NBE Institute for Nanoscience and Informatics (INI)
- Center for Nanotechnology
- Center for Nanoelectronics and Devices
- Center for Materials Science

#### Institute for Energy, Environment and Space (IEES)
- Center for Photonics and Smart Materials

#### Institute for Imaging and Visualization (IIV)
- Center for X-Ray Determination of the Structure of Matter
- Applications in materials physics and structural analysis

Zewail City has become a hub for international collaboration, hosting conferences and workshops that bring together physicists from around the world.

## 4. Major Research Facilities

### 4.1 ETRR-2 Research Reactor (مفاعل بحوث أنشاص-2)

**Location:** Inshas, Nuclear Research Center  
**Type:** Light Water Open Pool Reactor (Multi-Purpose)  
**Power:** 22 MW (thermal)  
**First Criticality:** November 27, 1997  
**Construction Cost:** US$75 million  
**Annual Operating Cost:** US$6 million

ETRR-2 (Experimental Training Research Reactor Number 2) is Egypt's primary nuclear research reactor, supplied by the Argentine company INVAP. It represents a significant upgrade from the aging ETRR-1 (a 4 MW Van de Graaff-type reactor commissioned in 1961).

**Technical Specifications:**
- **Fuel:** 19.75% enriched uranium U-235 (Egypt fabricates fuel rods domestically)
- **Moderator:** Light water
- **Coolant:** Light water (forced cooling at 2000 m³/h)
- **Reflector:** Beryllium
- **Control Rods:** Silver, Indium, Cadmium
- **Max Thermal Flux:** 2.8 × 10¹⁴ n/cm²-s
- **Max Fast Flux:** 2.2 × 10¹⁴ n/cm²-s

**Experimental Facilities:**
- 5 horizontal channels for neutron research and radiography
- 3 core irradiation facilities
- 26 reflector irradiation facilities
- 2 experimental loops for materials testing

**Applications:**
- **Radioisotope Production** - Medical and industrial isotopes
- **Neutron Radiography** - Non-destructive testing
- **Materials Testing** - Nuclear fuel and structural materials
- **Neutron Activation Analysis** - Trace element detection
- **Training and Education** - Nuclear engineering programs
- **Medical and Nuclear Solid-State Research**
- **Condensed Matter Research**

### 4.2 Fuel Manufacturing Pilot Plant (FMPP)

Located at the Nuclear Research Center in Inshas, this facility manufactures fuel rods for the ETRR-2 reactor using imported enriched uranium, demonstrating Egypt's capability in nuclear fuel fabrication.

### 4.3 Radioisotope Production Facility

A specialized facility at Inshas designed for the separation of radioisotopes from irradiated materials, supporting medical and industrial applications.

## 5. Notable Egyptian Physicists and Scientists

### 5.1 Ahmed Zewail (أحمد زويل) (1946–2016)

**Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1999)**

Ahmed Hassan Zewail was an Egyptian-American chemist and physicist known as the "Father of Femtochemistry." While his Nobel Prize was in Chemistry, his work bridged physics and chemistry, making him Egypt's most celebrated scientist.

**Key Achievements:**
- First Egyptian and Arab to win a Nobel Prize in a scientific field
- First African to win the Nobel Prize in Chemistry
- Pioneer of femtochemistry - studying chemical reactions on femtosecond (10⁻¹⁵ s) timescales
- Developed ultrafast laser techniques for observing transition states in chemical reactions
- Made critical contributions to ultrafast electron diffraction

**Education:**
- B.Sc. and M.Sc., Alexandria University (1967)
- Ph.D., University of Pennsylvania (1974)

**Career:**
- Professor at California Institute of Technology (Caltech)
- First Linus Pauling Chair of Chemical Physics at Caltech
- Director, Physical Biology Center for Ultrafast Science and Technology
- Founder of Zewail City of Science and Technology in Egypt

**Awards:**
- Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1999)
- Wolf Prize in Chemistry (1993)
- King Faisal International Prize (1989)
- Albert Einstein World Award of Science (2006)
- Order of the Nile (Egypt's highest honor, 1999)
- ForMemRS (Foreign Member of the Royal Society, 2001)

### 5.2 Farouk El-Baz (فاروق الباز) (b. 1938)

**Space Scientist and Geologist**

Farouk El-Baz is an Egyptian-American space scientist who played a pivotal role in NASA's Apollo program.

**Key Achievements:**
- Leading geologist on the Apollo program
- Selected landing sites for Apollo missions, including Apollo 11
- Trained Apollo astronauts in lunar observations and photography
- Secretary of the Landing Site Selection Committee
- Principal Investigator for Visual Observations and Photography
- Developed methods for discovering subsurface freshwater from space observations

**Education:**
- B.Sc., Ain Shams University (1958)
- M.Sc., Missouri School of Mines and Metallurgy (1961)
- Ph.D., Missouri University of Science and Technology (1964)

**Career:**
- Bellcomm Inc. (NASA contractor) - Apollo Program
- Smithsonian Institution - Center for Earth and Planetary Studies
- Boston University - Research Professor and Director of Center for Remote Sensing
- Science Adviser to President Anwar Sadat (1978)

**Honors:**
- NASA's Apollo Achievement Award
- Exceptional Scientific Achievement Medal
- Member, U.S. National Academy of Engineering (2002)
- Asteroid 7371 El-Baz named in his honor
- Order of Merit (Egypt) - First Class
- Shuttlecraft "El-Baz" in Star Trek: The Next Generation named after him

### 5.3 Yahya El Mashad (يحيى المشد) (1932–1980)

**Nuclear Scientist**

Yahya El Mashad was a prominent Egyptian nuclear scientist who made significant contributions to nuclear engineering in Egypt and Iraq.

**Key Contributions:**
- Professor of nuclear engineering at Alexandria University
- Head of the Iraqi nuclear program (Osirak reactor)
- Supervised Iraq's nuclear cooperation agreement with France
- Worked at the Egyptian Atomic Energy Authority until 1967

**Education:**
- B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering, Alexandria University (1952)
- Ph.D. in Nuclear Engineering, Moscow (after studies interrupted by Suez Crisis in London)

**Assassination:**
El Mashad was assassinated in Paris on June 14, 1980, in an operation attributed to Mossad. His death was a significant setback to the Iraqi nuclear program.

### 5.4 Other Notable Scientists with Physics Background

#### Moustafa Youssef (b. 1973)
- Computer scientist and engineer with physics background
- First and only ACM Fellow in the Middle East and Africa
- Faculty of Engineering, Ain Shams University alumnus

#### Mohamed Atalla (1924–2009)
- Engineer and inventor of the MOSFET (MOS field-effect transistor)
- Pioneer in silicon semiconductors and security systems
- Faculty of Engineering, Cairo University alumnus

#### Ali Moustafa Mosharafa (1898–1950)
- Theoretical physicist (though his legacy requires further research)
- Made contributions to quantum mechanics and relativity

## 6. Physics Research Areas of Strength

Egyptian physics research has developed significant expertise in several key areas:

### 6.1 Nuclear and Radiation Physics

Egypt's nuclear program, while focused on peaceful applications, has developed substantial expertise in:
- **Reactor Physics** - Operation and optimization of research reactors
- **Radiation Detection and Measurement**
- **Radioisotope Production** - For medical and industrial applications
- **Radiation Protection and Safety**
- **Neutron Physics** - Scattering, activation analysis, radiography

### 6.2 High Energy Physics and Particle Physics

Egyptian physicists contribute to international high-energy physics through:
- **Theoretical High Energy Physics** - String theory, quantum field theory, cosmology (Ain Shams University has notable strength)
- **Experimental Particle Physics** - Participation in CERN collaborations
- **Astroparticle Physics**

### 6.3 Condensed Matter and Materials Physics

Major research areas include:
- **Semiconductor Physics** - Solar cell technology, device physics
- **Nanomaterials** - Synthesis and characterization (Mansoura University Nanotechnology Center)
- **Magnetic Materials**
- **Superconductivity**
- **Dielectric Materials**

### 6.4 Laser Physics and Photonics

Research at the National Research Centre and universities focuses on:
- **Laser Applications** - Industrial, medical, environmental
- **Nonlinear Optics**
- **Photonics and Optoelectronics**
- **Laser Spectroscopy**

### 6.5 Solar Energy and Photovoltaics

Given Egypt's abundant solar resources, significant research focuses on:
- **Solar Cell Technology** - Silicon, thin-film, and emerging technologies
- **Photovoltaic Systems** - Design and optimization
- **Solar Thermal Applications**
- **Energy Storage** - Battery technologies

### 6.6 Theoretical Physics

Strong theoretical physics groups at Cairo, Ain Shams, and Zewail City work on:
- **Quantum Field Theory**
- **General Relativity and Cosmology**
- **String Theory**
- **Mathematical Physics**

### 6.7 Environmental Physics

Research addresses:
- **Atmospheric Physics** - Climate studies, pollution monitoring
- **Remote Sensing** - Satellite data analysis (following Farouk El-Baz's pioneering work)
- **Water Resources** - Groundwater detection using geophysical methods
- **Desertification Studies**

### 6.8 Medical Physics

Applications include:
- **Radiation Therapy Physics** - Treatment planning, dosimetry
- **Medical Imaging** - MRI, CT, nuclear medicine
- **Radiation Protection in Medicine**

## 7. International Collaborations

### 7.1 CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)

Egypt has established significant collaboration with CERN:

- **Member of SESAME Council** - Egypt is a founding member of SESAME, modeled on CERN
- **Researcher Participation** - Egyptian physicists participate in CERN experiments
- **Technology Transfer** - CERN has provided technical expertise and equipment to Egyptian institutions
- **Training Programs** - Egyptian scientists receive training at CERN facilities
- **Grid Computing** - Egypt participates in the Worldwide LHC Computing Grid

### 7.2 SESAME (Synchrotron-light for Experimental Science and Applications in the Middle East)

**Location:** Allan, Jordan  
**Founded:** 2002 (under UNESCO auspices)  
**Official Opening:** May 16, 2017

SESAME is a major international research facility where Egypt plays a leading role:

**Egypt's Role:**
- **Founding Member** - One of the original members alongside Bahrain, Israel, Jordan, Pakistan, and Turkey
- **Current Members** - Cyprus, Egypt, Iran, Israel, Jordan, Pakistan, Palestinian Authority, and Turkey
- **Observer Nations** - Brazil, Canada, China, EU, France, Germany, Greece, Italy, Japan, Kuwait, Portugal, Russia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, UK, USA

**Scientific Significance:**
- First synchrotron radiation facility in the Middle East
- Third-generation light source (2.5 GeV storage ring)
- Only accelerator in the world fully powered by renewable energy (solar)
- Promotes "Science for Peace" in the Middle East

**Egyptian Scientists at SESAME:**
- Egyptian researchers are regular users of SESAME beamlines
- The facility enables collaboration between scientists from countries with strained diplomatic relations

**Beamlines:**
- IR (Infrared) spectromicroscopy
- XAFS/XRF (X-ray Absorption Fine Structure/X-ray Fluorescence)
- MS/XPD (Materials Science/X-ray Powder Diffraction)
- BEATS (BEAmline for Tomography at SESAME)
- HESEB (HElmholtz-SEsame Beamline)
- TXPES (Turkish soft X-ray PhotoElectron Spectroscopy)
- MX (Macromolecular Crystallography - planned)
- SAXS/WAXS (Small Angle and Wide Angle X-ray Scattering - planned)

### 7.3 IAEA (International Atomic Energy Agency)

Egypt is a long-standing member of the IAEA and participates in:
- **Technical Cooperation Programs** - Capacity building in nuclear applications
- **Research Reactor Utilization** - Optimizing use of ETRR-2
- **Nuclear Safety Standards** - Implementing international best practices
- **Safeguards Implementation** - Ensuring peaceful use of nuclear technology

Regional cooperation among Arab states in nuclear science and technology.

## 9. Challenges and Future Prospects

### Current Challenges

1. **Funding Constraints** - Limited research budgets compared to international standards
2. **Brain Drain** - Loss of talented physicists to opportunities abroad
3. **Equipment Limitations** - Need for modern experimental facilities
4. **Publication Metrics** - Improving international visibility of Egyptian physics research
5. **Industry Linkages** - Strengthening connections between physics research and industry

### Future Opportunities

1. **Nuclear Power Program** - Planned construction of nuclear power plants
2. **Renewable Energy Expansion** - Solar and wind energy development
3. **Space Program** - Egyptian Space Agency development
4. **SESAME Utilization** - Increased use of regional synchrotron facility
5. **International Collaborations** - Growing partnerships with global institutions

---

## 10. Summary and Conclusion

Egypt has established itself as a regional leader in physics research and education in the Middle East and Africa. With:

- **Comprehensive university system** with physics programs at major institutions
- **Strong nuclear research infrastructure** centered on the EAEA
- **World-class facilities** like ETRR-2 and participation in SESAME
- **Distinguished scientific heritage** exemplified by Ahmed Zewail and Farouk El-Baz
- **Active international collaborations** with CERN, IAEA, and major scientific nations
- **Research strengths** in nuclear physics, materials science, and theoretical physics

Egypt continues to develop its physics ecosystem, addressing challenges while leveraging its strategic position, human resources, and international partnerships to advance scientific knowledge and technological development for the benefit of the nation and the broader region.
