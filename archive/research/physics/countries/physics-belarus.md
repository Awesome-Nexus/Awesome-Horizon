# Physics Research Institutions in Belarus

## Executive Summary

Belarus has a rich tradition in physics research, with its scientific infrastructure centered around the **National Academy of Sciences of Belarus (NASB)** and **Belarusian State University (BSU)**. The country has made significant contributions to laser physics, nuclear physics, nanotechnology, and optical sciences. This report provides a comprehensive overview of physics research institutions, education programs, and notable scientists in Belarus.

---

## 1. Belarusian State University (BSU) - Physics Faculty

### Overview
The Belarusian State University, founded in 1921, is the premier institution for physics education in Belarus. The Faculty of Physics has been training physicists since the university's inception and remains the primary source of physics graduates in the country.

### Structure
- **10 Departments**: Covering major areas of physics
- **10 Research Laboratories**: For advanced scientific work
- **Belarusian Interuniversity Research Service Center**: Shared research facility
- **Six student research societies**: For undergraduate research engagement

### Educational Programs
| Level | Programs Available |
|-------|-------------------|
| Bachelor's | General Physics, Applied Physics, Radiophysics |
| Master's | Specialized programs in laser physics, nuclear physics, condensed matter |
| PhD (Candidate) | All major physics disciplines |
| Doctor of Sciences | Highest scientific degree |

### Key Departments

3. Department of Nuclear Physics

5. Department of Solid State Physics
6. Department of Radiophysics

## 3. B.I. Stepanov Institute of Physics, NASB

### Overview
The first scientific-research center of physical and mathematical profile in the Republic of Belarus, founded on **January 17, 1955** as the Institute of Physics and Mathematics of the Academy of Sciences of the BSSR.

### Historical Development
- **1955**: Founded as Institute of Physics and Mathematics
- **1959**: Reorganized into two independent institutes - Institute of Physics and Institute of Mathematics and Computer Engineering
- **1967**: Awarded the Order of the Red Banner of Labor
- **1992**: Branch in Mogilev transformed into Institute of Applied Optics
- **1992**: Institute of Molecular and Atomic Physics (IMAP) formed from 10 laboratories

### Key Founding Figures
| Name | Role | Contribution |
|------|------|--------------|
| A.N. Sevchenko | First Director | Established research directions |
| B.I. Stepanov | Director (1957-1985) | Laser physics pioneer |
| M.A. Elyashevich | Founding Scientist | Spectroscopy and plasma physics |
| N.A. Borisevich | Founding Scientist | Spectroscopy of complex molecules |
| F.I. Fedorov | Founding Scientist | Optics of anisotropic media |

### Current Research Directions
1. **Laser Physics**: Development and fabrication of new laser sources and systems
2. **Nonlinear and Quantum Optics**: Laser spectroscopy
3. **Nonlinear Dynamics of Complex Systems**
4. **Physics of Quantum-Dimensional Structures**
5. **Transfer of Optical Radiation and Optics of Scattering Media**
6. **Physics of Elementary Particles and Nuclear Reactions**

### Major Scientific Achievements
- Covariant theory of electromagnetic and acoustic waves in anisotropic media
- Discovery of lateral displacement of light beam (Fedorov effect)
- Spectroscopy of free complex molecules
- Development of tunable dye lasers
- Physical bases of dynamic holography
- Phase conjugation of light beams discovery
- Methods for nonlinear optical transformation of laser radiation frequency
- Semiconductor laser physics and nonlinear optics
- Vector parametrization of Lorentz group (Fedorov's method)

### Awards and Recognition
- **2 Lenin Prizes**
- **7 State Prizes of Belarus**
- **3 Prizes of the Council of Ministers of the USSR**
- **10 Prizes of Lenin Komsomol**
- **3 Heroes of Socialist Labour**
- **Order of the Red Banner of Labor (1967)**

### Scientific Journals Founded
- **Journal of Applied Spectroscopy** (International)
- **Nonlinear Phenomena in Complex Systems** (with BSU)

### Dissertation Councils
- **D 01.05.01**: Optics; Physics of Plasma; Laser Physics
- **D 01.05.02**: Theoretical Physics; Physics of Nucleus and Elementary Particles; High-Energy Physics

## 6. Joint Institute for Power and Nuclear Research "Sosny" (JIPNR-Sosny), NASB

### Overview
The legal successor of the Nuclear Power Engineering Institute of the BSSR Academy of Sciences, founded on **June 22, 1965**. It is the leading scientific organization in Belarus for nuclear energy and radiation technologies.

### Historical Development
- **1965**: Founded as Nuclear Power Engineering Institute
- **First Director**: Academician A.K. Krasin (Lenin Prize winner)
- **1973**: Special Design Bureau established
- **1985**: World's first mobile nuclear power plant Pamir-630D created
- **Post-Chernobyl**: Active involvement in radiation safety and monitoring

### Main Activities
1. **Nuclear Energy**: Scientific support for Belarusian NPP development
2. **Nuclear and Radiation Technologies**: For various economic sectors
3. **Radioactive Waste Management**: R&D in waste processing and storage
4. **Fundamental Research**: Nuclear physics, particle physics, high-energy physics

### Key Facilities
- **IRT-1000 Research Reactor**: Upgraded to 4,000kW capacity
- **Radiochemistry Laboratory**: Hot cells for high-activity materials
- **Thermophysical Experimental Installations**
- **Subcritical Assembly "Yalina"**
- **Critical Stand "Hyacinth"**
- **Multi-purpose Electron Accelerator UELV-10-10**
- **Industrial Gamma Installation UGU-420**

### Products and Services
- Radiation sterilization of medical goods and food
- Radiation resistance analysis of materials
- Plasma-electrolytic treatment of metals
- Development of radiation technologies
- Flowmeters and heat-meters verification
- Gamma-spectrometric analysis
- Radon monitoring

### Spin-off Companies
1. **Belocryo LLC** (1993): Cryogenic tanks and gasifiers
2. **BelIKO** (1997): Automated radiation monitoring systems
3. **Isotope Technologies CJSC** (1998): Ionizing radiation sources for medical and industrial use

### Contact Information
- **Postal Address**: 220109 Minsk, PO Box 119
- **Website**: https://sosny.bas-net.by/en/

## 7. Research Institute for Nuclear Problems (INP), BSU

### Overview
Founded on **September 1, 1986**, by decree of the Soviet government. It is affiliated with Belarusian State University and focuses on nuclear physics, particle physics, and nanotechnology.

### Leadership
| Period | Director |
|--------|----------|
| 1986-2013 | Vladimir G. Baryshevsky (Founding Director) |
| 2013-present | Sergei A. Maksimenko |

### Major Research Areas
1. Nuclear and elementary particle physics
2. Cosmo-particle physics and nuclear astrophysics
3. Extreme states of matter (ultrahigh temperatures and pressures)
4. Novel composite, nano- and microstructured materials
5. Radio- and nuclear technologies
6. Ionizing radiation measurement methods
7. **Nanoelectromagnetism**: Electromagnetic radiation interaction with nanosized objects

### Major Achievements
- **Parametric X-ray Radiation (PXR)**: Theoretical prediction and experimental discovery
- **Channeling Radiation**: Prediction of new radiation type from relativistic particles in crystals
- **Lead Tungstate (PbWO4) Crystals**: Developed for CERN's CMS and ALICE detectors
- **Higgs Boson Discovery**: INP scientists part of CMS collaboration at CERN
- **Nanoelectromagnetism Theory**: Comprehensive theory of electromagnetic wave scattering from carbon nanotubes
- **Volume Free-Electron Lasers**: New class of electromagnetic radiation sources

### Contact Information
- **Address**: Bobruyskaya str. 11, Minsk, 220030, Republic of Belarus
- **Staff**: ~97 (as of 2014)
- **Website**: https://inp.bsu.by
