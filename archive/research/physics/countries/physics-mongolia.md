# Physics in Mongolia: A Comprehensive Overview

Mongolia has a distinguished tradition in physics research, with strong foundations in nuclear physics, theoretical physics, and high-energy physics. The country's physics community has been significantly shaped by its long-standing membership in the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR) in Dubna, Russia, dating back to 1956. Mongolian physicists have made notable contributions to nuclear structure research, multiple particle production studies, and radiation physics while maintaining active international collaborations across Asia and Europe.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Mongolian Physical Society (MPS)

The **Mongolian Physical Society** is the premier professional organization representing physicists in Mongolia, operating under the Mongolian Academy of Sciences.

- **Founded**: Established as part of the Mongolian Academy of Sciences structure
- **Mission**: To promote physics research, education, and applications in Mongolia; to foster international scientific cooperation; to support the development of nuclear and radiation sciences
- **Activities**:
  - Organizes national physics conferences and workshops
  - Promotes physics education at all levels
  - Facilitates networking among physicists nationwide
  - Supports international collaborations, particularly with JINR
  - Represents Mongolia in international physics organizations
- **Membership**: Open to physicists, physics educators, and physics students
- **Affiliation**: Operates under the Mongolian Academy of Sciences (MAS)
- **Key Role**: Coordinates Mongolia's participation in the Asian Nuclear Physics Association (ANPhA)

### Mongolian Nuclear Society

- **Focus**: Promoting nuclear science and technology applications for peaceful purposes
- **Activities**: Conferences, workshops on nuclear physics, radiation safety, and nuclear energy development
- **Collaboration**: Works closely with the Nuclear Research Center at the National University of Mongolia and the Nuclear Energy Commission of Mongolia

## 4. Notable Physics Research Areas

### Nuclear Physics and Nuclear Technology

Mongolia has a strong tradition in nuclear physics research, with particular emphasis on:

- **Nuclear Structure**: Studies of nuclear decay processes, energy levels, and transition probabilities in lanthanide and ytterbium nuclei
- **Low-Energy Nuclear Physics**: Research on nuclear orientation methods and gamma-ray spectroscopy
- **Nuclear Decay Studies**: Investigation of daughter nuclei from radioactive decay
- **Applied Nuclear Techniques**: Isotope applications in industry, medicine, and environmental monitoring

### High Energy Physics

Active research programs include:

- **Hadron Physics**: Studies of multiple particle production in high-energy collisions
- **Nucleus-Nucleus Interactions**: Research on relativistic heavy-ion collisions
- **Cosmic Ray Physics**: Detection and analysis of high-energy cosmic particles

### Condensed Matter Physics and Materials Science

Research areas include:

- **Semiconductor Physics**: Electronic properties and device applications
- **Dielectric Materials**: Properties and applications
- **Rare Earth Materials**: Studies relevant to Mongolia's mineral resources
- **Nanomaterials**: Emerging research in nanoscale phenomena

### Radiation Physics and Environmental Radioactivity

- **Radiation Dosimetry**: Measurement and monitoring of radiation levels
- **Environmental Monitoring**: Radioactivity assessment in soil, water, and air
- **Radiation Safety**: Development of protection protocols
- **Radioecology**: Studies of radioactive contamination in the environment

### Theoretical Physics

- **Mathematical Physics**: Formal methods and mathematical foundations
- **Computational Physics**: Numerical simulations and modeling
- **Nuclear Theory**: Theoretical models of nuclear structure and reactions

### Geophysics and Atmospheric Physics

- **Mining Geophysics**: Applications for Mongolia's mining industry
- **Atmospheric Studies**: Research on air quality and environmental physics
- **Climate Research**: Physics of the Mongolian climate and weather patterns

## 7. International Collaborations

### JINR (Joint Institute for Nuclear Research) - Dubna, Russia

**Status**: Mongolia has been a JINR Member State since its foundation in 1956

**Historical Significance**:
- One of the founding member states of JINR
- Academician N. Sodnom signed the JINR Charter
- Continuous collaboration for nearly 70 years

**Collaboration Activities**:

| Laboratory | Cooperation Themes | Activities |
|------------|-------------------|------------|
| BLTP (Bogoliubov Lab of Theoretical Physics) | Theoretical physics | Joint research, educational programs |
| FLNP (Frank Lab of Neutron Physics) | Neutron research | Ecological studies in Ulaanbaatar |
| FLNR (Flerov Lab of Nuclear Reactions) | Nuclear reactions | Radioecological studies |
| LIT (Lab of Information Technologies) | IT applications | Computational physics |
| LRBR (Lab of Radiation Biology) | Radiation research | Joint projects |

**Personnel Exchange** (as of 2021):
- 9 Mongolian experts work full-time at JINR
- ~15 Mongolian scientists visit JINR annually for short-term projects
- Similar number of JINR researchers visit Mongolia

**Educational Programs**:
- Mongolian students participate in JINR educational programs
- JINR serves as a "modern physics school" for Mongolian specialists
- NUMAR-Gobi International School (since 2025) - nuclear methods training

## 9. Notable Physicists from Mongolia

### Namsrai Sodnom (1923-2002)

- **Position**: Academician, President of Mongolian Academy of Sciences, Rector of Mongolian State University
- **Role at JINR**: First Plenipotentiary Representative (1956), JINR Vice-Director (1967-1973)
- **Achievement**: Signed the JINR Charter on behalf of Mongolia; organized national science and education
- **Legacy**: The 100th anniversary of his birth was celebrated in 2023 at JINR

### Tseepeldorj Baatar

- **Born**: July 1, 1945
- **Position**: Academician of Mongolian Academy of Sciences, Professor, Doctor of Physical and Mathematical Sciences
- **Role**: Head of Laboratory of High Energy Physics, Institute of Physics and Technology
- **JINR Role**: Member of JINR Scientific Council (1994-2009, 2011-present)
- **Research Area**: High-energy physics, multiple particle production in hadron-nuclear and nucleus-nucleus interactions
- **Publications**: Author of approximately 200 scientific papers
- **Achievement**: Leading figure in Mongolian high-energy physics research

### Suren Davaa

- **Born**: November 23, 1953
- **Position**: Professor, Candidate of Sciences in Physics and Mathematics, Senior Officer at Nuclear Energy Commission of Mongolia
- **Former Positions**:

- **Research Area**: Nuclear physics, nuclear energy infrastructure, nuclear orientation methods
