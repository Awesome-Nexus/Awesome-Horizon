# Physics in South Korea: A Comprehensive Overview

South Korea has emerged as a rapidly growing physics power in Asia, with significant investments in basic science research, world-class research facilities, and strong international collaborations. This document provides a comprehensive overview of the physics landscape in South Korea.

---

## 1. National Physics Societies

### Korean Physical Society (KPS)
- **Founded:** 1952 in Busan during the Korean War
- **Membership:** Approximately 15,000 members (as of 2020)
- **Structure:** 12 topic-specific divisions including:
  - Particle and Field Physics
  - Atomic and Molecular Physics
  - Astrophysics
  - Condensed Matter Physics
  - Statistical Physics
  - Biophysics
  - Nuclear Physics
  - Plasma Physics
  - Optics
  - Computational Physics
  - Physics Education
  - Applied Physics

- **Publications:**
  - *Journal of the Korean Physical Society* (flagship journal, published by Springer)
  - Covers all fields of physics from statistical physics to particle physics

- **International Collaborations:**
  - Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the American Physical Society (APS) since 2012
  - MOU with the Japan Society of Applied Physics
  - Active participation in Asian Pacific Physics Conference

## 2. Major University Physics Departments

### Seoul National University (SNU)
- **Location:** Gwanak-gu, Seoul (main campus)
- **Founded:** 1946
- **Physics Department:** Department of Physics & Astronomy
- **Research Areas:**
  - Nuclear Physics, Particle Physics, and Cosmology
  - Atomic, Molecular, and Optical Physics (AMO)
  - Condensed Matter Physics
  - Statistical Physics
  - Biophysics
  - Nano Physics and Nano Photonics

- **Notable Features:**
  - Ranked #1 in South Korea, #38 globally (QS 2026)
  - Center for Theoretical Physics (CTP)
  - Institute for Applied Physics (IAP)
  - Strong experimental and theoretical physics programs
  - Home to the IBS Center for Correlated Electron Systems

### KAIST (Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology)
- **Location:** Daejeon (Daedeok Innopolis)
- **Founded:** 1971 (as KAIS), renamed KAIST in 1981
- **Department:** Department of Physics
- **Research Focus:**
  - Condensed matter physics
  - Particle physics
  - Quantum information
  - Statistical physics
  - Biophysics

- **Notable Achievements:**
  - Ranked #53 globally (QS 2026)
  - Birthplace of IBS (Institute for Basic Science)
  - Hosts Korea Institute for Advanced Study (KIAS)
  - First public research-oriented science and engineering institution in Korea
  - Full scholarships for all students including international students

### POSTECH (Pohang University of Science and Technology)
- **Location:** Pohang, North Gyeongsang Province
- **Founded:** 1986 by POSCO (steel company)
- **Physics Department:** Department of Physics
- **Research Strengths:**
  - Condensed matter physics
  - Materials science
  - Nanophysics
  - Quantum materials

- **Key Facilities:**
  - Pohang Accelerator Laboratory (PAL) - 1994
  - PAL-XFEL (X-ray Free Electron Laser) - 2016
  - Third XFEL in the world, first in Asia
  - Home to multiple IBS Centers:
    - Center for Artificial Low Dimensional Electronic Systems
    - Center for Van der Waals Quantum Solids
    - Center for Self-assembly and Complexity

### Yonsei University
- **Location:** Seoul (Sinchon Campus) and Songdo (International Campus)
- **Founded:** 1885 (as Severance Hospital), university status 1957
- **Department:** Department of Physics (College of Science)
- **Research Areas:**
  - Condensed matter physics
  - Optical physics
  - Statistical mechanics
  - Biophysics
  - Medical physics

- **Notable Features:**
  - Part of SKY universities (most prestigious in Korea)
  - Strong interdisciplinary research with medical school
  - Home to IBS Center for Nanomedicine

### Korea University
- **Location:** Seoul (Anam-dong)
- **Founded:** 1905 (as Bosung College)
- **Department:** Department of Physics (College of Science)
- **Research Focus:**
  - Condensed matter physics
  - Statistical physics
  - Materials physics
  - Computational physics

- **Notable Features:**
  - One of Korea's SKY universities
  - Strong research infrastructure with Science Library
  - Home to IBS Center for Molecular Spectroscopy and Dynamics

### Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU)
- **Location:** Seoul (Humanities Campus) and Suwon (Natural Sciences Campus)
- **Founded:** 1398 (Sungkyunkwan), modern university 1946
- **Department:** Department of Physics
- **Research Strengths:**
  - Nanotechnology and nanophysics
  - Condensed matter physics
  - 2D materials
  - Spintronics

- **Key Research Centers:**
  - Sungkyunkwan Advanced Institute of Nanotechnology (SAINT)
  - IBS Center for Integrated Nanostructure Physics (CINAP)
  - IBS Center for Neuroscience Imaging Research
  - N-Center (Nanotechnology and Neuroscience)

### Korea Research Institute of Standards and Science (KRISS)
- **Founded:** 1975 (as Korea Standards Research Institute)
- **Renamed:** 1991
- **Location:** Daejeon
- **Role:** National measurement standards laboratory
- **Membership:** General Conference on Weights and Measures (CGPM)
- **Budget:** ~$108 million USD (2011)
- **Staff:** ~410 (2012)
- **Key Functions:**
  - Maintains South Korea's primary measurement standards
  - National metrology institute since 1999
  - Time signal broadcast (HLA radio station)

### Korea Institute of Fusion Energy (KIFE)
- **Location:** Daejeon
- **Key Facility:** KSTAR (Korea Superconducting Tokamak Advanced Research)
- **Focus:** Nuclear fusion research
- **International Role:** Major contributor to ITER project

### Korea Atomic Energy Research Institute (KAERI)
- **Founded:** 1959
- **Location:** Daejeon
- **Staff:** ~1,431
- **Budget:** ~$534.5 million KRW
- **Key Facilities:**
  - HANARO research reactor (1995)
  - SMART (System-Integrated Modular Advanced Reactor)
  - Radioisotope production
- **Spin-offs:** Korea Institute of Nuclear Safety (KINS), KEPCO E&C, Korea Nuclear Fuel Co.

### Pohang Accelerator Laboratory (PAL)
- **Founded:** 1994
- **Location:** Pohang (POSTECH campus)
- **Facilities:**
  - Pohang Light Source (PLS-II) - 3rd generation synchrotron
  - PAL-XFEL (X-ray Free Electron Laser) - 4th generation light source

## 4. Major Research Facilities

### KSTAR (Korea Superconducting Tokamak Advanced Research)
- **Type:** Tokamak fusion device
- **Location:** Korea Institute of Fusion Energy, Daejeon
- **Construction Completed:** September 14, 2007
- **First Plasma:** June 2008

**Technical Specifications:**
- Major radius: 1.8 m
- Minor radius: 0.5 m
- Magnetic field: 3.5 T
- Heating power: 14 MW
- Plasma current: 2 MA

**World Records:**
- December 2016: 50 million°C for 70 seconds
- December 2020: 100 million°C for 20 seconds
- November 2021: 100 million°C for 30 seconds
- February 2024: 100 million°C for 48 seconds

**Significance:**
- One of the first fully superconducting tokamaks
- Key contributor to ITER project development
- Testing ground for fusion technologies

### PAL-XFEL (Pohang Accelerator Laboratory X-ray Free Electron Laser)
- **Type:** 4th generation light source
- **Location:** Pohang
- **Construction Cost:** ~$390 million USD
- **First Light:** June 2016

**Capabilities:**
- 10 billion times brighter than 3rd generation synchrotron
- Femtosecond pulse duration
- Coherent X-ray beams

**Research Applications:**
- Life sciences (protein crystallography)
- Materials science
- Chemistry
- Physics
- Ultrafast dynamics studies

### RAON (Rare Isotope Science Project)
- **Type:** Heavy ion accelerator facility
- **Location:** Daejeon (IBS)
- **Unique Feature:** World's first facility combining ISOL and IF methods
- **Purpose:** Rare isotope production for nuclear physics research

### HANARO
- **Type:** Research reactor
- **Location:** KAERI, Daejeon
- **Design:** Based on Canadian MAPLE design
- **Power:** 30 MW
- **Uses:**
  - Neutron scattering research
  - Radioisotope production
  - Nuclear materials testing
  - Training

### Aleph Supercomputer
- **Location:** IBS Center for Climate Physics, Pusan National University
- **Type:** Cray XC50
- **Performance:** 1.43 petaflops
- **Purpose:** Climate physics simulations

### Olaf Supercomputer
- **Location:** IBS Headquarters
- **Achievement:** Ranked 10th on Green500 (2023)
- **Purpose:** General scientific computing

## 5. Notable Physicists

### Nobel Laureates of Korean Descent

While no Korean citizen has yet won the Nobel Prize in Physics, several physicists of Korean descent have made significant contributions:

#### 1. **Tsung-Dao Lee (李政道)**
- **Birth:** 1926 (Shanghai, China)
- **Death:** August 4, 2024
- **Achievement:** Nobel Prize in Physics (1957) - Youngest science laureate post-WWII at age 30
- **Work:** Parity violation in weak interactions (with Chen Ning Yang)
- **Connection:** Ethnic Korean ancestry; made significant contributions to particle physics

#### 2. **Samuel C.C. Ting (丁肇中)**
- **Birth:** 1936 (Ann Arbor, Michigan, USA)
- **Achievement:** Nobel Prize in Physics (1976)
- **Work:** Discovery of the J/ψ particle
- **Current Position:** Thomas Dudley Cabot Professor at MIT
- **Major Project:** Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer (AMS) on International Space Station

## 6. Physics Research Areas of Strength

### 1. Condensed Matter Physics
- **Strongest research area in Korean physics**
- Key institutions: SNU, KAIST, POSTECH, IBS Centers
- Focus areas:
  - 2D materials (graphene, transition metal dichalcogenides)
  - Quantum materials
  - Strongly correlated electron systems
  - Topological insulators
  - Superconductivity
  - Spintronics

### 2. Particle Physics and Cosmology
- IBS Center for Theoretical Physics of the Universe
- Active participation in CERN experiments (CMS, ATLAS)
- Dark matter research (axion experiments, underground physics)
- Neutrino physics
- Cosmology and early universe studies

### 3. Quantum Information Science
- Rapidly growing field
- Key centers:
  - IBS Center for Quantum Nanoscience
  - IBS Center for Trapped Ion Quantum Science
  - DGIST Quantum Computing research
- Focus: Quantum computing, quantum sensing, quantum communication

### 4. Nanophysics and Nanomaterials
- Strong industrial-academic collaboration
- Samsung partnership with SKKU
- Key areas:
  - Carbon nanotubes and graphene
  - Nanofabrication
  - Quantum dots
  - Molecular electronics

### 5. Nuclear and Fusion Energy
- KSTAR tokamak research
- ITER contributions
- KAERI nuclear research programs
- Rare isotope research (RAON facility)

### 6. AMO (Atomic, Molecular, Optical) Physics
- Ultrafast laser physics
- Attosecond science
- Quantum optics

### 7. Biophysics
- Single molecule biophysics

## 7. International Collaborations

### CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research)
- **Korea's Role:** Observer state, major collaborator
- **Experiments:**
  - CMS (Compact Muon Solenoid) - Korean groups participate in detector construction and data analysis
  - ATLAS - Korean contributions to detector upgrades
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Detector components
  - Computing resources
  - Physics analysis
  - Higgs boson research participation

### ITER (International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor)
- **Location:** Cadarache, France
- **Korea's Role:** 7th member party (joined 2003)
- **Contribution Share:** 9.1% of construction costs
- **ITER Korea:** Established 2007 under Korea Institute of Fusion Energy
- **Procurement Responsibilities:**
  - Four sectors of vacuum vessel
  - Blanket shield block
  - Thermal shields
  - Tritium storage and delivery system
- **Domestic Agency:** Based in Daejeon

## 10. Summary: Why South Korea is a Rising Physics Power

South Korea's rapid ascent in physics research is driven by:

1. **Substantial Government Investment:** Consistent funding increases for basic science through IBS and other programs

2. **World-Class Facilities:** KSTAR, PAL-XFEL, and soon RAON provide unique research capabilities

3. **Strategic International Partnerships:** Active participation in ITER, CERN, and other major collaborations

4. **Talent Attraction:** IBS Centers attract international scientists (30% of researchers are foreign)

5. **Industry-Academia Integration:** Strong partnerships with Samsung, Hyundai, and other conglomerates

6. **Education Excellence:** Top-ranked universities producing high-quality graduates

7. **Research Freedom:** IBS Centers operate without fixed timeframes, enabling long-term fundamental research

8. **Focus on Emerging Fields:** Leadership in quantum computing, 2D materials, and quantum materials

With continued investment and strategic planning, South Korea is positioned to become one of the world's leading nations in physics research within the next decade.
