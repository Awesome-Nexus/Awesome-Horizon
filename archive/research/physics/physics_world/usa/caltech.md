## History

Caltech's physics program has its roots in the early 20th century when astronomer George Ellery Hale and physicist Robert Andrews Millikan shaped the institution's scientific direction. The Norman Bridge Laboratory of Physics, opened in 1922, marked the beginning of Caltech's dedicated physics research facilities.

### Key Historical Milestones

- **1920**: Throop University renamed California Institute of Technology
- **1922-1925**: Norman Bridge Laboratory of Physics constructed and expanded
- **1923**: Robert A. Millikan awarded Nobel Prize in Physics (Caltech's first physics Nobel)
- **1932**: W. K. Kellogg Radiation Laboratory opened
- **1969**: George W. Downs Laboratory and C. C. Lauritsen Laboratory opened
- **2000**: Institute for Quantum Information (IQI) established, later becoming IQIM in 2011
- **2014**: Walter Burke Institute for Theoretical Physics founded
- **2015**: LIGO makes first direct detection of gravitational waves
- **2017**: Barry Barish and Kip Thorne awarded Nobel Prize in Physics for LIGO
- **2024**: John Hopfield (former Caltech faculty) awarded Nobel Prize in Physics for neural network work

## Research Areas and Specializations

### 1. Experimental Elementary Particle Physics
- **Focus**: Discovering new particles and interactions beyond the Standard Model
- **Key Experiments**:
  - **CMS** at CERN's Large Hadron Collider (searching for dark matter, new particles up to TeV scales)
  - **NOvA and DUNE** at Fermilab (neutrino oscillations, mass patterns, CP violation)
  - **Mu2e** (charged lepton flavor violation)
  - **LDMX** (accelerator-produced dark matter search)
  - **SuperCDMS** (direct dark matter detection)

### 2. Theoretical Elementary Particle Physics
- String theory and unification of interactions
- Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) and hadron properties
- Quantum properties of black holes
- Cosmology, dark matter, and dark energy
- Mathematical physics

### 3. Condensed Matter Physics
- Correlated electron systems
- Topological quantum matter
- Quantum dynamics and entanglement
- High-temperature superconductivity
- Graphene and carbon nanotube systems
- Nanomechanical and nanoelectronic systems
- Quantum electromechanics
- Phase transitions and disordered systems

### 4. Quantum Optics and Information
- Quantum computation and quantum information theory
- Cavity quantum electrodynamics
- Quantum algorithms and error correction
- Quantum communication and networks
- **HQ**: Institute for Quantum Information and Matter (IQIM)

### 5. Experimental Atomic/Molecular/Optical (AMO) Physics
- Ultracold atom arrays for quantum information and many-body physics
- Precision measurements in cold polar molecules
- Atom-light interactions in photonic crystals
- Hybrid superconducting quantum circuits
- Optomechanical sensors
- Neurophotonics and neuromolecular sensing

### 6. Nuclear Physics
- Precision measurements of free neutron decay
- Neutron electric dipole moment measurements
- Searches for physics beyond the Standard Model

### 7. Astrophysics
- **High-energy astrophysics**: X-ray and gamma-ray astronomy (Space Radiation Laboratory)
- **Cosmic ray physics**: Composition and origin of energetic particles
- **Millimeter/submillimeter astronomy**: Star/planet formation, galaxies (Caltech Submillimeter Observatory)
- **Galactic compact objects**: Black holes, neutron stars, white dwarfs

### 8. Theoretical Astrophysics (TAPIR)
- Planets, stars, and stellar evolution
- Black holes and neutron star interactions
- Gravitational-wave astrophysics
- Cosmology and galaxy formation
- Numerical and analytical general relativity

### 9. Cosmology
- Cosmic microwave background radiation measurements
- Large scale structure studies
- Dark matter direct detection
- Dark energy research

### 10. Gravitational-Wave Physics and Astrophysics
- LIGO observations and data analysis
- Binary black hole and neutron star mergers
- Future LISA mission preparations

### Off-Campus and Affiliated Facilities

#### LIGO (Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory)
- **Operator**: Caltech and MIT jointly operate for NSF
- **Detectors**: Two 4-km interferometers in Hanford, Washington and Livingston, Louisiana
- **Research Center**: Caltech houses one of two primary research centers
- **Achievement**: First direct detection of gravitational waves (2015), 2017 Nobel Prize
- **Current Status**: Advanced LIGO operations with sensitivity improvements ongoing

#### Observatories
- **Palomar Observatory** (San Diego County)
- **W. M. Keck Observatory** (Mauna Kea, Hawaii) - 31+ years of operation
- **Owens Valley Radio Observatory**
- **Caltech Submillimeter Observatory** (currently idle, planned relocation)

### Research Institutes

#### Institute for Quantum Information and Matter (IQIM)
- **Founded**: 2000 (as IQI), expanded 2011
- **Status**: NSF Physics Frontiers Center
- **Faculty**: 30+ members from Physics, Applied Physics, EAS, Computer Science
- **Research Thrusts**:
  1. Quantum Information
  2. Quantum Many-Body Physics
  3. Quantum Optics
  4. Mechanical Quantum Systems
- **Notable Alumni**: Many leaders in quantum computing at Google, IBM, etc.

#### Walter Burke Institute for Theoretical Physics
- **Founded**: 2014
- **Endowment**: $70+ million
- **Director**: Hirosi Ooguri
- **Research Areas**: High energy physics, condensed matter, astrophysics, cosmology
- **Named For**: Walter Burke, Caltech trustee and Sherman Fairchild Foundation president

#### Space Radiation Laboratory (SRL)
- X-ray and gamma-ray detector development
- Cosmic ray research
- Space-based astrophysics missions

#### Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL)
- NASA-funded research center managed by Caltech
- Collaborations on space-based physics experiments
- Detector technology development

## Graduate Program

### Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.)

**Duration**: Typically 5-6 years (minimum 12 terms of enrollment)

**Program Structure**:
1. **Coursework**: Advanced courses in at least three subfields of physics
2. **Candidacy Exam**: Written exams in classical physics and quantum mechanics
3. **Research Phase**: Join a research group and conduct independent research
4. **Thesis**: Write and publicly defend a doctoral thesis
5. **Publications**: Expected to publish in peer-reviewed journals

**Research Opportunities**:
- Elementary particle physics
- Nuclear physics
- Cosmic-ray, gamma-ray, and X-ray astronomy
- Submillimeter astronomy
- Condensed-matter physics
- Atomic/molecular/optical physics
- Quantum information
- Gravitational physics and cosmology
- Astrophysics and theoretical physics

**Admission Requirements** (Fall 2026):
- Bachelor's degree in physics or equivalent
- GRE General Test: **Optional**
- GRE Physics Subject Test: **Strongly Recommended**
- Transcripts, 3 letters of recommendation, CV, personal statement
- Strong undergraduate preparation in:
  - Classical Mechanics (Goldstein level)
  - Electromagnetism (Reitz and Milford level)
  - Quantum Mechanics (Dicke and Wittke level)
  - Advanced Calculus (Apostol level)

**Financial Support**: Full funding for all Ph.D. students (tuition, stipend, health insurance)

### Master of Science (M.S.)
- Not a terminal degree option for most students
- Awarded along the way to Ph.D. after completing coursework requirements
- Minimum 135 units of graduate work required

## Undergraduate Program

### Physics Option (Major)

**Degree**: B.S. in Physics

**Curriculum Highlights**:
- Strong foundation in theoretical and applied physics
- Small class sizes (Caltech has ~1,000 undergraduate students total)
- Research opportunities starting from freshman year
- Close interaction with faculty and graduate students

**Admission**:
- Highly selective (~3-4% acceptance rate)
- Requires SAT or ACT scores
- Strong emphasis on STEM preparation
- Letters of recommendation required (1 STEM, 1 Humanities/Social Science)

**Notable Features**:
- Undergraduate research is strongly encouraged
- Many undergraduates co-author papers with faculty
- Access to world-class facilities including JPL
- Senior thesis option

## Recent Achievements and Breakthroughs

### 2024-2025 Highlights

1. **2024 Nobel Prize in Physics**
   - John Hopfield (former Caltech faculty, 1980-1996) awarded for foundational work on neural networks
   - 48th Nobel Prize for Caltech faculty/alumni/postdocs

2. **2025 Breakthrough Prize in Fundamental Physics**
   - 13,500+ LHC scientists honored, including Caltech CMS team
   - Recognized for precision tests of the Standard Model
   - Caltech team led by Maria Spiropulu and Harvey Newman contributed to Higgs boson discovery (2012)

3. **LIGO 10-Year Anniversary**
   - October 2025 marked 10 years since first gravitational wave detection
   - Celebratory talks by Kip Thorne, Rana Adhikari, and others

4. **Quark Model 60th Anniversary**
   - 2024 marked 60 years since Murray Gell-Mann introduced quarks (1964)

5. **IQIM 25th Anniversary**
   - Celebrating 25 years of quantum research at Caltech (founded 2000)

### Historic Breakthroughs

- **2015**: LIGO detects gravitational waves from binary black hole merger (GW150914)
- **2017**: First neutron star merger observed in gravitational waves and light (GW170817)
- **2012**: Higgs boson discovery at LHC with major Caltech CMS contribution
- **1964**: Quark model proposed by Murray Gell-Mann

## Summary

Caltech's Department of Physics stands as one of the premier physics institutions globally, combining a rich historical legacy with cutting-edge modern research. With 48 Nobel Prizes associated with the institute, world-class facilities including LIGO and the Keck Observatory, and pioneering work in quantum information through IQIM, Caltech continues to push the boundaries of fundamental physics. The department's small size fosters an intimate, collaborative environment where undergraduates and graduate students work alongside world-renowned faculty on everything from the smallest elementary particles to the largest structures in the universe.
