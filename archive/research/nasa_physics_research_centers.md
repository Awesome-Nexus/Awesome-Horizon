# NASA Physics Research Centers and Programs

## Executive Summary

NASA operates 10 major field centers across the United States, each contributing unique capabilities to the agency's physics research portfolio. This document provides comprehensive information about physics research centers, their divisions, major missions, international collaborations, and notable discoveries.

| **First Direct Exoplanet Image** | 2008 | Hubble/Keck | Fomalhaut b, HR 8799 planets |

| **Gravitational Lensing of Distant Galaxies** | 1990s-present | Hubble, JWST | Study of dark matter distribution |
| **First Exoplanet Atmosphere Detection** | 2001 | Hubble | Sodium in HD 209458b atmosphere |
| **Dark Energy Confirmation** | 2000s-present | Hubble, ground supernovae surveys | Understanding cosmic acceleration |
| **JWST Early Galaxy Observations** | 2022-present | James Webb Space Telescope | Galaxies earlier and more massive than expected |
| **First Neutron Star Merger (GW170817)** | 2017 | Fermi, LIGO, Virgo | Multi-messenger astronomy breakthrough |
| **Pulsar Wind Nebulae** | 2000s | Chandra | Understanding pulsar environments |
| **Supermassive Black Hole in Milky Way** | 2002-2022 | Various, culminating in EHT | Sagittarius A* imaging (Nobel Prize 2020) |
