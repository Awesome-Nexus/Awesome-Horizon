### Notable Physics Facilities

#### SESAME (Synchrotron-light for Experimental Science and Applications in the Middle East)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Location** | Allan, Balqa Governorate, Jordan |
| **Established** | May 30, 2002 (under UNESCO); Opened May 16, 2017 |
| **Website** | https://www.sesame.org.jo/ |
| **Energy** | 2.5 GeV |
| **Member States** | Cyprus, Egypt, Iran, Israel, Jordan, Pakistan, Palestine, Turkey |
| **Observers** | Brazil, Canada, China, CERN, EU, France, Germany, Greece, Italy, Japan, Kuwait, Portugal, Russia, Spain, Sweden, UAE, UK, USA |
| **Director** | Khaled Toukan |
| **Annual Budget** | $6 million |
| **Unique Feature** | World's first large accelerator powered entirely by renewable energy |

**SESAME Beamlines (as of 2024):**

| Beamline | Status | Purpose |
|----------|--------|---------|
| IR (Infrared) Spectromicroscopy | Operational since Nov 2018 | Materials science, biology |
| XAFS/XRF | Operational since July 2018 | X-ray absorption, fluorescence |
| MS/XPD (Materials Science/X-ray Powder Diffraction) | Operational since Dec 2020 | Materials characterization |
| BEATS (Tomography) | Operational since Feb 2024 | 3D imaging |
| HESEB | Operational since Feb 2024 | Helmholtz-SESAME beamline |
| TXPES (Turkish beamline) | Inaugurated Dec 2025 | Photoelectron spectroscopy |
| MX (Macromolecular Crystallography) | In design | Protein crystallography |
| SAXS/WAXS | Planned | Scattering experiments |
