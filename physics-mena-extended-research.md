## 1. Iraq

### University of Baghdad - College of Science

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Country** | Iraq |
| **City** | Baghdad |
| **URL** | https://www.uobaghdad.edu.iq |
| **Established** | 1959 (University); 1949 (College of Science) |

**Description:**
The University of Baghdad is the largest university in Iraq and the tenth largest in the Arab world. The College of Science houses the Physics Department, which has historically been a center for scientific research in Iraq. The university suffered significantly during the 2003 invasion and subsequent conflicts, with over 90% student dropout rates in some classes during the occupation period.

**Notable Programs:**
- B.Sc. in Physics
- M.Sc. and Ph.D. in various physics specializations
- Research areas include: Condensed matter physics, Nuclear physics, Laser physics, and Geophysics
- The university operates the Institute of Laser for Postgraduate Studies at the Al-Jadriya campus

**Key Features:**
- Four campuses across Baghdad: Al-Jadriyah, Bab al-Muadhdham, Wazeriya, and Nahda Road
- The College of Science building was designed by Walter Gropius and The Architects' Collaborative
- First listed in Times Higher Education World University Rankings in September 2018
- Ranked 741-750 in QS World University Rankings 2026

## 2. Syria

### Damascus University - Faculty of Sciences

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Country** | Syria |
| **City** | Damascus |
| **URL** | http://www.damascusuniversity.edu.sy |
| **Established** | 1923 (University); Faculty of Sciences founded 1946 |

**Description:**
Damascus University is the largest and oldest university in Syria, founded as the Syrian University in 1923. Before the Syrian civil war (2011), it was one of the most reputable universities in the Arab World. The Faculty of Sciences includes a Physics Department that has historically been a center for physics education and research in Syria.

**Current Status:**
- The university has experienced significant disruptions due to the ongoing conflict
- On 15 January 2013, 82 people were killed during the Aleppo University bombings (affecting sister institutions)
- Despite challenges, the university continues to operate
- Ranked in Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2024 (+1501 band)
- The only Syrian university ranked in THE World University Rankings

**Notable Programs:**
- B.Sc., M.Sc., and Ph.D. in Physics
- Higher Institute of Laser Research and Applications
- Higher Institute of Seismologic Studies and Research
- Research areas: Laser physics, Nuclear physics, and Seismology

**Notable Alumni:**
- Mahmoud Abbas (President of Palestine)
- Duraid Lahham (B.Sc. in Chemistry and Physics)
- Several Syrian Prime Ministers and political figures

- Military raids and arrests of students
- Resource limitations due to occupation
