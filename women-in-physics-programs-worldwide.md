## 1. International Programs

### 1.1 IUPAP Working Group on Women in Physics (WG5)
- **URL:** https://iupap.org/wg/wg5/
- **Region:** International (Global)
- **Description:** The International Union of Pure and Applied Physics (IUPAP) Working Group 5 is dedicated to the study and promotion of women in physics worldwide. It surveys the present situation of women physicists, analyzes problems, and recommends solutions. The working group organizes the International Conference on Women in Physics (ICWIP) every three years.
- **Type:** International Organization, Advocacy

### 1.2 International Conference on Women in Physics (ICWIP)
- **URL:** https://iupap.org/wg/wg5/icip/
- **Region:** International (Rotating Host Countries)
- **Description:** Held every three years since 2002, ICWIP brings together women physicists from around the world to discuss challenges, share best practices, and develop strategies to increase women's participation in physics. Past conferences have been held in countries including UK, Brazil, South Korea, USA, Canada, and India.
- **Type:** International Conference

### 1.3 Organization for Women in Science for the Developing World (OWSD)
- **URL:** https://owsd.net/
- **Region:** International (Focus on Developing Countries)
- **Description:** Formerly TWOWS, OWSD is a program unit of UNESCO and based at TWAS. It provides research training, career development, and networking opportunities for women scientists from developing countries. Offers PhD fellowships and early career fellowships for women in STEM including physics.
- **Type:** International Organization, Fellowships

### 1.4 L'Oréal-UNESCO For Women in Science International Awards
- **URL:** https://www.forwomeninscience.com/
- **Region:** International (Global)
- **Description:** Created in 1998, this program honors five eminent women scientists each year from five different regions of the world. It also includes the International Rising Talents program for young women scientists and national/regional fellowship programs in over 100 countries.
- **Type:** Awards, Fellowships

### 1.5 UNESCO Women in STEM Initiative
- **URL:** https://www.unesco.org/en/gender-equality/education/stem
- **Region:** International (Global)
- **Description:** UNESCO works to promote gender equality in STEM education and careers globally. They publish reports on the STEM gender gap, support policy development, and coordinate the International Day of Women and Girls in Science (February 11).
- **Type:** International Organization, Advocacy

### 1.6 International Day of Women and Girls in Science
- **URL:** https://www.unesco.org/en/days/women-girls-science
- **Region:** International (Global)
- **Description:** Observed annually on February 11, this UN-recognized day promotes full and equal access to and participation in science for women and girls. Physics institutions worldwide organize events and activities to celebrate women physicists.
- **Type:** International Event

### 1.7 CERN Diversity and Inclusion Programs
- **URL:** https://home.cern/about/who-we-are/diversity
- **Region:** International (Europe-based, Global participation)
- **Description:** CERN operates several initiatives to support women in physics including the Women in Technology group, shadowing programs, and the Early Career, Gender & Diversity Office (ECGD) for LHCb collaboration. CERN also sends researchers to schools to inspire young women into science.
- **Type:** Research Institution Programs

### 1.8 European Platform of Women Scientists (EPWS)
- **URL:** https://epws.org/
- **Region:** Europe (EU-focused)
- **Description:** EPWS is an international non-profit organization representing the needs, concerns, interests, and aspirations of women scientists in Europe. It provides a platform for networking, advocacy, and policy recommendations to the European Commission.
- **Type:** Regional Network, Advocacy

## 2. Regional Networks

### 2.1 Nordic Network for Diversity in Physics (NORNDiP)
- **URL:** https://www.norndip.net/
- **Region:** Nordic Countries (Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, Sweden)
- **Description:** A network of women physicists from Nordic countries promoting diversity in physics with support from NordForsk. They organize an annual conference bringing together researchers, educators, and students to explore cutting-edge physics research and discuss gender equality.
- **Type:** Regional Network, Conference

### 2.2 European Platform of Women Scientists (EPWS)
- **URL:** https://epws.org/
- **Region:** Europe
- **Description:** An international non-profit organization that represents the needs and aspirations of women scientists in Europe. EPWS provides networking opportunities, policy advocacy, and works with the European Commission on gender equality in science.
- **Type:** Regional Network, Advocacy

### 2.3 Women in Physics in South Africa (WiPiSA)
- **URL:** https://saip.org.za/WIPISA/ (via SAIP)
- **Region:** South Africa
- **Description:** A forum under the South African Institute of Physics (SAIP) formed to address the underrepresentation of women in physics. WiPiSA organizes workshops, conferences, and receives government funding from the Department of Science and Technology.
- **Type:** National Network, Advocacy

### 2.4 Society of Japanese Women Scientists (SJWS)
- **URL:** https://sjws.or.jp/old/english/index.html
- **Region:** Japan
- **Description:** Established in 1958, SJWS fosters friendship among female scientists and facilitates knowledge exchange. The organization works to improve the status of women researchers in Japan and collaborates with the Physical Society of Japan on gender initiatives.
- **Type:** National Organization, Network

### 2.5 Indian Women Scientists' Association (IWSA)
- **URL:** https://iwsa.net/
- **Region:** India
- **Description:** Founded in 1973, IWSA is a voluntary, non-profit organization serving Indian women scientists. With eleven branches across India, IWSA runs hostels for working women, organizes science camps for children, and provides networking and advocacy for women in STEM including physics.
- **Type:** National Organization, Network

### 2.6 Women in STEMM Australia
- **URL:** https://womeninstemm.au/
- **Region:** Australia
- **Description:** An organization that connects, empowers, and advocates for STEMM women and girls to have equality in the Australian workplace. They provide networking opportunities, mentorship, and advocacy for women in physics and related fields.
- **Type:** National Network, Advocacy

## 3. National-Level Women in Physics Committees

### 3.1 APS Committee on the Status of Women in Physics (CSWP)
- **URL:** https://www.aps.org/about/governance/committees/cswp
- **Region:** United States
- **Description:** The American Physical Society's CSWP provides guidance for APS activities related to women in physics. It conducts site visits to institutions, publishes reports on gender equity, administers grants for Women in Physics groups, and organizes the Conferences for Undergraduate Women in Physics (CUWiP).
- **Type:** Professional Society Committee, Advocacy

### 3.2 IOP Women in Physics Group
- **URL:** https://www.iop.org/physics-community/special-interest-groups/women-physics-group
- **Region:** United Kingdom
- **Description:** A Special Interest Group of the Institute of Physics focused on the careers of women in physics. The group organizes events, provides networking opportunities, awards the Bell Burnell Prize, and contributes to UK government inquiries on diversity in STEM.
- **Type:** Professional Society Group, Network

### 3.3 German Physical Society (DPG) Women in Physics
- **URL:** https://www.dpg-physik.de/
- **Region:** Germany
- **Description:** The DPG supports women in physics through various initiatives including the National Pact for Women in MINT Careers ("Komm, mach MINT"). German physics institutions participate in this network to increase women's participation in physics and STEM fields.
- **Type:** Professional Society, National Initiative

### 3.4 Italian Physical Society (SIF) Gender Initiatives
- **URL:** https://en.sif.it/
- **Region:** Italy
- **Description:** The Italian Physical Society works with the National Institute for Nuclear Physics (INFN) on the "More Women in Physics" competition, which awards female physics students to promote their presence in physics research and education.
- **Type:** Professional Society, Advocacy

### 3.5 Brazilian Physical Society (SBF) Gender Equity Working Group
- **URL:** https://www.sbfisica.org.br/
- **Region:** Brazil
- **Description:** SBF has established a Gender Equity Working Group (GT-Gênero) to address underrepresentation of women in Brazilian physics. The society tracks female participation in events and publishes reports on gender equity in the physics community.
- **Type:** Professional Society Committee, Advocacy

### 3.6 Canadian Association of Physicists (CAP) Equity Initiatives
- **URL:** https://www.cap.ca/
- **Region:** Canada
- **Description:** CAP works on gender equity initiatives including the CAP-CRM Prize in Theoretical and Mathematical Physics, which recognizes excellence including contributions from women physicists. CAP also participates in the TRIUMF diversity initiatives.
- **Type:** Professional Society, Advocacy

### 3.7 French Physical Society (SFP) Women in Physics
- **URL:** https://www.sfpnet.fr/
- **Region:** France
- **Description:** The Société Française de Physique promotes women in physics through various initiatives including awards, conference programming, and participation in national efforts to increase women's representation in STEM (Femmes et Sciences).
- **Type:** Professional Society, Advocacy

### 3.8 Australian Institute of Physics (AIP) Women in Physics
- **URL:** https://www.aip.org.au/
- **Region:** Australia
- **Description:** The AIP works with the Australian Government Women in STEM Ambassador and supports various initiatives to increase women's participation in physics, including school outreach programs and awards recognizing women physicists.
- **Type:** Professional Society, Advocacy

### 3.9 Physical Society of Japan (JPS) Gender Initiatives
- **URL:** https://www.jps.or.jp/
- **Region:** Japan
- **Description:** The JPS has established committees to address gender equality and increase the percentage of women members. They organize the Fumiko Yonezawa Memorial Prize for female physicists and participate in international gender equity initiatives.
- **Type:** Professional Society, Advocacy

## 5. Mentorship Programs

### 5.1 APS CSWP Mentor-Mentee Matching Program
- **URL:** https://www.aps.org/about/governance/committees/cswp
- **Region:** United States
- **Description:** The Committee on the Status of Women in Physics facilitates mentorship connections between women physicists at different career stages. The program helps early-career physicists navigate academic and professional challenges.
- **Type:** Mentorship Program

### 5.2 SPIE Women in Optics Mentorship
- **URL:** https://spie.org/career-advancement/diversity-equity-inclusion/women-in-optics
- **Region:** International
- **Description:** SPIE offers mentorship programs specifically for women in optics and photonics, connecting early-career professionals with established scientists in the field.
- **Type:** Professional Society Mentorship

### 5.3 500 Women Scientists Mentorship Program
- **URL:** https://www.500womenscientists.org/
- **Region:** International
- **Description:** A grassroots organization with local pods worldwide that provide mentorship, networking, and advocacy for women in STEM including physics. They offer a request-a-scientist platform for connecting with women experts.
- **Type:** Grassroots Network, Mentorship

### 5.4 Society of Women Engineers (SWE) Mentoring
- **URL:** https://swe.org/
- **Region:** International (US-based)
- **Description:** While focused on engineering, SWE's mentorship programs support women in related physical sciences. They offer both formal and informal mentoring opportunities for students and professionals.
- **Type:** Professional Society Mentorship

### 5.5 Women in Technology at CERN (WIT)
- **URL:** https://wit-community.web.cern.ch/
- **Region:** International (Switzerland-based)
- **Description:** WIT provides mentorship and networking for women working at CERN across all career stages. They organize shadowing programs, career talks, and networking events specifically for women in physics and technology.

## 6. Scholarships and Fellowships

### 6.1 APS Women in Physics Group Grants
- **URL:** https://www.aps.org/funding-recognition/grant/women-group-grants
- **Region:** United States
- **Description:** Annual grants of up to $1,000 for student-led Women in Physics groups at colleges and universities. These grants support activities that improve recruitment and retention of undergraduate women in physics.
- **Type:** Grant Program

### 6.2 Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA)
- **URL:** https://marie-sklodowska-curie-actions.ec.europa.eu/
- **Region:** Europe
- **Description:** EU-funded fellowships for researchers at all career stages. Named after the pioneering physicist, these fellowships support mobility and career development for women and men in physics research across Europe.
- **Type:** Fellowship Program

### 6.3 Lise Meitner Fellowship
- **URL:** https://www.fwf.ac.at/en/
- **Region:** Austria
- **Description:** The Austrian Science Fund (FWF) offers the Lise Meitner program supporting excellent female researchers in all disciplines including physics. The fellowship enables women to lead independent research projects.
- **Type:** Fellowship Program

### 6.4 L'Oréal-UNESCO National Fellowships
- **URL:** https://www.forwomeninscience.com/
- **Region:** Multiple Countries (100+ nations)
- **Description:** National and regional fellowship programs for early-career women scientists including physicists. These fellowships provide research funding, recognition, and networking opportunities.
- **Type:** Fellowship Program

### 6.5 OWSD PhD Fellowships
- **URL:** https://owsd.net/
- **Region:** Developing Countries
- **Description:** Fellowships for women scientists from developing countries to pursue PhD studies in STEM fields including physics. Funded by the Swedish International Development Cooperation Agency (Sida).
- **Type:** Fellowship Program

### 6.6 OWSD Early Career Fellowship
- **URL:** https://owsd.net/
- **Region:** Developing Countries
- **Description:** Fellowship program supporting women who have completed their PhDs in STEM to establish independent research careers at their home institutions in developing countries.
- **Type:** Fellowship Program

### 6.7 INFN "More Women in Physics" Awards
- **URL:** https://www.infn.it/
- **Region:** Italy
- **Description:** The National Institute for Nuclear Physics (INFN) offers awards and scholarships to female physics students through the "More Women in Physics" (Più Donne nella Fisica) competition.
- **Type:** Award Program

### 6.8 Fumiko Yonezawa Memorial Prize
- **URL:** https://www.jps.or.jp/
- **Region:** Japan
- **Description:** Annual prize recognizing distinguished achievements by female scientists in physics. Named after the first female president of the Physical Society of Japan, the award promotes women's participation in physics.
- **Type:** Award Program

### 6.9 Daphne Jackson Fellowship
- **URL:** https://daphnejackson.org/
- **Region:** United Kingdom
- **Description:** Fellowships for scientists returning to research after a career break of two years or more, with many recipients in physics. The program provides retraining and support for women returning to STEM careers.
- **Type:** Returner Fellowship

### 6.10 Schlumberger Foundation Faculty for the Future Fellowships
- **URL:** https://www.slb.com/about/diversity-and-inclusion/education/schlumberger-foundation
- **Region:** Developing Countries
- **Description:** Fellowships for women from developing and emerging economies to pursue PhD or postdoctoral studies in STEM including physics at leading universities worldwide.
- **Type:** Fellowship Program
