## 1. Space Telescopes & Missions (2024-2025 Updates)

### James Webb Space Telescope (JWST)
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Launch** | December 25, 2021 |
| **Operator** | NASA / ESA / CSA |
| **Wavelength** | Infrared (0.6-28.3 μm) |
| **Major Discoveries (2024-2025)** | Galaxy MoM-z14 (280 million years after Big Bang); exotic helium-carbon atmospheres in exoplanets; TRAPPIST-1 system atmospheric studies; protostar EC-53 observations |
| **Data Access** | [MAST Archive](https://archive.stsci.edu/), [JWST Data Home](https://science.nasa.gov/mission/webb/) |
| **Status** | Fully operational, Cycle 3 & 4 observations ongoing |

- **Key 2025 Result:** JWST observed galaxy MoM-z14, appearing only 280 million years after the Big Bang - pushing the boundaries of observable universe closer to cosmic dawn
- **Exoplanet Breakthrough:** Detection of PSR J2322-2650b with exotic helium-and-carbon-dominated atmosphere unlike any previously seen
- **Awards:** Received SPIE 2026 George W. Goddard Award for Space and Airborne Optics

## 7. Latest Cosmology Results (2024-2025)

### CMB Results

#### Planck (Legacy)
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Mission** | ESA CMB satellite (2009-2013) |
| **Legacy Status** | Reference CMB temperature measurements |
| **Key Parameters** | H₀ = 67.4 ± 0.5 km/s/Mpc, Ωₘ = 0.315 ± 0.007 |

#### ACT (Atacama Cosmology Telescope) - FINAL RESULTS
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Final Data Release** | DR6 (March 2025) |
| **Status** | Decommissioned (operations 2008-2022) |
| **Key Finding** | Hubble constant consistent with Planck |

- **2025 Results:**
  - Highest resolution CMB polarization measurements to date
  - 5× resolution of Planck; 3× sensitivity improvement
  - Stringent tests of ΛCDM cosmology confirm standard model
  - No evidence for non-standard dark energy from primary CMB
- **Successor:** Simons Observatory now operating on same site

#### SPT (South Pole Telescope)
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Latest Release** | SPT-3G D1 (June 2025) |
| **Coverage** | 1500 deg² at 95, 150, 220 GHz |

- **SPT-3G D1 Results:**
  - Temperature and E-mode polarization power spectra
  - Up to 35% uncertainty reduction vs. previous releases
  - New benchmarks for ground-based CMB cosmology
  - Combined CMB-SPA (Planck+ACT+SPT): nₛ = 0.9684 ± 0.0030

### Hubble Constant Tension (2025 Update)

| Measurement Method | H₀ Value | Source |
|-------------------|----------|--------|
| **Early Universe (CMB)** | 67.4 ± 0.5 km/s/Mpc | Planck |
| **Late Universe (Cepheids/SN Ia)** | 73-74 km/s/Mpc | SH0ES (Riess et al.) |
| **JWST Independent** | 70.4 ± 3% km/s/Mpc | Chicago-Carnegie Hubble Program (June 2025) |
| **Time-delay Lensing** | ~73-76 km/s/Mpc | H0LiCOW/TTK, TDCOSMO (December 2025) |

- **December 2025 Developments:**
  - New time-delay lensing measurements from Keck Observatory strengthen tension
  - Local measurements consistently higher than CMB predictions
  - JWST CCHP measurement falls between CMB and local values with larger uncertainty
  - **Status:** Tension remains one of cosmology's most pressing mysteries

- **Possible Explanations:**
  - Systematic errors in distance ladder
  - New physics beyond ΛCDM
  - Modified gravity at cosmic scales

### Notable 2025 Cosmology Papers

1. **ACT DR6 Final Release:** Most stringent ΛCDM tests to date; standard model passes with no evidence for new physics
2. **SPT-3G D1 Release:** Precision cosmology from ground-based CMB; combined constraints with Planck and ACT
3. **JWST H₀ Measurement:** Independent distance ladder with potentially reduced systematics
4. **Hubble Tension Confirmation:** Multiple independent local probes consistently find H₀ ~73 km/s/Mpc
