## Caribbean Islands

### Cuba

#### University of Havana (Universidad de La Habana)
- **Location:** Vedado district, Havana
- **Founded:** 1728 (oldest university in Cuba, one of the first in the Americas)
- **Website:** [www.uh.cu](https://www.uh.cu)
- **Students:** ~60,000
- **Physics Programs:**
  - Faculty of Physics (Facultad de Física)
  - Undergraduate and graduate programs in physics
  - Research in theoretical and applied physics
  - Strong connections with mathematics and computer science
- **Research Areas:** Theoretical physics, condensed matter physics, optics, and materials science
- **Notable Features:** One of the most prestigious universities in Latin America; extensive research output in physics and related sciences

#### CUJAE - Technological University of Havana (Universidad Tecnológica de la Habana José Antonio Echeverría)
- **Location:** Marianao, Havana
- **Founded:** 1964
- **Website:** [www.cujae.edu.cu](https://www.cujae.edu.cu)
- **Students:** ~7,500
- **Physics-Related Programs:**
  - Faculty of Electrical Engineering
  - Faculty of Automation and Biomedical Engineering
  - Faculty of Telecommunications Engineering
  - Geophysical Engineering program
  - Research centers in renewable energy, microelectronics, and biomedical engineering
- **Research Centers:**
  - CETER - Center for Studies on Renewable Energy Technologies (1992)
  - CIME - Center for Microelectronics Research (1969)
  - CIPEL - Center for Electro-Energy Research and Tests (1988)
  - CEMAT - Center for Mathematical Studies for Technical Sciences (2000)
- **Notable Features:** Designated "University of Excellence" in 2017; strong focus on engineering physics and applied sciences

#### Other Cuban Physics Research
- **Institute of Physics:** Part of the Cuban Academy of Sciences
- **Center for Theoretical Physics:** Research in theoretical physics and mathematical physics
- **International Collaborations:** Strong ties with Latin American, European, and Asian universities

#### Arecibo Observatory (Historic)
- **Location:** Arecibo, Puerto Rico
- **Operated:** 1963-2020 (collapsed December 1, 2020)
- **Former Website:** [www.naic.edu](https://www.naic.edu)
- **Historical Significance:**
  - World's largest single-aperture telescope (305m) for 53 years
  - Nobel Prize-winning research: Russell Hulse and Joseph Taylor discovered the first binary pulsar in 1974 (Nobel Prize in Physics 1993)
  - Key facility for radio astronomy, planetary radar, and ionospheric research
- **Current Status:** NSF announced telescope will not be rebuilt; Arecibo C3 (Arecibo Center for Culturally Relevant and Inclusive Science Education) established as educational center
- **Legacy:** Contributed to gravitational wave research, near-Earth object studies, and the search for extraterrestrial intelligence (SETI)

## Regional Networks and Collaborations

### Caribbean Physics Networks

- **University of the West Indies (UWI) System:** Regional university serving 18 Caribbean territories with physics programs at Mona (Jamaica), St. Augustine (Trinidad), Cave Hill (Barbados), Five Islands (Antigua), and Open Campus
- **Caribbean Accreditation Authority for Education in Medicine and Other Health Professions (CAAM-HP):** Accredits physics-related health programs
- **Caribbean Community (CARICOM):** Facilitates academic mobility and collaboration

### Pacific Physics Networks

- **University of the South Pacific (USP):** Regional university owned by 12 Pacific island countries; main physics hub for the Pacific
- **Pacific Islands Universities Research Network (PIURN):** Facilitates research collaboration
- **South Pacific Regional Environment Programme (SPREP):** Collaborates on climate and environmental physics research

### Atlantic/Nordic Island Networks

- **University of the Arctic (UArctic):** Network of 200+ institutions in the Arctic region including Iceland, Greenland, Faroe Islands
  - **north2north mobility program:** Student exchange program
- **Nordic Volcanological Centre:** Based at University of Iceland
- **West Nordic University Collaboration:** Joint programs between Faroe Islands, Greenland, and Iceland

### Mediterranean Networks
