## 3. ENERGY PHYSICS

### ITER Organization (France)
**URL:** https://www.iter.org

**Physics Focus:** Fusion energy, plasma physics, tokamak technology

**Description:** ITER ("The Way" in Latin) is one of the most ambitious energy projects in the world. In southern France, 35 nations are collaborating to build the world's largest tokamak, a magnetic fusion device designed to prove the feasibility of fusion as a large-scale, carbon-free source of energy.

**Key Physics Areas:**
- Magnetic confinement fusion
- Plasma physics
- Superconducting magnets
- Tritium breeding
- Nuclear fusion reactor physics

**Participants:** China, European Union, India, Japan, Korea, Russia, United States

---

### Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS) (USA)
**URL:** https://cfs.energy

**Physics Focus:** Commercial fusion energy, high-temperature superconducting magnets

**Description:** CFS is the world's largest and leading commercial fusion energy company. The company is using revolutionary high-temperature superconducting (HTS) magnets developed in collaboration with MIT to build smaller and lower-cost tokamak fusion systems.

**Key Physics Areas:**
- High-temperature superconducting magnets
- Tokamak fusion systems
- Plasma confinement
- Fusion power plant design

**Projects:** SPARC (demonstration machine), ARC (commercial power plant)

### TAE Technologies (USA)

**Key Physics Areas:**
- Field-reversed configuration (FRC) fusion
