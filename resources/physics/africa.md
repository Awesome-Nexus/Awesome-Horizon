## 1. Pan-African Initiatives

### 1.1 African School of Fundamental Physics and Applications (ASP)
- **URL:** https://africanschoolofphysics.org/
- **Founded:** 2010
- **Description:** A biennial school that brings together African and international physicists to teach advanced physics topics to African graduate students. Founded by Kétévi A. Assamagan (Brookhaven National Laboratory) and an international organizing committee.
- **Editions:**
  | Year | Location | Host Country |
  |------|----------|--------------|
  | 2010 | Stellenbosch | South Africa |
  | 2012 | Accra | Ghana |
  | 2014 | Dakar | Senegal |
  | 2016 | Kigali | Rwanda |
  | 2018 | Windhoek | Namibia |
  | 2022 | Gqeberha (Port Elizabeth) | South Africa |
  | 2024 | Marrakesh | Morocco |
  | 2026 | Nairobi | Kenya (planned) |

- **Programs:**
  - **Students Program:** 3-week intensive program for 60-80 African graduate students (MSc/PhD level) covering particle physics, nuclear physics, astrophysics, cosmology, accelerator physics, medical physics, computing
  - **Teachers Program:** Training for 70-80 high school physics teachers
  - **High School Learners Program:** Outreach to 1000-2000 students
  - **ASP Forum:** Engagement with policymakers
  - **Mentorship Program:** Continuous coaching for alumni
  - **Online Lecture Series:** Weekly seminars

- **Funding Partners:** ICTP (Italy), Brookhaven National Laboratory (USA), CERN, Fermilab, INFN (Italy), South African National Research Foundation, U.S. Department of Energy, and multiple international partners

### 1.4 CERN-Africa Partnerships
- **South Africa - CERN Collaboration:**
  - Formal partnership since 1992 (ICA with Foundation for Research Development)
  - SA-CERN Programme celebrating 15+ years
  - Participation in ALICE and ATLAS experiments at LHC
  - Membership in ISOLDE (radioactive ion beam facility)
  - About 29 senior researchers, 13 postdocs, 50+ MSc/PhD students participating
  - Hosts summer students and high-school teachers
  - Hosted first African School of Physics (2010)
  - Partnership with iThemba LABS on accelerator technologies

- **Other African Countries at CERN:**
  - Egypt: Associate member of CERN (since 2022)
  - Algeria: Scientific cooperation agreements
  - Morocco: Active participation in experiments, hosted ASP 2024
  - Tunisia: Research collaborations
  - Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, Rwanda: Research partnerships and ASP participation

### 1.5 IAEA Collaborations in Africa
- **African Regional Cooperative Agreement (AFRA):**
  - 49 African countries receive technical cooperation
  - Focus areas: nuclear medicine, agriculture, water management, energy, radiation safety
  
- **Research Reactors:**
  - Algeria: NUR reactor, Es-Salem reactor
  - Egypt: ETRR-2
  - Ghana: Ghana Research Reactor-1 (GHARR-1)
  - Morocco: MA-R1 reactor (CNESTEN)
  - Nigeria: NIRR-1
  - South Africa: SAFARI-1 (NECSA)

- **Capacity Building:**
  - Research Reactor School in Africa Region
  - International Centre based on Research Reactor (ICERR) initiative
  - Training in reactor physics and safe operation

### 1.6 Square Kilometre Array (SKA) Project
- **URL:** https://www.sarao.ac.za/
- **SKAO Headquarters:** https://www.skao.int/
- **Description:** World's largest radio telescope project, co-hosted in South Africa and Australia

- **African Partner Countries:**
  - South Africa (host country)
  - Botswana
  - Ghana
  - Kenya
  - Madagascar
  - Mauritius
  - Mozambique
  - Namibia
  - Zambia

- **SKA Precursor in South Africa:**
  - **MeerKAT:** 64-dish radio telescope array in Northern Cape
  - **KAT-7:** 7-dish engineering testbed (completed 2012)
  - **HERA:** Hydrogen Epoch of Reionization Array

- **African SKA Human Capital Development Programme:**
  - Nearly 1000 grants awarded (2005-2017) for studies in astronomy and engineering
  - Training programs for technicians
  - Astronomy courses in Kenya, Mozambique, Madagascar, Mauritius

- **Legacy:** Mosharafa Prize for distinguished scientists

- **Field:** High energy physics
- **Contributions:** Research at CERN, former Chair of Physics Department at Cairo University

#### National Research Facilities

**iThemba Laboratory for Accelerator Based Sciences (iThemba LABS)**
- **URL:** https://tlabs.ac.za/
- **Parent Organization:** National Research Foundation (NRF)
- **Locations:** Faure (Cape Town) and Johannesburg
- **Description:** Africa's premier facility for accelerator-based science, only facility of its kind on the African continent
- **Accelerators:**
  - 200-MeV Separated Sector Cyclotron (SSC)
  - 70-MeV cyclotron (SAIF - South African Isotope Facility, inaugurated 2023)
  - 6-MV Tandem accelerator (Johannesburg) with AMS
  - 3-MV Tandetron
- **Research Areas:**
  - Nuclear physics (fundamental and applied)
  - Medical radioisotope production
  - Cancer treatment (proton and neutron therapy)
  - Materials science
  - Radiation biophysics
  - Accelerator mass spectrometry (archaeology, climate research)
- **Training:** Southern African Institute for Nuclear Technology and Sciences (SAINTS)

**South African Radio Astronomy Observatory (SARAO)**
- **URL:** https://www.sarao.ac.za/
- **Description:** National facility responsible for SKA and MeerKAT
- **Telescopes:**
  - MeerKAT (64-dish array in Northern Cape)
  - Hartebeesthoek Radio Astronomy Observatory (HartRAO)
- **Research:** Radio astronomy, space geodesy

**South African Astronomical Observatory (SAAO)**
- **URL:** https://www.saao.ac.za/
- **Location:** Cape Town, Sutherland
- **Telescopes:** Southern African Large Telescope (SALT) - largest single optical telescope in Southern Hemisphere

**National Institute for Theoretical and Computational Sciences (NITheCS)**
- **URL:** https://www.nithecs.ac.za/
- **Description:** Multidisciplinary institute for theoretical and computational sciences
- **Physics Fields:**
  - Astronomy & Astrophysics
  - Theoretical Physics
  - Mathematics
  - Data Science
- **Nodes:** Stellenbosch University, University of the Witwatersrand, North-West University

**Centre for High Performance Computing (CHPC)**
- **Location:** Cape Town
- **Description:** Fastest supercomputer in Africa
- **Activities:** Processes 2,000+ jobs daily for CERN ALICE project

**NECSA (South African Nuclear Energy Corporation)**
- **URL:** https://www.necsa.co.za/
- **Research Reactor:** SAFARI-1 (20 MW, Pelindaba)
- **Activities:** Nuclear research, isotope production, waste management

**Koeberg Nuclear Power Station**
- **Operator:** Eskom
- **Description:** Only nuclear power plant in Africa
- **Units:** 2 pressurized water reactors (900 MWe each)
- **Status:** Unit 1 life extension approved (2024) for additional 20 years

## 7. Notable African Physicists

### 7.1 Nobel Laureates

**Ahmed Zewail (Egypt, 1946-2016)**
- **Prize:** Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1999)
- **Achievement:** Femtochemistry - study of chemical reactions on ultrafast timescales
- **Legacy:** First Egyptian and Arab Nobel laureate in science

**Allan MacLeod Cormack (South Africa, 1924-1998)**
- **Prize:** Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine (1979)
- **Achievement:** Development of computed tomography (CT scanning)
- **Education:** University of Cape Town

**Sir Aaron Klug (South Africa/UK, 1926-2018)**
- **Prize:** Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1982)
- **Achievement:** Development of crystallographic electron microscopy
- **Education:** University of Cape Town, University of the Witwatersrand

### 7.2 Pioneers and Founders

**Ali Moustafa Mosharafa (Egypt, 1898-1950)**
- **Achievement:** Leading Arab theoretical physicist of the early 20th century
- **Contributions:** 25 research papers in quantum mechanics and relativity
- **Legacy:** Mosharafa Prize for distinguished scientists

**Francis Kofi Ampenyin Allotey (Ghana, 1932-2017)**
- **Achievement:** Mathematical physicist, founder of AIMS Ghana
- **Contributions:** Allotey Formalism in soft X-ray spectroscopy
- **Legacy:** National Centre for Mathematical Sciences (Ghana)

### 7.3 Contemporary Leaders

- **Origin:** Born in Guinea, grew up in Togo
- **Position:** Physicist at Brookhaven National Laboratory (USA)

**Neil Turok**
